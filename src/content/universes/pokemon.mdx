## Setting

The Pokémon world (1996-present) is a setting where magical creatures are the norm rather than the exception. Pokémon possess innate elemental abilities organized into 18 types (Normal, Fire, Water, Electric, Grass, Ice, Fighting, Poison, Ground, Flying, Psychic, Bug, Rock, Ghost, Dragon, Dark, Steel, Fairy), each with defined offensive and defensive relationships. Human Trainers capture, raise, and battle Pokémon, directing their abilities in combat through a system of four-move slots, type matchups, stats, natures, and abilities. While the setting presents this as natural ecology rather than magic, the creatures' abilities — teleportation, elemental manipulation, psychic powers, ghostly possession — function as a comprehensive magic system channeled through bonded creatures rather than cast directly.
