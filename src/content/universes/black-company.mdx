---
name: The World of the Black Company
creator: Glen Cook
description: A dark fantasy world seen through the eyes of a mercenary company's annalist, where powerful sorcerers wage wars of domination and the line between employer and enemy shifts constantly — the setting emphasizing the grunt-level experience of soldiers caught between forces beyond their comprehension.
---

The world of the **Black Company** is deliberately unnamed and undermapped — readers experience it through the limited, ground-level perspective of **Croaker**, the company's physician and annalist. The setting spans multiple continents and centuries, from the northern wars against the Lady and the Dominator to the southern campaigns around Khatovar and the Shadowlands.

The world's defining feature is its power hierarchy: the **Lady** and the **Dominator** are near-godlike sorcerers who ruled empires through magical dominance. The **Ten Who Were Taken** — powerful sorcerers magically bound to serve the Lady — are the ruling class. Below them, ordinary people and soldiers navigate a world shaped by forces they cannot control. Cook uses this structure to examine how power operates from the bottom up: the Black Company doesn't fight for justice or destiny, but for pay, survival, and loyalty to their brothers.
