## Setting

The *Mercy Thompson* novels (Patricia Briggs, 2006– ) are set in the modern-day Tri-Cities area of Washington State and a wider America undergoing a slow, managed supernatural revelation. The **Fae** were the first to go public, compelled by government pressure; they now live on reservations and maintain a tense relationship with human authorities. The **werewolves** revealed themselves voluntarily under the strategic guidance of the Marrok, Bran Cornick, who serves as the alpha of all North American packs and carefully controls their public image. **Vampires** remain hidden from the general public, organized into regional seethes under master vampires.

Mercedes "Mercy" Thompson is a **walker** — a coyote-shifter whose abilities derive from Native American spiritual traditions rather than the European-origin lycanthropy of the werewolves. Her unique nature places her outside the standard supernatural hierarchies, giving her partial immunity to vampire and fae magic and the ability to perceive ghosts. The series explores the political and social consequences of supernatural integration, with pack bonds, fae obligations, and vampire politics forming overlapping systems of power and obligation.
