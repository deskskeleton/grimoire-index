## Setting

The Hyborian Age occupies a mythic prehistory roughly twelve thousand years before the present day, after the cataclysm that destroyed Atlantis and before the dawn of any civilization known to modern archaeology. Its geography is a distorted mirror of the real world: Aquilonia and Nemedia correspond loosely to Western Europe, Stygia to Egypt, Turan to the Ottoman lands, Cimmeria to the Celtic north. The dominant cultures range from the feudal Hyborian kingdoms to the ancient sorcerer-states of Stygia and Acheron. Civilization is a thin veneer over barbarism, and Howard's stories consistently argue that the vitality of the barbarian outlasts the decadence of the civilized. Sorcery is real but almost always associated with corruption, cruelty, and inhumanity.
