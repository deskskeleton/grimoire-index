## Setting

The *Dresden Files* novels (Jim Butcher, 2000– ) are set in a contemporary Chicago whose visible cityscape overlies a structured supernatural ecology. The principal political bodies are the **White Council** (the international wizards' government), the **Sidhe Courts** of Summer and Winter, the three vampire **Courts** (Red, White, Black), various demonic factions and the **Order of the Blackened Denarius**, and the angelic powers represented by the Knights of the Cross. Their relations are governed by the **Unseelie Accords**, a treaty framework that enumerates rights of safe passage, neutral grounds, formal duels, and rules of war.

The mundane world is largely unaware of all of this. The wizardly **technology-disruption effect** — magic interferes with modern electronics — gives the masquerade a structural reason to hold: practising wizards cannot easily integrate with surveillance-era infrastructure even if they want to. Adjacent to physical Chicago is the **Nevernever**, the spirit world, which can be entered and traversed at specific thinned locations and which connects to faerie demesnes, hellish reaches, and other planes. This catalog currently covers Dresden Files magic — the wizards' Art — as one entry; vampire courts, faerie magic, and the Knights' divine grace are candidate future entries.
