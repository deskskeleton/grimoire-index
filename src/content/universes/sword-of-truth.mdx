name: The World of the Sword of Truth
creator: Terry Goodkind
description: A world divided between the New World and the Old World by a magical barrier, where Additive Magic (creation and enhancement) and Subtractive Magic (destruction and void) represent the fundamental duality of magical power, and the Confessors — women who can enslave with a touch — serve as the judicial branch of the Midlands.
---

The world of the **Sword of Truth** series is divided by boundaries both magical and political. The **New World** consists of the Midlands (governed by the Confessors and the Central Council), Westland (a magic-free zone), and D'Hara (a militaristic empire). The **Old World** lies beyond a barrier erected to contain the Imperial Order and its anti-magic ideology.

Magic in this world operates through the duality of Additive and Subtractive power, with the rare war wizard (like Richard Rahl) possessing both. Goodkind uses the setting to explore themes of individual liberty, the relationship between reason and power, and the dangers of collectivist ideology — themes that become increasingly explicit as the series progresses.
