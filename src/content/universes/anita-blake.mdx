## Setting

The *Anita Blake: Vampire Hunter* novels (Laurell K. Hamilton, 1993– ) take place in a version of contemporary America — primarily St. Louis, Missouri — where the existence of vampires, lycanthropes, and other preternatural creatures is publicly acknowledged and regulated by law. Vampires gained legal personhood through a Supreme Court ruling (Addison v. Clark), and vampire-run businesses operate openly. The federal government licenses both **animators** (practitioners who raise the dead as zombies, typically for legal testimony or family closure) and **vampire executioners** who hold warrants of execution for rogue vampires.

Supernatural politics are organized around power structures: vampire society is hierarchical, with **Master Vampires** controlling cities and owing fealty to a council of elder vampires. Lycanthrope communities are organized into packs, prides, and other animal-specific groups governed by dominance and challenge. The intersection of these communities — and the human legal system's imperfect attempts to regulate them — generates most of the series' conflicts.
