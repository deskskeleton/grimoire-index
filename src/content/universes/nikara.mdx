## Setting

R. F. Kuang's *Poppy War* trilogy (*The Poppy War*, 2018; *The Dragon Republic*, 2019; *The Burning God*, 2020) is set in the Nikara Empire, a civilisation closely modelled on modern Chinese history — the Nikara's wars with the Federation of Mugen parallel the Second Sino-Japanese War, and the trilogy's political arc tracks through revolution, civil war, and the struggle against imperial interference. The world's geography, ethnic divisions, and political structures are drawn from Chinese history with deliberate, acknowledged parallels.

Magic in Nikara takes the form of **shamanism**: practitioners who can enter trance states (originally through psychoactive substances, particularly opium derivatives) and connect to the Pantheon — a realm of gods drawn from Chinese mythology, including the Phoenix, the Dragon, the Tortoise, and others. These gods are real, conscious entities whose power shamans can channel, but the connection is not benign. The gods are alien intelligences that can overwhelm and consume the shaman's mind, and sustained channeling erodes the boundary between self and deity. The Nikara military academy at Sinegard teaches that shamanism was eradicated generations ago; the trilogy reveals that this is a deliberate lie, and the rediscovery of shamanic power drives the protagonist Rin's arc from war orphan to weapon of mass destruction.
