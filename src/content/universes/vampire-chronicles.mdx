## Setting

Anne Rice's interconnected novels span from ancient Egypt to the modern era, following vampires who move through real historical settings — eighteenth-century Paris, nineteenth-century New Orleans, contemporary America. The supernatural is hidden in plain sight; vampires live among humans, adopting new identities as centuries pass. The world expands over the course of the series to include the Mayfair Witches (a dynastic family of hereditary witches in New Orleans bound to a powerful spirit called Lasher) and the Talamasca, a secretive scholarly order that studies the paranormal.

## Tone

The series treats immortality as a psychological and existential condition rather than a simple power fantasy. Rice's vampires are defined as much by loneliness, aesthetic obsession, and moral ambiguity as by their supernatural abilities, and the mythology deepens with each book — ultimately tracing all vampirism to a single supernatural origin event in pre-dynastic Egypt.
