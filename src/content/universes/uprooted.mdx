## Setting

Naomi Novik's *Uprooted* (2015) is set in a valley on the border of Polnya, a kingdom loosely modelled on historical Poland. The valley's defining feature is **the Wood** — an ancient, malevolent forest that corrupts everything it touches, transforming people into puppets, tainting water and soil, and slowly expanding its borders. Villages survive under the protection of **the Dragon**, a centuries-old wizard who lives in a tower and periodically takes a young woman from the valley for ten years of service — a tithe the villagers accept as the price of his protection.

The novel's magical landscape is defined by the tension between two approaches to spell-craft. The Dragon practises precise, formulaic magic drawn from written cantrips and exact pronunciation — a learned, intellectual tradition. The protagonist Agnieszka discovers a radically different mode: intuitive, improvisational magic woven through folk songs, emotional resonance, and deep connection to the valley's living landscape. The plot turns on whether these two incompatible approaches can be reconciled to face the Wood's deepest corruption. The Wood itself is revealed to be not merely evil but grieving — the magical residue of an ancient tragedy between humans and the wood-people, a race destroyed by human expansion.
