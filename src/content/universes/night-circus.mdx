## Overview

Le Cirque des Rêves — the Circus of Dreams — arrives without announcement and opens only at nightfall. Its tents contain wonders that blur the line between stage illusion and genuine magic: a garden made entirely of ice, a cloud maze suspended in midair, a bonfire that burns in impossible colors. To its visitors, the circus is an experience of pure wonder. Behind the scenes, it is a battlefield.

The circus exists as the venue for a long-running competition between two ancient magicians, Prospero the Enchanter and Mr. A.H., who each train a protégé and set them against one another using the circus as their canvas. The rules of the contest are never fully explained to the competitors themselves, and the stakes — which prove to be lethal — only become clear as the story unfolds. Magic in this world is deeply tied to artistry, emotion, and the physical senses rather than to formal systems or codified rules.
