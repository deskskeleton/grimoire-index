## Structure

The Chronicles of Amber posit a single real place — the city of Amber, seat of Order — from which all other realities radiate outward as progressively less real "Shadows." At the far end of the spectrum lie the Courts of Chaos, the equally primal pole of entropy and flux. Everything between — including our Earth — is a Shadow, a partial reflection of Amber that can be walked to and subtly reshaped by those with the blood of Amber's royal family. The metaphysics are deliberately loose: Zelazny treats the multiverse as a narrative playground rather than a rigorous system, and much of the series' tension arises from characters discovering that the rules they assumed were incomplete or wrong.

## Related works

Zelazny's broader bibliography shares thematic DNA with Amber — particularly *Lord of Light* (1967), in which technologically advanced humans assume the roles of Hindu gods on a colonized world. Though set in a separate universe, *Lord of Light* explores many of the same questions about the relationship between power, identity, and the stories people tell about divinity.
