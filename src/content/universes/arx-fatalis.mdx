## Setting

Arx is a world in crisis. The sun has died or been extinguished, forcing every surface civilization — humans, goblins, trolls, ratmen, and others — underground into a shared subterranean realm of interconnected cavern levels. The former surface kingdoms now occupy different strata of this underground world, maintaining uneasy coexistence punctuated by territorial conflicts and resource competition. Magic in Arx is practiced through rune-based spellcasting that requires the caster to physically draw runic symbols in the air, combining multiple runes in sequence to produce different effects. The city of Arx serves as the human settlement, with a castle, temple district, and market carved from living stone. Below lie increasingly dangerous levels populated by hostile creatures, culminating in the domain of Akbaa, a demon god whose cult seeks to summon him into the material world. The setting's claustrophobic underground atmosphere and the desperation of civilizations sharing limited space give magic a survival-tool quality absent from more expansive fantasy worlds.
