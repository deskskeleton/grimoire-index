## Setting

Tim Powers' *Last Call* (1992) is set in and around Las Vegas, where the neon-lit casino culture sits atop a deeper mythic landscape. The novel's central conceit is that the Fisher King of Arthurian legend is a real metaphysical office — a throne of symbolic sovereignty over the American West — and that this throne can be won or lost through games of Tarot-derived poker. The wasteland imagery of the Grail myth maps onto the Nevada desert, and the novel's antagonist, Georges Leon, has maintained his hold on the throne through decades of rigged games that allow him to consume and replace other people's identities. The magic operates through coincidence, symbol, and archetype rather than through overt supernatural spectacle.
