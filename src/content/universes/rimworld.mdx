## Setting

Rimworld's universe spans a galaxy of settled worlds at varying technological levels, from neolithic tribes to transcendent archotech civilizations whose capabilities are indistinguishable from magic. The player's colony occupies a single tile on a procedurally generated world, building from nothing toward eventual escape or permanence. The Royalty expansion introduced the Empire, a feudal interstellar polity whose nobles wield psycasts — genuine psychic abilities channeled through neural amplification — governed by a rigid hierarchy of psychic sensitivity tiers. The Anomaly expansion added a horror dimension: eldritch entities, flesh constructs, and reality-warping anomalies that breach into the world from somewhere outside conventional space. The setting's genius lies in its layered technology spectrum, where a single colony might contain tribal warriors, industrial engineers, and psychically gifted nobles coexisting in a frontier settlement threatened by both mundane raiders and cosmic horrors.
