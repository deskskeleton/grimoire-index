## Setting

The Stellaris galaxy begins in an age of early interstellar expansion, with dozens of procedurally generated civilizations discovering faster-than-light travel and encountering each other for the first time. The galaxy's history unfolds through diplomatic, military, and economic competition, but its deepest mysteries lie in the remnants of Precursor civilizations and the existence of the Shroud — an extradimensional plane of psychic energy accessible only to species that have developed psionic potential. The Shroud contains powerful entities that offer pacts and bargains, including the Eater of Worlds, the Whisperers in the Void, and the Instrument of Desire. These entities grant extraordinary powers but at costs that can destabilize or destroy entire civilizations. The game treats psionics as one of three possible evolutionary endpoints for intelligent life, positioning it as functionally magical within a science-fiction framework — telepathy, telekinesis, precognition, and psychic shields all operate through mechanisms that the game's materialist setting cannot fully explain.
