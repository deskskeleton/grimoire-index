## Setting

The *Hollows* novels (Kim Harrison, 2004– ) are set in an alternate-history Cincinnati where the **Turn** — a catastrophic pandemic caused by a bioengineered virus hidden in genetically modified tomatoes — killed roughly a quarter of the human population in the 1960s. In the chaos that followed, the **Inderlanders** (the collective term for supernatural beings including witches, vampires, living and undead, werewolves, pixies, and elves) revealed their existence and integrated into society. Humans and Inderlanders now coexist openly, with the **Inderlander Security service (IS)** policing supernatural crimes and the human-run **Federal Inderland Bureau (FIB)** handling human-on-human cases.

The series is centered on the Hollows, a neighborhood in Cincinnati across the river from the human-dominated city proper, where Inderlanders predominantly live. Rachel Morgan operates as an independent runner — a freelance bounty hunter and security specialist — alongside a living vampire and a pixie. Beneath the procedural surface, a deeper political layer involves **demons** imprisoned in the ever-after (a decaying parallel dimension), ley lines that connect reality to the ever-after, and an ancient history tying witches and demons together far more closely than modern witch society wants to admit.
