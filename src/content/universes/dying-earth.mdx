## Setting

The Dying Earth is set in the final age of our own planet, so far in the future that the sun has become a dim red ember threatening to go out at any moment. Civilizations beyond counting have risen and fallen, leaving behind ruins, monsters, half-forgotten spells, and artifacts whose original purposes are lost. The landscape is a patchwork of petty kingdoms, decadent cities, and wilderness haunted by creatures both natural and manufactured. Technology and magic are indistinguishable; what remains of either is hoarded jealously by those who possess it. The tone is ironic, mannered, and darkly comic — Vance's prose style treats catastrophic events with wry detachment, and his characters are predominantly self-interested rogues navigating a world that has run out of time.
