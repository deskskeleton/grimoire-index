## Setting

Robert Jackson Bennett's *The Tainted Cup* (2024) is set in the Empire of Khanum, a sprawling civilisation whose technology, medicine, and military power all derive from the biological harvesting of massive sea-leviathans — titanic creatures that periodically make landfall, destroying everything in their path. The empire has developed an elaborate system of biological engineering called **grafting**, in which substances derived from leviathan tissue are used to augment human bodies, granting enhanced senses, cognition, strength, or other capabilities. The cost is physical transformation: the grafted develop bark-like skin, compound eyes, altered limbs, and other inhuman features.

The story follows Dinios Kol, an investigator grafted with perfect memory, and his brilliant but reclusive superior Ana Dolabra, whose cognitive grafts have given her extraordinary analytical abilities at significant personal cost. Their murder investigation unfolds against the backdrop of leviathan season — the annual threat of catastrophic landfall — revealing how deeply the empire's power structures depend on the exploitation of these creatures and the people who are altered to fight them.
