## Setting

Moana's world is rooted in Polynesian mythology reimagined through Disney's narrative framework. The ocean is alive and possesses its own will, choosing Moana as the one who will restore Te Fiti's heart — a greenstone that represents the goddess's creative power. When Maui stole the heart to gift humanity with the power of creation, Te Fiti's corruption transformed her into Te Kā, a volcanic demon of destruction, and unleashed a spreading blight that threatens all island life. Maui himself is a demigod whose powers of shapeshifting are channeled through his magical fishhook, without which he is reduced to mortal capability. The Realm of Monsters exists beneath the ocean as a bioluminescent underworld ruled by Tamatoa, a giant crab, and populated by creatures from Polynesian legend. Magic in this world is divine and elemental — it flows through gods, demigods, and the ocean itself rather than through human practitioners, making Moana's role as the ocean's chosen one a matter of destiny and character rather than learned skill.
