## Overview

Tevanne is a merchant city dominated by four great houses — Dandolo, Michiel, Morsini, and Candiano — whose power derives from their control of scriving, a technology-like magic that allows practitioners to alter the physical properties of objects by inscribing them with logical arguments in an ancient hieroglyphic language. A scrived carriage might be convinced it is always rolling downhill; a scrived bolt might believe it has been fired from a cannon. The result is a society that resembles an industrial revolution powered by metaphysical engineering rather than steam.

As the trilogy progresses, the scope expands far beyond Tevanne to encompass the nature of the hierophantic language itself and the divine beings — the Makers — who originally created it. The fundamental question of the series shifts from who controls scriving to whether reality itself can be rewritten at the deepest level, and what the moral consequences of such rewriting might be.
