## Setting

The world of Cult of the Lamb is divided between the Lands of the Old Faith, a series of increasingly hostile biomes ruled by four Bishops who serve a dying cosmic order, and the Lamb's cult commune, a clearing in the woods that grows from a modest campfire into a thriving settlement of devoted followers. The cosmology centers on The One Who Waits, a chained deity imprisoned by the Bishops in a dimension called The Gateway, who resurrects the Lamb and grants it a Red Crown — the source of all its supernatural power. The Old Faith's Bishops each embody a domain (plague, famine, war, death) and command legions of heretical creatures. The world's religious economy is literal: followers generate devotion through worship, rituals, and sacrifice, which the Lamb channels into combat abilities and divine miracles. The tension between nurturing a flock and exploiting it for power runs through every system, creating a world where pastoral care and dark ambition are mechanically inseparable.
