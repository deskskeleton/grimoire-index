---
name: The Sandman
creator: Neil Gaiman
description: A cosmology where seven anthropomorphic Endless embody universal concepts, presiding over realms like the Dreaming alongside gods, demons, and mortals whose fates intertwine across millennia.
---

The Sandman universe spans the full breadth of mythology, theology, and human imagination. At its apex are the Endless — seven siblings who are not gods but fundamental aspects of existence itself. Each maintains a personal realm that serves as the functional engine of their concept: Dream rules the Dreaming, where all dreams and stories reside; Death guides every soul at the moment of its passing; Destiny reads the book that contains all events. Below and beside the Endless, the universe teems with gods from every human pantheon, demons who rule a politically fractious Hell, faeries, and mortal sorcerers whose power is borrowed or bargained for.

The cosmology is layered and literary. Hell is a real place with a lock and key; Lucifer can resign his post and hand the key to Dream. The Dreaming contains libraries of books never written, nightmares given autonomous form, and gates through which mortals pass every night without knowing it. History, myth, and fiction blur — Shakespeare writes plays commissioned by Dream, and Orpheus's severed head persists through centuries. The rules are consistent but poetic: the Endless are bound by responsibilities they cannot abandon without catastrophic consequence, and the stories mortals tell reshape the metaphysical landscape as surely as any spell.
