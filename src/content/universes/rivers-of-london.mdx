## Setting

The *Rivers of London* novels (Ben Aaronovitch, 2011– ) are set in a contemporary London where magic is an acknowledged but classified reality within the Metropolitan Police Service. The **Folly**, a Palladian townhouse near Russell Square, serves as both headquarters and residence for the last officially sanctioned English wizard, Detective Chief Inspector Thomas Nightingale, and his apprentice PC Peter Grant. The Folly was once the base of a large corps of wizard-practitioners trained at an academy that was effectively destroyed during the Second World War; Nightingale is the sole surviving graduate.

Alongside the wizardly establishment, London's rivers are embodied as **river gods and goddesses** — Mama Thames controls the tidal Thames south of Teddington Lock, Father Thames claims the upper river, and their respective tributaries (the Fleet, the Tyburn, the Lea, and others) manifest as lesser spirits with their own personalities and political agendas. These supernatural entities negotiate territory with each other and with the Folly. The series blends police-procedural structure with a slowly expanding map of Britain's magical ecology, including fae, revenants, and genius loci tied to specific London locations.
