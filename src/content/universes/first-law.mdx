The **Circle of the World** is Joe Abercrombie's grimdark fantasy setting, spanning two trilogies and several standalone novels. It is a world shaped by centuries of manipulation by the Magi — immortal practitioners of the Art who were students of Juvens, the first Magus. The setting's defining feature is its cynical realism: heroic narratives are systematically deconstructed, political power is shown as fundamentally corrupt, and the "wise wizard" archetype is revealed as a ruthless manipulator.

The world's magical history centers on the conflict between **Bayaz** (First of the Magi, who controls the Union — the setting's dominant civilization) and **Khalul** (his rival, who controls the Gurkish Empire through a network of Eaters). Both have violated Juvens' First Law — the prohibition against touching the Other Side directly — and both use their magical knowledge primarily for political control rather than heroic purposes.

By the Age of Madness trilogy, industrialization is transforming the Union, and magical influence has become even more hidden behind institutional power. The series traces how power perpetuates itself regardless of revolution, reform, or individual heroism.
