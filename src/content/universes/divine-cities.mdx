## Setting

Robert Jackson Bennett's Divine Cities trilogy (*City of Stairs*, 2014; *City of Blades*, 2016; *City of Miracles*, 2017) is set on **the Continent**, a landmass that was once the seat of a theocratic empire ruled by six **Divinities** — literal gods whose miracles reshaped the physical world. Under divine rule, the Continent's cities defied natural law: staircases led to rooms that could not exist, weapons struck with impossible accuracy, and entire regions operated under altered physics dictated by whichever Divinity held sway. The Continent used its divine power to enslave and colonize the nation of **Saypur** for centuries. The balance shattered when the Saypuri revolutionary **the Kaj** killed the Divinities using a mysterious weapon, causing most miracles to collapse — buildings folded in on themselves, impossible geometries failed, and the Continent was left a diminished, grieving ruin. The trilogy takes place decades after the Divine War, as Saypuri agents navigate the political and metaphysical aftermath, discovering that some miracles persist as **residuals** and that the divine is not as dead as the world assumed.
