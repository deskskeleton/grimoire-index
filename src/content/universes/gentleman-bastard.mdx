name: The Therin Tableau
creator: Scott Lynch
description: A Renaissance-Italian-inspired fantasy world where a small, feared caste of Bondsmagi hold a monopoly on sorcery — enforced by political treaties and the threat of annihilation — while the rest of civilization operates through mundane commerce, crime, and statecraft.
---

The **Therin Tableau** is the setting of Scott Lynch's Gentleman Bastard sequence. It is a world of city-states, merchant princes, and elaborate criminal enterprises, modeled broadly on Renaissance Venice and the Mediterranean world. Magic exists but is deliberately marginalized — the **Bondsmagi of Karthain** hold an effective monopoly on sorcery, and their power is so overwhelming that the rest of civilization has negotiated treaties to contain them rather than compete with them.

The setting's defining tension is between mundane cleverness (embodied by Locke Lamora and his crew of confidence artists) and magical power (embodied by the Bondsmagi). The Bondsmagi could theoretically dominate the world, but internal politics, treaty obligations, and their own factional conflicts prevent unified action. This creates a world where magic is present but socially quarantined — a feared, resented force that most people encounter only through its political consequences.
