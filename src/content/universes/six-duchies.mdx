## Setting

Robin Hobb's Realm of the Elderlings spans sixteen novels across four trilogies and one tetralogy. The primary setting is the Six Duchies — a feudal kingdom on the eastern coast — with later series expanding to the Rain Wild River, Bingtown, the Pirate Isles, and Kelsingra. Magic in this world is deeply personal and politically charged: the Skill (telepathy) is the royal family's prerogative, the Wit (animal bonding) is a persecuted ability likened to bestiality by the ignorant, and the White Prophecy shapes events through individuals who can perceive and manipulate the future. The Elderlings themselves — the ancient civilization whose ruins dot the landscape — used all three forms of magic at levels that modern practitioners can barely comprehend.
