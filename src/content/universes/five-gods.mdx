name: The World of the Five Gods
creator: Lois McMaster Bujold
description: A theologically rich world where five gods (Father, Mother, Son, Daughter, and Bastard) actively intervene through human agents — saints who channel divine will and sorcerers who carry demons bound by the Bastard's order — with the divine operating through consent and alignment rather than compulsion.
---

The **World of the Five Gods** is the setting of Lois McMaster Bujold's *Chalion* novels (*The Curse of Chalion*, *Paladin of Souls*) and the *Penric and Desdemona* novellas. The world's theology is literal: five gods exist, maintain distinct spheres of influence, and intervene in mortal affairs through willing human agents.

The setting's distinctive theological mechanic is **consent**. The gods cannot compel human action — they can only work through people who open themselves to divine will. This creates a theology of radical free will: miracles happen when a person's desires align perfectly with a god's intent, and the alignment must be genuine, not performative. Bujold uses this framework to explore questions of faith, duty, and the intersection of personal choice with cosmic purpose.
