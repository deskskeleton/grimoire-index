## Setting

The BioShock series (2007-2013) presents two parallel settings unified by thematic concerns about ideology and power. Rapture, the setting of BioShock and BioShock 2, is an underwater city where the discovery of ADAM — a mutagenic substance produced by sea slugs — enabled the creation of Plasmids, genetic modifications granting abilities like telekinesis, pyrokinesis, and electrical discharge. Columbia, the setting of BioShock Infinite, features Vigors — functionally identical to Plasmids but produced through different pseudo-scientific means — and the Tears, rifts between parallel realities that Elizabeth can manipulate. Both settings use their power systems as metaphors for the destructive consequences of ideological extremism.
