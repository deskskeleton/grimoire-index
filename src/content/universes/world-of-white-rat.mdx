name: World of the White Rat
creator: T. Kingfisher
description: A fantasy world populated by multiple active gods who grant their followers distinct supernatural gifts, where paladins are divine berserkers, the god of bureaucracy runs the most competent temple, and a dead god has left his warriors broken and dangerous.
---

T. Kingfisher's interconnected novels are set in a world where gods are real and actively involved in mortal affairs through their temples and chosen agents. The Temple of the White Rat — god of small comforts, fair dealing, and bureaucracy — is staffed by healers, lawyers, and administrators who wield divine authority with pragmatic competence. Other gods include the Dreaming God (prophecy and madness), the Forge God (craft and artifice), and the Saint of Steel, a minor war god whose sudden death left seven paladins stranded with divine battle-fury they can no longer fully control.

The setting blends high-stakes theological drama with grounded, often darkly humorous character work. Paladins are not shining paragons but deeply traumatized veterans managing supernatural rage. Gnoles — badger-like creatures with their own earthy magic — inhabit the margins. Demon-possessed clockwork constructs threaten civilization. The world operates on the principle that divine power is real but comes with institutional baggage, personal cost, and the perpetual question of what happens when the god who made you is no longer there to explain what you are.
