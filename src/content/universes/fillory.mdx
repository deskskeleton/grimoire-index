## Overview

The world of *The Magicians* operates on two levels. On Earth, magic is real but hidden from public view and practiced through grueling academic study at institutions like Brakebills University in upstate New York. Magic here is technical, demanding, and decidedly unglamorous — less wish fulfillment, more graduate school. The curriculum involves years of memorizing hand positions, studying environmental variables, and learning dead languages, all to produce effects that a casual observer might mistake for parlor tricks.

Fillory, the secondary world, is a place drawn from a beloved series of children's novels that the characters grew up reading — and which turns out to be an actual, reachable dimension. It operates on different, more generous magical rules than Earth, with talking animals, questing gods, and ambient enchantment. The tension between Fillory's storybook promise and its messier reality is central to the series' project of interrogating what fantasy fiction actually offers its readers.
