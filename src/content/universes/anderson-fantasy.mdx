## Setting

Poul Anderson's fantasy works span several decades and settings but share a common sensibility rooted in Northern European myth, the Matter of France, and the cosmic struggle between Law and Chaos. In *Three Hearts and Three Lions* (1961), Holger Carlsen, a Danish-American engineer, is transported during World War II to a parallel world where the legends of Charlemagne and his paladins are historical fact. This world is divided between the forces of **Law** — Christianity, feudal order, and human civilisation — and **Chaos** — the realm of Faerie, with its elves, trolls, and shape-shifting enchantments. The borderlands between these domains are contested and dangerous, and the balance between them determines the fate of both worlds.

In *Operation Chaos* (1971) and its sequel *Operation Luna* (1999), Anderson imagines an alternate twentieth century in which magic was rediscovered during the Renaissance and developed alongside (and largely in place of) technology. Werewolves serve in the U.S. Army, witches hold professional credentials, broomsticks replace automobiles, and the Korean War is fought with magical as well as conventional arms. The setting is satirical and affectionate — a Cold War America where the supernatural is domesticated but never entirely safe.
