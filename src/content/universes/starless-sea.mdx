## Overview

The Starless Sea is a place beneath the earth where stories are preserved, tended, and lived. Its Harbor houses an enormous library maintained by three orders — Keepers, Collectors, and Guardians — each devoted to the curation of narrative in its many forms. Stories here are not merely written; they manifest as honey, as oceans, as architecture, as living beings. Doors appear throughout the surface world for those whom stories call, and keys unlock not just physical passages but metaphorical thresholds between reading a story and inhabiting it.

The realm operates on fairy-tale logic rather than physical law. Things come in threes, bargains carry binding weight, symbols possess literal power, and the boundary between fiction and reality is porous to the point of meaninglessness. Zachary Ezra Rawlins discovers this world when he finds a book in a library that contains a story about his own childhood — a moment when he chose not to open a painted door, a choice the narrative has been waiting for him to revisit.
