## Setting

Darkover is the third planet of the Cottman star system, a cold, mountainous world settled by a lost Terran colony ship whose crash survivors intermarried with the native chieri and over millennia developed a feudal civilization centred on hereditary psychic abilities collectively called **laran**. The seven great families of the **Comyn** — Hastur, Elhalyn, Ardais, Aillard, Ridenow, Alton, and Aldaran — each carry distinct laran gifts bred through generations of deliberate intermarriage. The **Towers** (Arilinn, Dalereuth, Neskaya, and others) serve as training institutions and workplaces where telepaths combine their abilities in **circles** to perform large-scale matrix operations.

Darkover's defining cultural law is the **Compact**, which bans any weapon that can kill beyond arm's reach, a response to the devastating Ages of Chaos when matrix-powered superweapons nearly destroyed the planet. When the Terran Empire rediscovers Darkover, the collision between a technological interstellar civilization and a feudal psi-based culture drives much of the series' tension. Marion Zimmer Bradley's Darkover novels (published from 1958 to 1996, with later contributions by other authors) span thousands of years of in-world history, from the original colonization through the Ages of Chaos, the Compact era, and the period of Terran recontact.
