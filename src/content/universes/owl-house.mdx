## Setting

The Boiling Isles is a landmass formed from the decomposing body of a fallen titan, surrounded by a boiling sea. Its inhabitants — witches, demons, and various magical creatures — practice magic through a biological organ called the bile sac, which connects to the heart and allows practitioners to cast spells by drawing circles in the air. Emperor Belos has reorganized magical society around the Coven System, which restricts each witch to a single school of magic (potions, plants, abomination, illusion, oracle, healing, beast keeping, bard, construction) branded onto their wrist, suppressing all other magical abilities. This system of control is revealed as part of Belos's plan to destroy all witches, making the political structure of magic the show's central conflict. Luz Noceda, a human without a bile sac, discovers that the titan's body contains geometric glyphs — ice, fire, light, plant — that can be drawn, combined, and activated to produce magic without biological prerequisites, revealing that the current system is a corruption of an older, more democratic magical tradition.
