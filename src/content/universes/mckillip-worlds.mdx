## Setting

Patricia A. McKillip's major fantasy works inhabit distinct but thematically connected worlds. The **Riddle-Master** trilogy (*The Riddle-Master of Hed*, 1976; *Heir of Sea and Fire*, 1977; *Harpist in the Wind*, 1979) takes place across a realm of small kingdoms governed by land-rulers who share a mystical bond with their territory, overseen by the enigmatic **High One** in Erlenstar Mountain. The world's intellectual and magical tradition is **riddlery** — the discipline of encoding fundamental truths in the form of riddles with strict answers, taught at the College of Caithnard. The trilogy follows Morgon, Prince of Hed, who bears three mysterious stars on his face and must unravel the deepest riddles of his world's history to understand what he is.

*The Forgotten Beasts of Eld* (1974) is set in a different world where the wizard-woman **Sybel** lives on Eld Mountain with a menagerie of legendary creatures she controls through the art of **Calling** — summoning beasts by knowing their true names. McKillip's works are unified by the conviction that magic is fundamentally an act of understanding: to know a thing's true name or essential nature is to hold power over it, and the ethical weight of that power is the central question her protagonists face.
