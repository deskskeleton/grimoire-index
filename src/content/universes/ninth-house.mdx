## Overview

In Bardugo's version of New Haven, Yale's famous secret societies are not merely exclusive social clubs — they are genuine magical orders, each specializing in a distinct occult discipline. Skull and Bones practices necromancy, Book and Snake deals in prognostication, Scroll and Key opens portals, and so on. Their rituals are powered by Grays, the ghosts of the dead who are drawn to New Haven in unusual concentrations.

Lethe, the ninth and least known society, exists to police the other eight and ensure their magic does not spill over into the mundane world. The protagonist, Alex Stern, is recruited to Lethe because she possesses a rare innate ability to see Grays without ritual assistance. The setting layers real Yale geography, architecture, and institutional culture with a hidden world of exploitation, in which the dead are consumed as fuel by the privileged living.
