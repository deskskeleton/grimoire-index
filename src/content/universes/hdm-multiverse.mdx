## Setting

*His Dark Materials* (trilogy 1995–2000, companion volumes 2003– ) and *The Book of Dust* (2017– ) are set across a **multiverse** of parallel worlds. The primary world — Lyra's Oxford — resembles a Victorian-Edwardian England governed by a theocratic institution called the Magisterium, where every human's soul takes the external form of a sentient animal companion called a **dæmon**. A secondary world — Will's Oxford — is recognizably our own. Other worlds include the land of the dead, the realm of the mulefa (wheeled beings who depend on seedpod trees), and the arctic wastes where armoured bears hold sovereignty.

The trilogy's cosmological substrate is **Dust** — known in different worlds as Shadows, dark matter, sraf, or Rusakov particles. Dust is an elementary particle of consciousness: it is attracted to sentient beings, accumulates around adults (especially after puberty), and is repelled by certain forms of severing or spiritual trauma. The Magisterium in Lyra's world regards Dust as physical evidence of Original Sin and seeks to eliminate or control it; the trilogy's arc reveals Dust to be benign, necessary, and the foundation of consciousness itself. The **Subtle Knife** — an artifact that cuts windows between worlds — and the **alethiometer** — a truth-telling compass powered by Dust — are the principal magical instruments.
