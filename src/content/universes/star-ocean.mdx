## Setting

The Star Ocean universe spans a galaxy governed by the Pangalactic Federation, an interstellar civilization bound by the Underdeveloped Planet Preservation Pact (UP3) that prohibits interference with pre-spacefaring worlds. The series' recurring narrative structure places protagonists from advanced civilizations on underdeveloped planets where Symbology — the science of manipulating reality through symbolic geometric patterns — is indistinguishable from magic to the local population. The deeper lore reveals increasingly meta layers: the universe exists within a simulated reality called the Eternal Sphere, created by beings from a higher dimensional plane called 4D Space. This revelation reframes all Symbological power as manipulation of the simulation's underlying code, positioning magic as programming in a universe that is literally a video game within the fiction. The tension between technological rationalism and apparent mysticism drives the series' thematic core across multiple entries spanning thousands of years of in-universe history.
