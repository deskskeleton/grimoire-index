---
name: The World of the Belgariad
creator: David Eddings
description: A high fantasy world governed by competing divine Prophecies — one for Order, one for Chaos — where a small group of sorcerers guided by the Purpose work across millennia to ensure the correct Prophecy prevails, with magic operating through the elegant simplicity of focused will released through a spoken word.
---

The world of the **Belgariad** and **Malloreon** is divided between the western kingdoms (aligned with the god Aldur and the Purpose) and the eastern Angarak nations (formerly aligned with the dark god Torak). The world's history is shaped by a cosmological conflict between two competing Prophecies — the Light and the Dark — each working to bring about a final confrontation that will determine the universe's nature.

Eddings' world is deliberately constructed as a classic high-fantasy setting: distinct cultures mapped to clear geographical regions, a prophecy-driven plot, and magic that operates through clean, intuitive principles. The setting's charm lies in its character interactions and the warmth of its ensemble cast rather than in grimdark complexity — it is unabashedly a story about good versus evil, told with humor and affection.
