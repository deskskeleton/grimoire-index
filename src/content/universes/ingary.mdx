## Setting

Diana Wynne Jones' *Howl's Moving Castle* (1986) and its sequels (*Castle in the Air*, *House of Many Ways*) are set in the kingdom of Ingary and neighboring lands. Ingary operates by fairy-tale logic that Jones simultaneously embraces and subverts: the eldest of three siblings is expected to fail, witches hand out curses, and seven-league boots are standard travel equipment. Magic in Ingary is personal, colorful, and deeply tied to individual character. Wizards like Howl Pendragon are public figures — vain, dramatic, and emotionally complicated. The setting blends Welsh sensibility with fairy-tale convention, and its magic tends to manifest through relationships, bargains, and personal transformation rather than through systematic rules.
