## Setting

Naomi Novik's *Scholomance* trilogy (*A Deadly Education*, 2020; *The Last Graduate*, 2021; *The Golden Enclaves*, 2022) is set primarily inside the Scholomance itself — a semi-sentient magical construct suspended in the Void, built by the world's enclave wizards to give magical children a chance at surviving to adulthood. In the world outside, maleficaria — monsters that feed on mana and are drawn to magical children — make growing up as a wizard extraordinarily dangerous. The Scholomance was designed to concentrate that danger into a single, defensible location, but its protections have degraded over the decades.

Inside, there are no teachers, no administrators, and no adults. The school generates coursework, provides a library, and maintains its structure, but students must fend for themselves against the maleficaria that infiltrate the building through air vents, pipes, and cracks in the wards. The social structure is dominated by **enclaves** — powerful magical communities in the outside world whose children receive better supplies, better alliances, and better survival odds. Enclaveless wizards are left to scramble for scraps. Graduation is a literal gauntlet through a hall filled with maleficaria, and the trilogy's arc concerns El Higgins — a wizard with an affinity for mass destruction — and her efforts to change the system entirely.
