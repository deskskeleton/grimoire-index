## Setting

The Discworld is the setting of forty-one novels by Terry Pratchett (1983–2015), plus shorter fiction, maps, companion books, and adaptations. Magic is woven into the world's substance: the Disc has a high background magical field, and reality is locally negotiable. The two large institutional traditions are wizardry (the Unseen University in Ankh-Morpork — male, academic, status-obsessed) and witchcraft (rural, female, suspicious of theory). They coexist uneasily and rarely overlap. Many minor traditions sit alongside them: Igors (surgical pseudo-magic), wee free men (drunken pictsies), the Auditors (cosmological bookkeeping), Death itself.
