## Setting

N. K. Jemisin's Inheritance Trilogy (*The Hundred Thousand Kingdoms*, 2010; *The Broken Kingdoms*, 2010; *The Kingdom of Gods*, 2011) is set in a world dominated by the Arameri family, who rule from the floating palace of Sky over the Hundred Thousand Kingdoms. Their power derives from a singular advantage: they hold the enslaved gods themselves as weapons. After a divine war called the Gods' War, the victorious god Itempas (order, light, stasis) condemned the defeated Nahadoth (chaos, darkness, change) and numerous godling children to servitude under the Arameri, while the third creator Enefa (balance, life, death) was killed.

The trilogy explores what happens when that enforced order begins to crack. Yeine Darr, a barbarian noblewoman summoned to Sky, discovers her connection to the dead goddess Enefa. Oree Shoth, a blind artist in the city below Sky, finds she can see magic and paint with godblood. Sieh, the godling of childhood, confronts mortality when the divine hierarchy shifts. The world's magic operates on multiple tiers: the raw reality-shaping power of the Three, the lesser but still formidable abilities of the godlings, the inherited gifts of god-blooded mortals, and the learned craft of scriveners who channel divine language into mortal sigils.
