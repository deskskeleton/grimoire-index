## Setting

*Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba* (manga 2016–2020, anime 2019– ) is set in early-twentieth-century Japan during the Taishō period. The world's central conceit is that **demons** — once-human predators created by the blood of a progenitor, Muzan Kibutsuji — have been a persistent, hidden threat for centuries. They regenerate from almost any wound, can only be killed by sunlight or decapitation with a special alloy blade, and grow stronger by consuming humans. The **Demon Slayer Corps** is the institutional response: a volunteer army of swordsmen who train in **Breathing Styles** — combat disciplines that enhance the body through controlled breathing — and wield **Nichirin Blades** forged from ore exposed to sunlight. The Corps operates without government recognition, funded by patron families, and its existence is unknown to the broader population.

The demon hierarchy is anchored by the **Twelve Kizuki** — an upper and lower rank of Muzan's most powerful subordinates — and by Muzan himself, the first and strongest demon. On the human side, the **Hashira** (Pillars) are the nine strongest living slayers, each master of a specific Breathing Style. The story follows Tanjiro Kamado's campaign against Muzan after his family is killed and his sister Nezuko is turned into a demon.
