## Setting

Twig is set in an alternate 1920s where Mary Shelley's Frankenstein was nonfiction — the principles of biological engineering were discovered in the early nineteenth century and reshaped civilization. The British Crown maintains global dominance through Academies that produce bioengineered horrors: stitched-together soldiers, tailored plagues, living architecture, and designer organisms. The protagonists are the Lambs — a group of child experiments, each modified in different ways — who serve as the Crown's covert operatives. The "magic" is entirely biological science, but its effects are functionally supernatural.
