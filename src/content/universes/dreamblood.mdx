## Setting

N. K. Jemisin's Dreamblood duology (*The Killing Moon*, 2012; *The Shadowed Sun*, 2012) is set in Gujaareh, a prosperous city-state modelled on ancient Egypt, where dreaming is sacred and the priesthood of the goddess Hananja has developed narcomancy — the art of entering and manipulating dreams to harvest magical substances from the sleeping mind. Gujaareh's culture, law, medicine, and spiritual life all revolve around the Hetawa temple and its dream-priests.

The city's peace is maintained in part by the Gatherers, elite priests who collect dreamblood by entering the dreams of the dying, the suffering, or the corrupt and granting them a final, blissful death. Sharers use harvested dream-humors to heal the sick and injured. Sentinels protect the temple and the city using combat applications of dream-magic. The duology explores what happens when this system is corrupted — when a Gatherer becomes a Reaper, killing indiscriminately, and when the political leadership of Gujaareh exploits narcomancy for conquest rather than peace.
