## Setting

The Land is the setting of Stephen R. Donaldson's *Chronicles of Thomas Covenant* (two trilogies and a final tetralogy, beginning with *Lord Foul's Bane* in 1977). It is a secondary world of extraordinary natural beauty — lush grasslands, soaring mountains, ancient forests — whose vitality is an expression of **Earthpower**, the ambient magical force that pervades all living and geological matter. The health of the Land is not merely metaphorical: when the Land is well, its grass heals wounds and its waters restore strength; when it is corrupted, the corruption is literal and physical.

The Land's history is shaped by the conflict between its peoples and **Lord Foul the Despiser**, a cosmic antagonist imprisoned within the Arch of Time who seeks to break free by destroying the Land. The Lords of Revelstone, the Giants of Seareach, the Ramen horse-tenders, and the Haruchai warriors all draw on Earthpower in different ways to resist Foul's depredations. Into this world Donaldson drops Thomas Covenant, a modern-day leper whose white-gold wedding ring is an artifact of **wild magic** — a chaotic, transcendent force capable of rewriting the foundations of reality. The tension between Covenant's disbelief in the Land and his possession of this ultimate power drives the entire series. The Land also features the **Sunbane**, a corruption of Earthpower imposed by Lord Foul during the Second Chronicles, which perverts the natural cycle of seasons into a destructive magical phenomenon.
