The Kate Daniels series (Ilona Andrews, 2007- ) is set in a version of Atlanta transformed by the Shift, a catastrophic event that introduced alternating waves of magic and technology into the world. During magic waves, technology fails — cars stall, phones die, skyscrapers warp and collapse — while magical creatures, ley lines, and supernatural powers surge to full strength. During tech waves, the process reverses: electricity flows, engines run, and magic weakens to a whisper. Society has adapted to this unpredictability by maintaining parallel infrastructure: horses alongside cars, swords alongside guns, and medieval fortifications alongside modern buildings.

The political landscape of post-Shift Atlanta is dominated by competing supernatural factions. The Pack — a coalition of shapeshifters governed by the Beast Lord — controls significant territory and operates as a quasi-military organization. The People, a necromantic corporation, pilot undead vampires (mindless predators, not the romanticized kind) via remote psychic control and wield enormous economic influence. The Order of Merciful Aid polices supernatural threats. Various mythological beings, gods, and ancient powers have re-emerged with the magic waves, and Atlanta sits at a nexus of ley lines that makes it a perpetual flashpoint. Kate Daniels navigates this landscape while concealing her own lineage — she is the daughter of Roland, an ancient immortal whose power-word magic shaped civilizations.
