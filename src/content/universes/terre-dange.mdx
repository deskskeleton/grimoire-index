## Setting

Jacqueline Carey's **Kushiel's Legacy** sequence (beginning with *Kushiel's Dart*, 2001, and spanning six novels in two linked trilogies) is set in Terre d'Ange, a nation occupying roughly the geography of France in an alternate early-medieval Europe. The D'Angeline people descend from **Blessed Elua**, an angel born of the blood of Yeshua ben Yosef and the tears of the Magdalene, who wandered the earth followed by eight angelic Companions until the One God granted them a homeland. Each Companion founded a noble province and bequeathed specific gifts to their descendants: Cassiel's self-discipline, Naamah's gifts of love and desire, Shemhazai's thirst for knowledge, Kushiel's capacity to find redemption through punishment, and others.

The surrounding nations each possess their own cultural and magical traditions, from the Skaldi's rune-magic and the Yeshuite messianic prophecy to Carthage's forbidden demon-summoning (**horologia**) and the Tsingani's divinatory gift of the **dromonde**. The gods of this world are real and occasionally intervene directly in mortal affairs. The series' primary viewpoint character, **Phedre no Delaunay**, is an **anguissette** — one marked by the angel Kushiel to experience pain as pleasure — whose unique nature makes her both a gifted courtesan-spy and an instrument of divine purpose.
