## Setting

Disney's animated canon does not share a single continuity but establishes a remarkably consistent magical grammar across decades of films. Magic flows through archetypal channels: villains curse through spite and jealousy (Maleficent's spinning wheel, the Evil Queen's poisoned apple, Ursula's voice-stealing contract), fairy godmothers bestow blessings with conditions and time limits (Cinderella's midnight deadline), genies grant wishes with ironic constraints, and true love's kiss breaks enchantments that no other force can overcome. This grammar creates a moral cosmology where magic reflects the character of its wielder — benevolent magic is selfless and conditional, dark magic is selfish and contractual, and the most powerful force in any Disney universe is authentic emotional connection rather than supernatural ability. The tradition has evolved from the passive enchantments of Snow White and Sleeping Beauty through the active agency of characters like Elsa, Rapunzel, and Moana, but the core principle that magic serves story rather than system remains the defining feature of Disney's approach.
