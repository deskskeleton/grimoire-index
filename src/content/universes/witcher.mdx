---
name: The Witcher World (The Continent)
creator: Andrzej Sapkowski
description: A dark fantasy world shaped by the Conjunction of the Spheres — an ancient cataclysm that merged multiple dimensions — where humans, elves, dwarves, and monsters coexist uneasily, and magic is a volatile natural force harnessed by sorcerers and mutated monster-hunters alike.
---

The Continent is a landmass populated by humans (relative newcomers), elder races (elves, dwarves, gnomes), and a vast bestiary of monsters that arrived during the Conjunction of the Spheres, an interdimensional collision that deposited creatures and magical energy into the world. Magic permeates the setting as Chaos, a raw elemental force that some individuals — Sources — can innately sense and channel. Two major magical traditions have emerged: academic sorcery, practised by formally trained mages organised into political bodies like the Brotherhood of Sorcerers and the Lodge of Sorceresses, and witcher mutations, an alchemical process that transforms children into monster-hunting professionals with enhanced physical abilities and limited combat magic.

The world is politically fractured into Northern Kingdoms perpetually threatened by the expansionist Nilfgaardian Empire. Sapkowski uses this setting to explore moral ambiguity, the politics of prejudice (humans persecute elves and non-humans despite being colonisers themselves), and the impossibility of true neutrality. The witcher Geralt of Rivia navigates a world where monsters are sometimes more humane than the humans who hire him to kill them.
