## Setting

The Supernatural universe presents a version of America where every folk belief, urban legend, and religious tradition contains a kernel of dangerous truth. Monsters are real, Heaven and Hell are bureaucracies staffed by angels and demons with their own political factions, and God (Chuck) is a writer who treats creation as his narrative playground. Magic exists across a vast spectrum: human practitioners use spell components, incantations, and ritual circles drawn from grimoires; demons grant power through soul contracts; angels channel divine grace; and ancient pagan gods draw strength from worship and sacrifice. The Men of Letters, a secret scholarly society, catalogued supernatural phenomena and magical artifacts for generations before their destruction. The Winchester brothers operate at the intersection of all these traditions, using whatever works — holy water, iron, silver, Latin exorcisms, angel blades, witch-killing bullets — creating a pragmatic, kitchen-sink approach to the supernatural that reflects the show's blue-collar ethos.
