## Setting

The Legend of Zelda series (1986-present) is set primarily in Hyrule, a kingdom whose history is defined by the cyclical conflict between the bearers of the Triforce. Magic in Zelda takes many forms across entries: the original game's spell system (Shield, Jump, Life, Fairy), Ocarina of Time's song-based magic and Din's Fire/Nayru's Love/Farore's Wind goddess spells, Breath of the Wild's rune abilities (Magnesis, Stasis, Cryonis, Bombs) derived from Sheikah technology, and Tears of the Kingdom's Zonai-derived Ultrahand/Fuse/Ascend/Recall abilities. The Triforce itself is the setting's supreme magical artifact, granting wishes to those who touch it with a balanced heart. Each game reinterprets what magic means in Hyrule while maintaining the thematic core of divine relics, musical invocation, and the hero's destined connection to sacred power.
