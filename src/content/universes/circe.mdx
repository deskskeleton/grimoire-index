## Overview

Miller's mythological world is the Greek pantheon rendered with psychological realism. The Titans have fallen, the Olympians rule, and beneath them exists a vast hierarchy of nymphs, lesser gods, and monsters whose lives are shaped by the whims of those above them. Mortals live and die in the span of a divine afternoon, which gives their choices a weight that immortals struggle to understand.

Circe's island of Aiaia sits at the margins of this world — close enough to the centers of divine power to be dangerous, remote enough to permit independence. It is here that Circe develops pharmakeia, a form of magic rooted in the natural world rather than in divine authority. Her exile becomes the condition for her discovery, and her island becomes a laboratory where the boundaries between divine, mortal, and monstrous are tested.
