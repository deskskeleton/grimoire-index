## Setting

*A Song of Ice and Fire* (and its HBO adaptation *Game of Thrones*) is set across the continent of Westeros and the broader Known World. Magic in this setting is deliberately sparse, ambiguous, and frequently entangled with religion. For most of recorded history, magic has been in retreat — dragons died out, the seasons grew unpredictable, and overt supernatural phenomena faded into legend. The events of the series coincide with magic's return: dragons are reborn, ancient ice creatures stir beyond the Wall, and dormant magical traditions reactivate. Martin treats magic as something that disrupts rather than enables, and its practitioners are rarely heroic figures — they are priests, assassins, greenseers, and people touched by forces they do not fully control.
