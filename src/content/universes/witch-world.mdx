## Setting

Andre Norton's **Witch World** series, begun with *Witch World* (1963) and spanning dozens of novels and short stories over four decades, is set on a world accessible from Earth through interdimensional **gates** — remnants of a vanished civilisation whose technology was indistinguishable from magic. The primary setting is the continent containing **Estcarp**, a matriarchal nation ruled and defended by the **Witches**, an order of women who wield mental magic channeled through personal jewels.

Beyond Estcarp lie numerous lands with their own magical traditions: **Escore**, the ancient eastern homeland where the Old Race once practised high magic before catastrophe drove them west; **High Hallack**, where the dames and lords contend with inhuman invaders; and **Arvon**, where the Were-Riders shift between human and animal form. The world's deep history involves the **Adepts**, sorcerers of immense power who nearly destroyed Escore through reckless experimentation with dark forces, and whose legacy — sealed tombs, cursed artifacts, dormant gates — provides much of the series' ongoing danger. Simon Tregarth, a fugitive from mid-twentieth-century Earth, passes through the Doorknocker Gate and discovers he possesses latent magical talent that makes him unique among the men of Estcarp.
