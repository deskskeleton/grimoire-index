The Southern Vampire Mysteries (Charlaine Harris, 2001-2013) are set in the small Louisiana town of Bon Temps and the surrounding parishes, in a version of the present day where vampires have recently "come out of the coffin" — their public revelation made possible by the Japanese invention of synthetic blood (marketed as TrueBlood), which gave vampires an alternative to feeding on humans and thereby a political argument for legal recognition. Other supernatural species — werewolves, werepanthers, shapeshifters, fairies, witches, maenads — remain hidden from human awareness to varying degrees, each operating under their own social structures and territorial rules.

The world is characterized by a layered disclosure model: vampires are public and politically active (lobbying for civil rights, running businesses, maintaining a hierarchical sheriff-and-king governance structure), while the two-natured (shifters and Weres) remain closeted for most of the series, and fairies operate almost entirely out of human sight. Sookie Stackhouse's telepathy — later revealed to be a product of her fairy heritage — places her at the intersection of these overlapping communities, making her valuable to and endangered by all of them.
