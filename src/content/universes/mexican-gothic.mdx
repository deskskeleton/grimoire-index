## Setting

Silvia Moreno-Garcia's *Mexican Gothic* (2020) is set in the early 1950s, primarily at **High Place**, a decaying Victorian mansion in the mountains of rural Mexico built by the English **Doyle** family. The Doyles made their fortune in silver mining, but the true source of their longevity and power is a vast underground **fungal organism** that permeates the house, the surrounding land, and the mine shafts beneath the estate. The family patriarch, **Howard Doyle**, discovered the fungus generations ago and entered into a symbiotic relationship with it — the fungus sustains his consciousness beyond natural death, and he in turn feeds it through the bodies and minds of family members, servants, and anyone who stays at High Place long enough.

The fungus generates a psychic network called the **gloom** — a shared mental space where the living, the dead, and the fungal intelligence itself intermingle. Exposure to fungal spores through contaminated food, water, and air makes newcomers increasingly susceptible to hallucinations, memory manipulation, and psychic coercion. The protagonist, **Noemí Taboada**, travels to High Place to check on her recently married cousin Catalina and must resist the gloom's creeping influence while uncovering the true horror of the Doyle family's bargain.
