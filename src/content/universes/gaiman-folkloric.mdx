---
name: Gaiman Folkloric Worlds
creator: Neil Gaiman
description: Hidden magical realms that exist alongside or beneath the mundane world, operating on fairy-tale logic where bargains bind, names hold power, and the ordinary world remains willfully blind to the extraordinary.
---

Neil Gaiman's standalone fantasy novels share a common sensibility: the magical world is not distant but adjacent, separated from the mundane by a wall, a crack in the pavement, or a simple failure of attention. London Below in Neverwhere is a shadow city beneath the streets where the homeless and forgotten become literal inhabitants of a fantastical underworld. Faerie in Stardust lies beyond a gap in a stone wall in an English village, a realm of witches, living stars, and ruthless commerce in magical goods.

These worlds operate on folkloric principles rather than systematic magic. Promises made cannot be broken without consequence. Names carry binding power. Doors open for those who know how to ask — or who have the right blood. The mundane world actively resists awareness of the magical, not through conspiracy but through a kind of psychological gravity that pulls ordinary people away from the extraordinary. Those who cross over find that the rules of fairy tales are not metaphors but operating instructions, and that the price of magic is always paid, though rarely in the currency you expect.
