## Setting

T. H. White's Arthurian England is a deliberately anachronistic medieval world where knights joust, castles dot the landscape, and a handful of magical beings operate at the margins of human society. Merlin, who ages in reverse, uses his foreknowledge and shapeshifting magic primarily as educational tools — transforming the young Wart into animals so he can learn lessons about governance, war, and human nature. The setting blends genuine medieval atmosphere with pointed modern satire, and magic serves more as metaphor than mechanism.
