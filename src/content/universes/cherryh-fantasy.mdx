## Setting

C. J. Cherryh's fantasy works span two major series with different settings but overlapping thematic concerns. The **Morgaine Cycle** (*Gate of Ivrel*, 1976; *Well of Shiuan*, 1978; *Fires of Azeroth*, 1979; *Exile's Gate*, 1988) follows Morgaine, the last survivor of a failed mission to close the **qhal Gates** — ancient devices that enable travel through time and space. The Gates were built by the qhal, a powerful elder race, but their misuse has corrupted timelines and threatens to unravel reality itself. Each novel takes place on a different world connected by Gates, and Morgaine's companion Vanye narrates as an oath-bound warrior from a feudal culture that barely understands the technology around him.

The **Fortress series** (*Fortress in the Eye of Time*, 1995; *Fortress of Eagles*, 2000; *Fortress of Owls*, 2001; *Fortress of Dragons*, 2002) is set in a single medieval-style world where wizardry operates through **Shaping** — the ability to define and create reality through will and naming. The wizard Mauryl **Shaped** Tristen, the protagonist, as a being who must discover his own nature and purpose. Both series share Cherryh's signature concern with the ethics of power: in each case, the characters who wield the deepest magic must ultimately choose between using it and containing or destroying it.
