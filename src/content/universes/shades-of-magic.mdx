## Setting

V. E. Schwab's *Shades of Magic* trilogy (*A Darker Shade of Magic*, 2015; *A Gathering of Shadows*, 2016; *A Conjuring of Light*, 2017) is built on the premise that four parallel Londons exist, each occupying the same geographic space but separated by dimensional barriers. **Grey London** is our world circa the Regency era — mundane, magicless, and unaware of the others. **Red London** (Arnes) is a thriving magical civilization where elemental magic flows freely and is woven into daily life. **White London** (Makt) is a harsh, dying world where magic is scarce and tyrant-rulers hoard what remains through violence and domination. **Black London** was consumed entirely by magic — an uncontrolled, predatory force called Osaron — and sealed off centuries ago to prevent contagion.

The worlds were once more connected, but the doors between them were deliberately closed after Black London's fall. The only beings who can still cross between worlds are the **Antari** — exceedingly rare blood-mages marked by one solid black eye, who use blood-script commands (*As Travars*, "to travel") to open passages. The trilogy follows Kell, one of the last two known Antari, and Lila Bard, a Grey London thief who becomes entangled in a crisis that threatens to unseal Black London's corruption.
