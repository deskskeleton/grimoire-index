## Setting

Tim Powers' *Declare* (2001) reimagines the Cold War as a shadow conflict fought not merely between ideologies but over access to supernatural entities. The djinn of Mount Ararat are ancient, non-human intelligences that predate recorded civilization, and both the British SOE and the Soviet GRU maintained covert operations aimed at controlling or bargaining with them. The novel interweaves the real biography of Kim Philby — the infamous double agent — with a fictional metaphysical explanation for his treachery. The setting spans Beirut, Moscow, wartime Paris, and the slopes of Ararat itself, grounding its supernatural elements in the documented tradecraft and bureaucratic paranoia of mid-century espionage.
