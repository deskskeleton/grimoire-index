## Overview

The Dark Tower multiverse is Stephen King's grand cosmology, connecting not only the eight novels of the Dark Tower series but also dozens of his other works. At the center of all existence stands the Dark Tower, a structure that serves as the axis point for an infinite number of parallel worlds. Six Beams — invisible lines of force guarded by animal Guardians — hold the Tower in place and keep reality stable. As the Beams break, the worlds move on, decaying in ways both physical and metaphysical.

Mid-World, the primary setting, is a post-apocalyptic landscape that blends Western frontier imagery with Arthurian legend and science fiction. Its gunslingers are knight-errants whose revolvers are forged from Excalibur's steel. The spaces between worlds — the todash darkness — are inhabited by monstrous entities, and travel between worlds is possible through thinny weak spots, magical doorways, and the Black Thirteen, a corrupted seeing-stone. Ka, the force of destiny, drives all things toward the Tower.
