---
name: Elfhame (Folk of the Air)
creator: Holly Black
description: A treacherous fae kingdom where mortal and immortal worlds overlap, governed by ancient rules of glamour, binding oaths, and the absolute prohibition against fae falsehood.
---

Elfhame is the primary fae realm in Holly Black's Folk of the Air trilogy, a place of dangerous beauty where the High Court rules over a society structured by power, cruelty, and elaborate magical etiquette. Mortals who enter Faerie are subject to its rules — vulnerable to glamour, compulsion, and the capricious whims of fae nobility — though a rare few develop resistance through prolonged exposure.

The political culture of Elfhame is inseparable from its magic. Oaths carry binding supernatural force, geasa impose magical prohibitions that cannot be broken without consequence, and the fae inability to lie creates a culture of elaborate linguistic maneuvering where truth is weaponized as effectively as any sword. The Court of Shadows operates as an intelligence network within this system, and succession to the throne is as much a matter of magical leverage as military strength.
