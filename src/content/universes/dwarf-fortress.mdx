## Setting

The world of Dwarf Fortress is generated fresh for each game, simulating thousands of years of history including the rise and fall of civilizations, wars, migrations, and the activities of individual historical figures. Gods are procedurally created with portfolios drawn from a vast list of spheres (death, nightmares, rivers, crafts, etc.), and some gods grant their followers secrets — most notably the secrets of life and death, which create necromancers capable of raising undead armies. Forgotten Beasts, procedurally generated megafauna with unique materials, body plans, and attack types, lurk in the underground cavern layers. The world contains no fixed spell system; instead, supernatural phenomena emerge from the simulation's rules. Vampires, werebeasts, and cursed artifacts all arise from historical events rather than predetermined encounters. The fortress mode places players in charge of a dwarven settlement where the intersection of mundane engineering and emergent supernatural threats creates stories of legendary complexity.
