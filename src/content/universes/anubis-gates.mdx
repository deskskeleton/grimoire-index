## Setting

Tim Powers' *The Anubis Gates* (1983) operates as secret history layered beneath the documented events of early-nineteenth-century London. Egyptian sorcerers — servants of the old gods whose power waned as Christianity spread — performed a massive ritual in 1802 that punched holes in time, creating unstable gates that allow passage between eras. The novel's magic is fueled by the fading residual energy of the Egyptian pantheon, and its practitioners are desperate, diminished figures rather than powerful archmages. The setting weaves together historical figures like Samuel Taylor Coleridge and Lord Byron with wholly invented horrors: the werewolf Dog-Face Joe, the body-switching clown Doyle eventually becomes, and the Master — an ancient sorcerer sustained far past his natural span by dark rituals.
