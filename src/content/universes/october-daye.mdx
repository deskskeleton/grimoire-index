The October Daye series (Seanan McGuire, 2009- ) is set in a version of the San Francisco Bay Area where the fae have maintained a hidden presence for centuries, their holdings concealed behind don't-look-here spells that deflect mortal attention. Faerie society is organized into a feudal structure of kingdoms and duchies, each governed from a knowe — an enchanted stronghold that exists partially outside normal space, accessed through hidden doors in mortal-world locations. The Kingdom of the Mists covers much of Northern California and is ruled (often poorly) by a succession of monarchs whose legitimacy depends on their bloodline's connection to the Three: Oberon, Maeve, and Titania, the progenitors of all fae races.

The world's central tension is the relationship between pureblooded fae and changelings — the half-human, half-fae children who occupy a precarious social position in both worlds. Changelings inherit diluted versions of their fae parent's magic and are generally treated as second-class citizens in fae society. October "Toby" Daye's discovery of her Dochas Sidhe heritage — a bloodline with the unique ability to manipulate the balance of fae and human blood — upends the assumption that changeling status is fixed, and introduces a power that the pureblooded nobility finds both invaluable and deeply threatening.
