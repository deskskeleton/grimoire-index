## Setting

Burning Wheel, first published in 2002, is not tied to a specific setting but provides a gritty, low-to-mid fantasy framework that emphasizes character motivation over world-saving heroics. The game's core conceit is that characters are defined by their Beliefs — explicit statements of intent that drive play and earn advancement. Magic exists in multiple subsystems: human Sorcery (tax-based spellcasting), Faith (prayer-based miracles), Elven Song (emotional magic woven through music), Dwarven Greed (obsessive craft-magic), Orcish Hatred (rage-fueled brutality), and Spirit Binding (summoning and negotiating with entities). Each subsystem reflects its practitioners' cultural values and costs them accordingly.
