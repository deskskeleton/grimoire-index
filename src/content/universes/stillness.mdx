## Setting

The setting of N. K. Jemisin's Hugo-sweeping *Broken Earth* trilogy (*The Fifth Season*, *The Obelisk Gate*, *The Stone Sky*; 2015–2017) is a single continent ironically named the Stillness. Tectonic activity is severe enough that civilizations are periodically obliterated by **Seasons** — multi-year apocalyptic intervals triggered by volcanism, ash-clouded skies, and ecosystem collapse — and the continent's accumulated **Stone Lore** is the cultural memory of how to survive them.

The Stillness is governed by the Sanzed Empire and stabilized by **orogenes**, a hereditary caste of practitioners whose innate ability to absorb and redirect kinetic and thermal energy makes them indispensable for road, rail, and city safety. The orogenes' practical value coexists with extreme social hatred: identified roggas in rural communities are killed, and Fulcrum-trained orogenes serve under the surveillance of the **Guardians**, a separate order whose own (distinct, unexplained-for-most-of-the-trilogy) abilities suppress orogenic action. The trilogy's deep history extends to the lost civilization of **Syl Anagist** and the floating crystalline **obelisks** that civilization left behind — devices that allow the highest-tier orogenes to operate at planetary scale. This catalog covers Orogeny as the primary system; Guardian abilities and the obelisk-network mechanics may merit follow-up entries.
