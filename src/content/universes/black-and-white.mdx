## Setting

The world of Black & White consists of a series of island realms populated by tribal civilizations who worship gods made manifest through collective belief. The player exists as a disembodied divine hand visible in the game world, capable of picking up objects, people, and resources, casting miracles through gesture-drawing, and influencing the world through a massive Creature — a semi-autonomous animal avatar that learns from the god's behavior and serves as their physical representative. The cosmology is explicitly transactional: gods exist because people believe in them, and a god without worshippers ceases to exist. This creates a dynamic where expanding one's sphere of influence through conversion (whether by benevolence or terror) is not merely a strategic goal but an existential necessity. The moral spectrum runs from radiant temples and grateful citizens to burned villages and terrified supplicants, with the game world's visual aesthetic literally shifting between bright pastoral beauty and dark Gothic horror based on the player's alignment.
