## Setting

Tamsyn Muir's *Locked Tomb* series (*Gideon the Ninth*, 2019; *Harrow the Ninth*, 2020; *Nona the Ninth*, 2022; *Alecto the Ninth*, 2024) is set in a far-future solar system where necromancy is the dominant — indeed, the only — form of magic. Ten thousand years before the series begins, John Gaius (the Necrolord Prime, King Undying, God Emperor) somehow achieved mastery over death itself, resurrected his closest allies as immortal Lyctors, and established the Nine Houses across the system's planets and moons.

Each House specialises in a different branch of necromancy: the Ninth in bones and osseous constructs, the Eighth in spirit-calling and soul magic, the Third in flesh and the body, the Sixth in psychometric analysis and spirit observation, and so on. Necromantic practice is bound by thanergetic and thalergetic energy — the energy released by death and the energy of life, respectively — and operates through formal theorems that treat death-manipulation as a quasi-scientific discipline. The cavalier-necromancer bond is both social institution and magical foundation: cavaliers provide physical protection and, in the ultimate expression of the system, their very souls. The series' deep lore involves the original sins of John Gaius, the nature of the Resurrection, and the existential threat of the Resurrection Beasts — cosmic entities created by John's original acts of planetary necromancy.
