## Setting

Call of Cthulhu, first published in 1981, is a tabletop RPG set primarily in the 1920s (with variants spanning from gaslight Victorian to modern and future eras). Players take the role of investigators — ordinary people drawn into encounters with the Cthulhu Mythos. Unlike heroic fantasy RPGs, characters are fragile and magic is dangerous; the central resource is Sanity, which erodes through exposure to alien truths and the casting of Mythos spells. The setting draws from H.P. Lovecraft, Clark Ashton Smith, Robert W. Chambers, and the broader weird fiction tradition. Trail of Cthulhu, published by Pelgrane Press using the GUMSHOE system, offers a complementary investigative approach to the same Mythos material.
