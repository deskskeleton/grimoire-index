## Setting

Calernia is a continent divided between the forces of Good and Evil, where the gods literally empower mortal champions through Names — archetypal Roles like the Black Knight, the White Knight, the Hierophant, and the Squire. Named individuals gain supernatural abilities, but more importantly, they are subject to narrative gravity: stories have weight in this world, and events tend to follow archetypal patterns (the lone hero defeats the tyrant; the villain's monologue enables the hero's escape; the third offer must be refused). The protagonist, Catherine Foundling, is a villain who systematically studies and exploits these narrative rules, turning the structure of stories into a weapon.
