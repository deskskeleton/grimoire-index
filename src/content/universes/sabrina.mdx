## Setting

Greendale exists at the intersection of mundane small-town America and a hidden magical society organized around the Church of Night, a Satanic coven that worships the Dark Lord as the source of all witchcraft. Witches and warlocks sign their names in the Book of the Beast during a Dark Baptism, pledging loyalty to Satan in exchange for magical power. The Academy of Unseen Arts serves as the educational institution for young witches, teaching spellcraft, demonology, and infernal traditions. The show's cosmology expands across seasons to include pagan gods, eldritch terrors from beyond creation, and alternative afterlives, but the core tension remains Sabrina's refusal to accept the binary choice between her mortal humanity and her witch heritage. Magic in Greendale is explicitly contractual — power comes from the Dark Lord and can be revoked — until Sabrina discovers that witchcraft predates Satan's claim on it, reframing the entire magical tradition as stolen rather than granted.
