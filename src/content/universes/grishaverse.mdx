The **Grishaverse** is the setting of Leigh Bardugo's interconnected fantasy novels, beginning with the *Shadow and Bone* trilogy (2012) and expanding through the *Six of Crows* duology and *King of Scars* duology. The world is modeled broadly on Imperial Russia, with the primary nation of Ravka divided by the Shadow Fold — a swath of magical darkness created by a Grisha of extraordinary power.

Grisha are born with the ability to manipulate matter at the molecular level, an ability they call the **Small Science** to distinguish it from superstition. Grisha are organized into three orders — **Corporalki** (body manipulators: Healers and Heartrenders), **Etherealki** (elemental manipulators: Squallers, Inferni, and Tidemakers), and **Materialki** (substance manipulators: Durasts and Alkemi) — and serve in Ravka's Second Army as military assets. The tension between Grisha as people and Grisha as state resources drives much of the series' political drama.
