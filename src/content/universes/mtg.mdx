## Setting

Magic: The Gathering, first published in 1993, is set across a multiverse of distinct planes — Dominaria, Ravnica, Innistrad, Zendikar, and dozens more — each with its own cultures, creatures, and magical traditions. The game's cosmology centers on mana, a magical energy drawn from land, which exists in five colors: White (order, community, law), Blue (knowledge, artifice, control), Black (ambition, death, self-interest), Red (emotion, chaos, freedom), and Green (nature, growth, instinct). These colors are not merely categories but philosophical positions, and the game's mechanical identity — the "color pie" — defines what each color can and cannot do. Planeswalkers, the game's protagonist figures, are the only beings capable of traveling between planes.
