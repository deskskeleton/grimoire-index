## Setting

Keyhouse Manor has been home to the Locke family for generations, and hidden throughout its rooms are magical keys forged from a substance called Whispering Iron, derived from a demonic dimension accessible through the Black Door in the manor's sea caves. Each key performs a single, specific function when used with a corresponding lock: the Head Key opens a person's skull to reveal and manipulate their memories, the Ghost Key separates spirit from body, the Gender Key transforms biological sex, the Anywhere Key opens any door to any location the user visualizes, and dozens more. The keys were originally forged by Revolutionary War-era Lockes who discovered the Black Door and the Whispering Iron beyond it. The central antagonist, Dodge (later revealed as a demon of Whispering Iron), manipulates the Locke children to collect keys and ultimately open the Black Door. A crucial rule governs the setting: adults cannot remember magic. Once a person reaches adulthood, all memories of the keys and their effects fade, creating a world where children bear the full burden of magical knowledge while the adults around them remain oblivious.
