## Setting

Wizardry (1981-2023) and Might and Magic (1986-2014) are the foundational dungeon-crawler CRPGs whose magic systems influenced virtually every RPG that followed. Wizardry's spell system uses unique nomenclature (KATINO for sleep, HALITO for fireball, DUMAPIC for mapping) organized into seven spell levels accessible by Mages and Priests. Might and Magic uses more conventional elemental and clerical spell categories across increasingly elaborate class systems. Both series pioneered the concepts of spell slots, class-restricted magic, and the dungeon-crawling party composition that requires balancing martial and magical roles. Their combined influence on Dragon Quest, Final Fantasy, and the JRPG genre makes them among the most historically consequential magic systems in gaming.
