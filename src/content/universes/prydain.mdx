## Setting

Lloyd Alexander's **Chronicles of Prydain** (1964–1968) unfold across a five-novel arc: *The Book of Three*, *The Black Cauldron*, *The Castle of Llyr*, *Taran Wanderer*, and *The High King*. The setting is Prydain, a land modelled on the Wales of the *Mabinogion* — a world of petty kingdoms, ancient forests, and ruined strongholds, overshadowed by the threat of **Arawn Death-Lord**, who rules from the fortress of Annuvin in the Land of the Dead.

Prydain is populated by enchanters, bards, warriors, and farmers, and its history is shaped by magical artifacts of great power: the **Black Crochan** (a cauldron that raises the dead as soulless Cauldron-Born warriors), **Dyrnwyn** (a flaming sword that destroys the unworthy), and the **Book of Three** (an oracular text that reveals the future at great cost). The protagonist, Taran, begins as an Assistant Pig-Keeper tending the oracular pig **Hen Wen** at the farm of the enchanter **Dallben**, and grows through the series into a figure worthy of kingship — not through magic but through character.
