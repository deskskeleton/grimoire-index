## Setting

Fonda Lee's *Green Bone Saga* (*Jade City*, 2017; *Jade War*, 2019; *Jade Legacy*, 2021) is set primarily on the island nation of Kekon, a society modelled on post-war East Asian nations (particularly Taiwan, Hong Kong, and South Korea) during a period of rapid modernisation and geopolitical tension. Kekon's defining resource is **jade** — a bioenergetic mineral found naturally on the island that, when worn against the skin by those with biological sensitivity, grants superhuman abilities.

Kekon's society is dominated by the **Green Bone clans** — warrior-aristocratic organisations that function as part martial order, part organised crime family, part governing institution. The two major clans, No Peak and the Mountain, control districts of the capital city of Janloon and compete for jade territory, political influence, and international reach. The trilogy follows the Kaul family, who lead No Peak, across three decades of clan war, foreign entanglement, and social transformation. Beyond Kekon, foreign nations covet jade for military applications, leading to international smuggling, the development of synthetic jade-tolerance drugs (SN1 and its successors), and a global arms race that threatens to upend Kekon's traditional way of life.
