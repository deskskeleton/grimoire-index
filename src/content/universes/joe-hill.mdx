---
name: Joe Hill's Inscapeverse
creator: Joe Hill
description: A loosely connected fictional universe where certain individuals manifest personal pocket dimensions and supernatural abilities fueled by obsessive creativity and psychological fixation, adjacent to but distinct from Stephen King's broader multiverse.
---

Joe Hill's novels and stories share a world where the supernatural is deeply personal — abilities and horrors emerge from individual psychology rather than codified magical systems. In this universe, creative obsession can literally reshape reality: a psychic vampire builds an entire dimension from stolen childhood joy, a young woman's desperate need to find lost things manifests as an impossible bridge, and a fungal plague responds to its hosts' emotional states. The connections between Hill's works are subtle — shared references, overlapping geographies, and a tonal consistency that treats the supernatural as an extension of character rather than an external force.

The universe maintains a loose but acknowledged connection to Stephen King's multiverse (Hill is King's son, and the works share references to Pennywise, Mid-World, and the concept of psychic "shine"), but operates with its own internal logic. Where King's cosmology tends toward grand mythic structures — the Dark Tower, the Turtle, ka — Hill's supernatural is more intimate, more closely tied to the specific psychology of its practitioners, and more interested in the question of what it costs a person to become the kind of mind that can break reality.
