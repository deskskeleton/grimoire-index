## Setting

The Queen's Thief series takes place in a world of small, rival kingdoms — Sounis, Eddis, and Attolia — situated around an inland sea in a landscape that echoes classical Greece, with a political culture closer to Byzantium. Technology sits roughly at a Renaissance level, with gunpowder weapons beginning to appear alongside swords and court diplomacy. The Mede Empire looms as a colonial threat from across the sea, applying pressure that drives much of the series' geopolitics.

## The gods

The gods of this world are real and occasionally active, though their interventions are subtle enough that many characters treat them as mythological rather than literal. Eugenides (the god of thieves), Hephestia, Moira, and others operate through dreams, signs, and quiet manipulation of probability rather than overt miracles. The tension between divine agency and human choice is one of the series' central themes — readers and characters alike are left to decide how much of what happens is luck, skill, or the hand of a god.
