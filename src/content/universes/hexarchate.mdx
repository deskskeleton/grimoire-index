---
name: The Hexarchate
creator: Yoon Ha Lee
description: A far-future interstellar empire whose technology and weaponry depend on universal adherence to a shared calendar — a mathematical-ritual consensus reality where heresy is not just rebellion but an existential threat to the physics that hold the state together.
---

The hexarchate is a totalitarian interstellar polity governed by six factions, each responsible for a different function of state: the Kel (military), the Shuos (intelligence and espionage), the Rahal (law and doctrine), the Nirai (technology and research), the Andan (cultural engineering), and the Vidona (enforcement and re-education). A seventh faction, the Liozh (ethics and philosophy), was destroyed for calendrical heresy before the events of the novels. The hexarchate's power structure is inseparable from its calendar — the mathematical framework that makes its exotic technologies possible — and maintaining calendrical orthodoxy is the regime's highest priority.

The setting spans multiple star systems connected by threshold drives, with space warfare conducted through formations of soldiers and ships whose geometric arrangements produce exotic effects. The series follows Cheris, a Kel infantry captain, and Jedao, an undead general infamous for massacring his own army, as they navigate a world where mathematics is literally weaponized and the calendar itself is a tool of political control. Lee uses the hexarchate to explore how authoritarian regimes sustain themselves through the intertwining of ideology, technology, and lived practice.
