## Shared metaphysics

The Cosmere is the umbrella for most of Brandon Sanderson's adult fantasy work. Its various worlds — Scadrial (Mistborn), Roshar (Stormlight Archive), Sel (Elantris), Nalthis (Warbreaker), Threnody, Taldain, and others — operate on different surface magic systems but share a common metaphysical foundation. Magic across the Cosmere is broadly understood as access to "Investiture," a finite metaphysical substance associated with one of sixteen Shards (fragments of a primordial divinity) that have come to inhabit different worlds.

Each Shard's character shapes the surface texture of the magic systems on its world. Scadrial, host to the Shards Preservation and Ruin, produces systems built around opposed pairs and discrete effects (Allomancy, Feruchemy, Hemalurgy). Roshar, host to Honor, Cultivation, and the splintered remains of Odium, produces systems built around oaths, growth, and emotional resonance (Surgebinding, Voidbinding). The mechanical surface of each world's magic is locally hard and well-defined, but cross-world interactions and metaphysical layers are presented more sparingly.

## Reading order note

Each Cosmere series is designed to be approachable independently. Cross-references are kept light enough that readers can begin almost anywhere. This catalog treats each surface magic system as its own entry, with Investiture as the implicit shared parent.
