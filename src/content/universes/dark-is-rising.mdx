## Setting

Susan Cooper's *The Dark Is Rising* sequence (1965–1977) is set across five novels: *Over Sea, Under Stone*, *The Dark Is Rising*, *Greenwitch*, *The Grey King*, and *Silver on the Tree*. The primary setting is contemporary Britain — the Cornish coast, the Buckinghamshire countryside around Huntercombe, and the Welsh hills of Cader Idris — but the landscape is layered with mythic depth. Beneath the familiar modern world lies an ancient conflict between the **Light** and the **Dark**, two cosmic forces that have struggled across all of human history.

The Light is served by the **Old Ones**, immortal beings who exist outside normal time and who have been born at intervals across the centuries. Merriman Lyon (Merlin) is the oldest; Will Stanton, an eleven-year-old boy who discovers his nature on his birthday, is the last. The Dark is served by agents including the **Black Rider** and the **Grey King**. Between them stands **Wild Magic**, a third force aligned with neither side — embodied by the sea, by Herne the Hunter, and by the Greenwitch, a ritual figure cast into the sea by the women of Trewissick. The sequence's arc follows the quest to gather six great **Signs** of the Light, recover the **grail** and the **harp**, and forge the **crystal sword** before the Dark rises for its final assault.
