## Setting

The Dune universe spans thousands of years of future history across a feudal galactic empire. After the Butlerian Jihad — a crusade against thinking machines — humanity replaced computers with specialized human institutions: Mentats (human calculators), the Bene Gesserit Sisterhood (political and genetic manipulators), and the Spacing Guild (navigators who fold space). All of these depend, directly or indirectly, on the spice melange, a geriatric drug found only on the desert planet Arrakis. Control of Arrakis — and thus the spice — is the central axis around which the series' politics, religion, and ecology revolve.

## Themes

Herbert's primary concern is the danger of charismatic leadership and humanity's tendency to surrender agency to messianic figures. The quasi-magical abilities in the series are framed as extreme human potential rather than supernatural gifts, and the consequences of their use are always political and ecological as much as personal.
