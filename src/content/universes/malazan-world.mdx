## Setting

*Malazan Book of the Fallen* (ten novels, 1999–2011) and its companion series by Steven Erikson and Ian Cameron Esslemont are set across a world conventionally called **Wu** by the fandom, encompassing multiple continents (Seven Cities, Genabackis, Lether, Korelri, and others) and spanning hundreds of thousands of years of history involving mortal civilizations, Elder races, and ascended beings.

The cosmology is anchored by the **Warrens** — parallel dimensions that serve as both the source and the conduit of mortal sorcery. Each Warren is associated with a particular magical aspect (fire, shadow, illusion, death, healing, etc.), and sorcerers access one or more Warrens by talent, training, or investiture. Before the Warrens, the world ran on **Holds** — an older, more primal system tied to positions of cosmic influence (King, Queen, Throne, etc.) rather than elemental aspects. Both systems coexist in the present timeline, and many plot threads involve the friction between them.

Power in Wu is stratified: mortal mages wield Warren-channeled sorcery; High Mages can hold open entire Warrens; **Ascendants** are beings who have transcended mortality and anchored themselves to positions of influence within the cosmological framework; and **Gods** sit atop the hierarchy — though they can be killed, unmade, or replaced. The Deck of Dragons, a divinatory card system, maps the current state of the cosmic power structure and is one of the world's most important navigational tools.
