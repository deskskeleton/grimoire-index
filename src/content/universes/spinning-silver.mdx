## Setting

Naomi Novik's *Spinning Silver* (2018) is set in a version of medieval Lithuania, where the mortal world borders the realm of the **Staryk**, a race of powerful, inhuman ice-fae who emerge during winter to raid human roads and hoard silver. The story follows three women — Miryem, a Jewish moneylender's daughter; Wanda, a peasant indentured to Miryem's family; and Irina, a duke's daughter married to the tsar — whose fates interlock around the collision between Staryk winter magic and the fire-demon **Chernobog**, an entity bound to the tsar's bloodline. The novel is structured as a fairy tale with the internal logic of debt, obligation, and exchange: every gift demands repayment, every bargain has binding force, and the boundary between metaphor and literal magic is deliberately thin. Miryem's talent for turning silver into gold begins as a figure of speech for her financial skill but becomes a genuine supernatural act when the Staryk king takes notice.
