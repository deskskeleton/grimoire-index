## Setting

The Buffyverse presents a world where the supernatural exists beneath a thin veneer of modern American normalcy. Sunnydale sits atop a Hellmouth, a mystical convergence point that attracts demonic activity, while Los Angeles harbors its own ecosystem of demons, law firms serving infernal clients, and interdimensional portals. Magic in this world is eclectic and syncretic, drawing from virtually every real-world occult tradition — Wiccan practice, chaos magic, Romani curses, technopaganism, and countless demon-derived rituals coexist without any single unified theory. The Watchers' Council maintains archives of magical knowledge stretching back millennia, while independent practitioners like Willow Rosenberg demonstrate that raw talent combined with obsessive study can produce power rivaling ancient sorcerers. The series treats magic with characteristic tonal range — it can be a source of wonder, addiction, corruption, feminist empowerment, or dark comedy, sometimes all within the same episode.
