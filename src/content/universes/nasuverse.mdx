The **Nasuverse** is the shared setting created by Kinoko Nasu and developed through Type-Moon's visual novels, anime adaptations, and mobile games. Its foundational works include the *Fate/stay night* visual novel (2004), *Tsukihime* (2000), and *Kara no Kyoukai* (1998), with the *Fate/Grand Order* mobile game (2015) massively expanding the setting's scope and audience.

The Nasuverse's magical metaphysics are unusually systematic for a franchise that spans so many media. At its core is the distinction between **Magecraft** (reproducible supernatural techniques that imitate natural phenomena) and **True Magic** (five miracles that accomplish what is genuinely impossible). The world itself has a will — **Gaia** — that actively suppresses the supernatural, meaning that all magical practice is a struggle against the planet's corrective force. This creates a setting where magic is real but embattled, powerful but constrained, and where the greatest practitioners are those who have found ways to work within or circumvent the World's resistance.

The franchise's enormous cast of heroic spirits (historical and mythological figures summoned as Servants in the Holy Grail War) provides a vehicle for exploring how different magical traditions, combat philosophies, and cultural mythologies interact within a unified mechanical framework.
