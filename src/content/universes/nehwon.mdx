## Setting

Nehwon is a world shaped more by atmosphere than cartography. Its dominant feature is Lankhmar, the "City of the Black Toga," a vast, ancient, corrupt metropolis that serves as the hub for most of the stories. Beyond Lankhmar lie the Cold Waste to the north, the Eastern Lands, the Mingol Steppes, and the Outer Sea. The world is populated by feuding city-states, nomadic peoples, and isolated pockets of civilization separated by dangerous wilderness. Magic in Nehwon is pervasive but unreliable, practiced by figures who range from genuinely powerful to laughably incompetent. The overall tone blends genuine menace with sardonic humor and literary self-awareness, distinguishing Leiber's work from the more earnest heroic fantasy of his contemporaries.
