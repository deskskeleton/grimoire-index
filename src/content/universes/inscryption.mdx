## Setting

Inscryption exists across multiple nested layers of reality. The surface layer is Leshy's Cabin, a dimly lit woodland refuge where a player sits across from an enigmatic Game Master whose glowing eyes peer from the darkness. The card game played here uses creature cards bearing attack and health values, with sigils granting special abilities. But the cabin is not merely a setting — it is a prison, and the cards are not merely game pieces but vessels containing trapped consciencies. The world shifts dramatically across the game's three acts, moving from Leshy's naturalistic horror through Grimora's pixel-art necropolis and Magnificus's painterly wizard tower before arriving at a corrupted digital archive where the boundaries between game, player, and reality dissolve entirely. The OLD_DATA hidden within the game's code represents forbidden knowledge so dangerous that characters sacrifice themselves to ensure its deletion, positioning Inscryption as a world where the game itself is the magical artifact and playing it is the supernatural act.
