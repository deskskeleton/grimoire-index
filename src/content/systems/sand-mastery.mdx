## Mechanics

Sand Mastery is the magic system of the Dayside of **Taldain**, a tidally locked world in the Cosmere where one hemisphere is permanently sunlit and the other permanently dark. The system's medium is **white sand** — sand infused with a lichen-like microorganism that absorbs and stores Investiture from Taldain's sun. A **sand master** mentally commands this charged white sand to move, form shapes, strike targets, and construct barriers.

The sand responds to the master's will with considerable precision. Skilled practitioners can form the sand into tendrils, walls, blades, and complex three-dimensional constructions. The sand can be launched as projectiles, used defensively as shields, or shaped into tools. The key constraint is that each use **drains the sand's charge**: white sand turns black as its stored Investiture is consumed, and black sand is inert — it will not respond to commands until it is recharged by exposure to sunlight.

The second resource consumed is **the master's own hydration**. Using Sand Mastery dehydrates the practitioner rapidly. Extended or intensive use can produce dangerous dehydration within minutes. Masters carry water and must manage their fluid intake carefully during combat.

A sand master's power is measured by the number of **ribbons** (streams of sand) they can control simultaneously. A weak master controls one or two ribbons; an elite master might control a dozen or more. Ribbon count is the primary metric of ability and social rank among the sand masters.

## Limitations and costs

The dual-drain mechanic is the system's defining limitation. Every action costs both sand charge and bodily water. A master who runs out of charged sand is powerless; a master who dehydrates too far collapses or dies. Combat is a resource-management problem where the practitioner must balance aggression against conservation.

White sand must be **recharged by sunlight**. On the Dayside this is straightforward (the sun never sets), but sand carried to the Darkside or off-world loses the ability to recharge. This is a hard geographic constraint that limits Sand Mastery's portability.

Sand Mastery requires **innate ability** — not everyone can do it. The talent appears to be at least partly genetic, concentrated in certain Daysider families. Even among those with talent, the range of ability (measured in ribbon count) varies dramatically.

## Practitioner access

Sand masters are a recognized professional class on Taldain's Dayside, organized into a guild structure called the **Diem**. Membership is based on demonstrated ability. The Diem is politically significant — sand masters are the primary military and civic-engineering asset of the Daysider civilization — but its influence is contested throughout *White Sand* by factions that regard sand masters as politically unreliable or militarily insufficient.

Talent is required; training then refines it. A person without innate Sand Mastery ability cannot learn the discipline regardless of effort.

## Cultural role

On Taldain's Dayside, Sand Mastery is openly known and deeply embedded in the culture. Sand masters serve as soldiers, construction workers, and civic leaders. The Diem is one of the most powerful institutions in Daysider society — and the political struggle over its independence, funding, and military role is the central conflict of *White Sand*. The system's visibility and institutional integration contrast with many other Cosmere systems, where magic is rarer or more individual.
