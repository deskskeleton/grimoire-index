## Mechanics

**Sygaldry** is the discipline of inscribing runes onto objects to create permanent magical devices. It is taught as a formal subject at the University under Master **Kilvin** in the Artificery workshop. Where Sympathy requires a practitioner to actively maintain a mental link between two objects and channel energy through their Alar (mental concentration), Sygaldry inscribes those same principles permanently into the object through rune-work.

The basic principle mirrors Sympathy: sygaldric runes establish **bindings** between concepts (heat, motion, light, force) and the object. A **sympathy lamp**, for example, uses sygaldric runes to convert ambient heat into light — it draws warmth from the surrounding air and emits visible light, requiring no fuel or active magical maintenance. This is the University's most commercially successful product.

Other sygaldric devices include: **heat-eaters** (runes that absorb heat from an area), **bloodless** devices (Kvothe's arrowcatch, which uses sygaldric principles to deflect incoming projectiles), and various warding and locking mechanisms. The discipline also produces **gram** — a protective device that absorbs hostile sympathy directed at the wearer.

Sygaldric runes are a formal script with specific meanings and combination rules. The artistry lies in combining runes to produce desired effects — some combinations are standard (taught in coursework), while novel combinations represent genuine invention. Kilvin's workshop operates as both a teaching space and a production facility, with advanced students producing devices for sale.

The relationship between Sygaldry and Sympathy is analogous to the relationship between circuit design and live electrical work — Sygaldry builds the hardware that implements Sympathetic principles in durable form.

## Limitations and costs

The primary limitation is **energy sourcing**. Sygaldric devices must draw energy from somewhere — they cannot create energy from nothing (Sympathetic law of conservation applies). A sympathy lamp draws heat from the environment; a protective gram absorbs hostile magical energy. If the energy source is insufficient, the device weakens or fails.

The second cost is **craftsmanship requirements**. Sygaldric runes must be inscribed with precision — errors in the rune-work produce devices that malfunction or fail dangerously. The discipline requires both theoretical knowledge (understanding rune combinations) and practical skill (metalworking, engraving, materials science).

The third limitation is **fixed function**. Once inscribed, a sygaldric device does what its runes specify and nothing else. It cannot be adjusted without re-inscription. This makes sygaldric devices reliable but inflexible compared to active Sympathy.

## Practitioner access

Sygaldry is taught at the University as a formal discipline. Access requires University enrollment and sufficient academic standing to enter the Artificery. Master Kilvin evaluates students' competence through practical examination. Outside the University, sygaldric knowledge is rare — the University maintains a near-monopoly on magical device production.

## Cultural role

Sygaldry is the University's primary source of income and cultural influence. Sympathy lamps are sold throughout the Four Corners, and their reliability and utility demonstrate the practical value of University training. The Artificery is the most commercially productive department, and its output supports the institution financially. In the broader world, sygaldric devices are valued consumer goods — expensive but superior to mundane alternatives.
