## Mechanics

**Foretelling** is a Talent — a specialized, innate magical ability — that manifests in a very small number of channelers (users of the One Power). When a Foretelling occurs, the channeler enters an involuntary trance state and speaks aloud a prophecy: a true statement about future events. The prophecy is typically delivered in cryptic, symbolic, or metaphorical language, and the channeler has no conscious control over what is said.

The defining feature of Foretelling is its **infallibility**. A true Foretelling always comes to pass. It cannot be wrong, and attempts to prevent a Foretelling from being fulfilled are themselves part of the Pattern's design. This is distinct from ordinary prediction, divination through the One Power, or political prognostication — all of which can be wrong. Foretelling speaks with the authority of the Pattern itself.

However, Foretellings are **uncontrolled and uninterpretable at the moment of delivery**. The channeler does not choose when to Foretell, cannot summon the ability at will, and often has only fragmentary memory of what they said. Interpretation is left to those present — and the history of the series is littered with Foretellings that were misinterpreted, manipulated, or deliberately suppressed by those who heard them.

The most significant Foretellings in the series include Gitara Moroso's prophecy of the Dragon Reborn's birth on the slopes of Dragonmount (which triggered Moiraine and Siuan's decades-long search) and Elaida's Foretellings about the royal house of Andor.

## Limitations and costs

The primary limitation is **total lack of control**. A channeler with the Foretelling Talent cannot invoke it, aim it at a specific question, or even guarantee that it will ever activate. Some channelers with the Talent go their entire lives with only one or two Foretellings; others never have one at all despite possessing the latent ability. This makes Foretelling useless as a tool and valuable only retroactively.

The second limitation is **interpretive opacity**. Foretellings are delivered in symbolic language that resists straightforward reading. The same Foretelling can support multiple contradictory interpretations, and the correct one is typically obvious only after the prophesied events have already occurred. This creates enormous political leverage for anyone who controls the record of what was said — and the Aes Sedai have spent centuries arguing about the meaning of key Foretellings.

A minor cost is **physical and emotional disruption**. The trance state is involuntary and can strike at any moment — during conversation, during ritual, during combat. The experience is described as disorienting, and the channeler is briefly helpless while speaking.

## Practitioner access

Foretelling is an innate Talent that manifests only in channelers — users of the One Power. It cannot be learned, and possessing the Talent does not guarantee that Foretellings will occur. The Talent is very rare; across the series' several-thousand-year history, only a handful of significant Foretellings are recorded.

## Cultural role

Within Aes Sedai culture, Foretellings are treated with extreme seriousness. They are recorded, archived, debated, and sometimes suppressed. The political weight of a Foretelling is enormous — a confirmed Foretelling can redirect Aes Sedai policy for decades or centuries. Outside the White Tower, Foretellings are conflated with the broader Randland tradition of prophecy (the Prophecies of the Dragon, the Karaethon Cycle), which includes prophecies from multiple sources. The general population regards prophecy as part of the world's cosmological furniture — real, important, but not a tool that ordinary people interact with directly.
