## Mechanics

Cursed energy is a tangible substance produced by negative human emotion — fear, hatred, anxiety, grief. Most people produce small amounts unconsciously, which leaks away harmlessly. Sorcerers are people whose biology lets them gather, control, and expend cursed energy deliberately. Concentrated cursed energy from human suffering also condenses into autonomous cursed spirits, which are the sorcerers' principal antagonists.

Each sorcerer manifests a single signature Cursed Technique — an inherited or innate magical ability that defines what they can do with their cursed energy. Examples include Satoru Gojo's Limitless (manipulation of space), Megumi Fushiguro's Ten Shadows Technique (summoning shikigami from his shadow), and Yuta Okkotsu's copy-and-mimicry capability. Cursed Techniques have hard rules: they do specific things in specific ways, with specific failure conditions. Mastering one's Technique is the work of a sorcerer's career.

Beyond the signature Technique, sorcerers have access to several universal techniques: Reverse Cursed Technique (using positive cursed energy to heal — difficult, energy-expensive), Cursed Energy Reinforcement (channeling energy into the body for offense and defense), Black Flash (a momentary perfect collision of physical strike and cursed energy, multiplying damage 2.5x), and Simple Domain (a defensive bubble that nullifies a Domain Expansion's guaranteed hit).

The system's signature high-tier mechanic is the Domain Expansion. A sorcerer who has mastered their Technique enough can expand their inner world — a pocket reality whose interior is shaped by their Technique — and trap opponents inside. Within an active Domain, the caster's Technique hits automatically, no longer requiring aim or precision; this is enormously dangerous and enormously expensive. Domains can also be incomplete (forgoing the auto-hit guarantee for cheaper deployment) and can clash, with the more refined Domain overwriting the weaker one.

A second specialized mechanism is the Binding Vow — a self-imposed restriction that, in exchange for sincerely accepted limitation, grants disproportionate strengthening. Vows can be made with oneself ("I will never use this Technique on a person younger than ten") or between two parties ("if you let me cast this, I will give you the following information"). Violations break the vow and cost dearly. The mechanic is presented as universal and rule-bound: the world enforces vows mechanically.

## Limitations and costs

Cursed energy is finite. A sorcerer running low casts weaker techniques, with smaller Domains, and reduced Reinforcement. Technique repetition burns through reserves quickly; a long fight is a stamina contest as much as a combat one.

Reverse Cursed Technique — the healing inversion — is technically available to any sorcerer but is hard to learn and energy-expensive. Most sorcerers cannot heal effectively in combat.

Cursed Techniques are typically a single fixed ability per sorcerer. There is no spell-list to choose from; the sorcerer's identity is bonded to one Technique with predictable properties. Technique-extension (adding new uses, refining mechanics) is possible but slow.

The Domain Expansion's auto-hit guarantee comes with significant cost — the caster is fully committed for the duration, vulnerable to interruption, and pays for it in cursed energy. A failed Domain (one that can be overwritten by a stronger opposing Domain) is worse than no Domain at all.

The masquerade is partial: most ordinary people in the setting do not know cursed spirits or sorcerers exist, and the jujutsu world goes to significant lengths to maintain that ignorance. Working sorcerers are formally licensed and graded (Grade 4 to Special Grade) and bound by the institutional norms of the three major schools.

## Practitioner access

Cursed-energy sensitivity is partly hereditary. The three great clans — Gojo, Zen'in, Kamo — concentrate certain Techniques in their bloodlines, and inherited Techniques are common. Outside the clans, sorcerers manifest unpredictably; some are born with strong potential, others develop sensitivity through trauma or environmental exposure. Formal training takes place at three Jujutsu High Schools (Tokyo, Kyoto, plus Tokyo's overseas sister institution).

## Cultural role

Jujutsu society operates as a hidden masquerade beneath modern Japan. Cursed spirits are real and frequently dangerous, but the general public believes hauntings to be psychiatric or supernatural superstition. The sorcerer caste is small, conservative, and politically fractious — the three great clans, the Higher-Ups, and the schools all maintain rivalries and feud over policy. The series' central plot turns on these political fractures more than on any single antagonist.
