## Mechanics

**Laran** is the Darkovan term for the full suite of psychic abilities carried in the blood of the Comyn families. At its most basic level, laran manifests as telepathy — the ability to sense and communicate with other minds — and empathy, the involuntary reception of emotions from those nearby. Most Comyn possess at least rudimentary telepathic sensitivity, but the specific form and strength of laran varies by family bloodline. The Alton gift is forced rapport, the ability to compel telepathic contact with any mind regardless of shields. The Aldaran gift is precognition. The Ridenow gift is empathy of unusual depth and range. The Ardais gift is a catalytic telepathy that can awaken laran in others. Other gifts include telekinesis and the rare, devastating ability of pyrokinesis — starting and controlling fire with the mind.

Raw laran is amplified and focused through **matrix crystals**, also called starstones or keyed matrices. A starstone is a crystalline structure attuned to a single user's psychic resonance; once keyed, it responds only to that person's mind and touching another's keyed matrix without invitation can cause agonizing psychic shock or death. Simple matrices are small gems, but larger and more complex constructions — lattice matrices, screens, relay nets — allow groups of telepaths to combine their power for large-scale workings.

The institutional framework for matrix work is the **Tower**. Each Tower (Arilinn, Neskaya, Dalereuth, Hali, and others) maintains a trained circle of telepaths who work together under a Keeper — a specially trained telepath who coordinates and channels the circle's combined laran. Tower circles can perform feats far beyond any individual: mining ore from deep underground, powering aircraft, constructing communication relays, controlling weather, and — in the Ages of Chaos — building weapons of mass destruction including clingfire (a napalm-like substance created through matrix manipulation) and bonewater dust.

The linked-circle technique requires precise coordination. Each member of the circle holds a specific role: the Keeper channels and directs, monitors watch the circle members' physical health, mechanics handle the technical matrix work, and telepaths provide raw power. The circle functions as a single psychic unit, and a break in concentration or a member's distress can cascade through the entire group.

## Limitations and costs

The most immediate cost of laran is **threshold sickness** — a dangerous psychic and physical crisis that strikes adolescents as their laran awakens. Symptoms include fever, disorientation, involuntary telepathic contact, uncontrolled manifestation of gifts, and in severe cases death. Without intervention from a trained telepath, threshold sickness kills a significant proportion of those who develop strong laran.

Sustained matrix work imposes severe **physical toll**. Circle work lasting hours or days leaves practitioners exhausted, and Keepers in particular burn through physical reserves at a dangerous rate. The tradition of celibacy for Keepers (challenged and partially overturned during the series) reflects the belief that sexual and emotional entanglements destabilize the precise psychic control required to channel a circle. Overreach — drawing more power through a matrix than the user can control — causes psychic burnout, permanent brain damage, or death.

The **social costs** are equally severe. Comyn politics revolve around laran bloodlines: forced marriages to concentrate gifts, political manoeuvring over Tower placements, and the tension between those who view laran as sacred trust and those who treat it as political weapon. The Towers themselves impose monastic discipline on their members, and leaving a Tower after training carries its own social stigma. The Compact — Darkover's ban on distance weapons — exists precisely because the Ages of Chaos demonstrated what happens when matrix technology is applied to warfare without restraint.

## Practitioner access

Laran is strictly hereditary, carried in the genetics of the Comyn families and their offshoots. The trait descends through both parents, and the great families historically practised deliberate intermarriage to concentrate specific gifts — a programme that also produced genetic instability, madness, and the occasional catastrophically overpowered individual. Non-Comyn Darkovans occasionally manifest trace laran, usually attributed to unacknowledged Comyn ancestry. The Terrans who rediscover Darkover are initially assumed to lack laran entirely, though this proves not to be universally true.

Training takes place in the Towers, which accept adolescents showing strong laran (often during or shortly after threshold sickness) and subject them to years of disciplined instruction in telepathic shielding, matrix handling, circle work, and the ethics of psychic contact. A subset of practitioners — hedge-telepaths, leroni outside the Tower system — exist but are regarded with suspicion by the formal establishment.

## Cultural role

Laran is openly known on Darkover and forms the basis of the entire social hierarchy. The Comyn rule because they possess laran; the Towers provide essential infrastructure (communication, mining, energy) through matrix work; and the Compact's prohibition on distance weapons makes laran the decisive factor in political and military conflict. The arrival of the Terran Empire forces Darkover to confront whether laran-based feudalism can coexist with technological civilization — a question that drives the later novels.

The cultural attitude toward laran is reverent but ambivalent. It is simultaneously a gift, a burden, a political tool, and a source of the worst atrocities in Darkovan history. The Ages of Chaos, when matrix-armed Towers fought wars of annihilation, remain the defining cultural trauma, and the Compact represents the collective decision that some applications of laran are too dangerous to permit regardless of their utility.
