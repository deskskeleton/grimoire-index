## Mechanics

In **Mage: The Awakening**, magic operates through the **Arcana** — ten fundamental domains of reality that a mage accesses after their Awakening. Awakening occurs when a mortal's soul travels to one of five **Supernal Realms** (higher realities beyond the Abyss) and inscribes their name on a **Watchtower**, establishing a permanent conduit to Supernal truth. The Watchtower determines the mage's **Path** — their primary magical affinity.

The five Paths and their ruling/inferior Arcana:

- **Acanthus** (Watchtower of the Lunargent Thorn) — Fate and Time; inferior: Forces
- **Mastigos** (Watchtower of the Iron Gauntlet) — Mind and Space; inferior: Matter
- **Moros** (Watchtower of the Lead Coin) — Death and Matter; inferior: Spirit
- **Obrimos** (Watchtower of the Golden Key) — Forces and Prime; inferior: Death
- **Thyrsus** (Watchtower of the Stone Book) — Life and Spirit; inferior: Mind

The ten Arcana are: **Death** (ghosts, entropy, soul manipulation), **Fate** (probability, oaths, blessings/curses), **Forces** (energy, elements, physics), **Life** (biology, healing, shapeshifting), **Matter** (inanimate material, transmutation), **Mind** (thought, emotion, psychic phenomena), **Prime** (magical energy, Mana, enchantment), **Space** (distance, sympathy, teleportation), **Spirit** (the Shadow realm, ephemeral beings), and **Time** (temporal perception, prophecy, time manipulation).

Each Arcanum is rated 1–5 dots. Spells are defined by their Arcanum level, Practice (the type of magical action: Knowing, Unveiling, Ruling, Shielding, Veiling, Weaving, Fraying, Perfecting, Unraveling, Patterning, Making, Unmaking), and various reach/scale parameters. The 2nd edition streamlines this into a flexible spellcasting system where players define effects by combining Arcana dots with Practices.

**Paradox** in Awakening is reframed as **the Abyss leaking in**. When a mage overreaches — casting improvised spells beyond their comfortable mastery, or performing magic in front of Sleepers (unAwakened mortals) — the Abyss intrudes, producing Paradox. Paradox can manifest as Abyssal entities, magical corruption, or anomalies. The Lie — the consensus reality produced by the Exarchs (ascended mages who rule the Supernal from beyond the Abyss) — actively suppresses magical awareness in Sleepers.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **Mana** — magical energy derived from Supernal truth, harvested from Hallows (sites where the Supernal leaks through the Abyss) or from souls. Mana fuels spellcasting beyond basic capabilities, powers enchantments, and mitigates Paradox. Mana is finite and replenishing it requires access to sacred sites or morally costly soul-harvesting.

The second cost is **Paradox and the Abyss**. Overreaching or casting vulgar magic in front of Sleepers invites Abyssal intrusion. The Abyss is not merely a punitive mechanic but an active antagonistic force — Abyssal entities seek to corrupt reality, and every Paradox event is an opportunity for the Abyss to gain purchase.

The third limitation is **Path specialization**. A mage's Path determines which two Arcana are Ruling (cheapest to advance) and which is Inferior (most expensive). While any mage can learn any Arcanum, the Path creates structural specialization — an Obrimos will always be better at Forces and Prime than at Death.

The fourth cost is **the Wisdom scale**. Awakening uses a morality mechanic (Wisdom) that degrades when mages commit Acts of Hubris — using magic selfishly, harming others with power, or treating lesser beings as tools. Low Wisdom makes a mage more vulnerable to the Abyss and less capable of certain magical feats.

## Practitioner access

Awakening is spontaneous and cannot be reliably triggered. A mortal's soul breaks through the Abyss and reaches a Supernal Watchtower during a moment of crisis, insight, or transformation. The five Atlantean Orders (Silver Ladder, Guardians of the Veil, Mysterium, Adamantine Arrow, Free Council) seek out newly Awakened mages and provide training, mentorship, and political structure. Sleepers — the vast majority of humanity — cannot use magic and actively suppress it through their disbelief (the Quiescence).

## Cultural role

Mage society is organized into **Orders** that disagree about magic's purpose: the Silver Ladder seeks to rule and uplift humanity, the Guardians of the Veil protect magical secrets, the Mysterium preserves occult knowledge, the Adamantine Arrow defends Awakened society through force, and the Free Council integrates magic with modern culture. Opposed to these are the **Seers of the Throne** — mages who serve the Exarchs and enforce the Lie. The political landscape is a cold war between Orders, Seers, and independent mages, with the Abyss as an ever-present existential threat.
