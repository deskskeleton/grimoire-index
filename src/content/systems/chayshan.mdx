## Mechanics

ChayShan is the magic system of the **JinDo** people on the Cosmere world of Sel. It is a martial discipline in which the practitioner performs a specific flowing exercise — a sequence of movements resembling tai chi or similar internal martial arts — to channel the **Dor** through their body. While performing the ChayShan forms, the practitioner gains dramatically enhanced speed, strength, reflexes, and physical awareness.

The system is movement-dependent: the Dor flows through the practitioner only while they are actively performing the forms. The movements must be continuous and correctly executed; interrupting the form cuts off the power. This gives ChayShan a distinctive combat profile — the practitioner is in constant, fluid motion, and their enhanced state lasts exactly as long as they keep moving.

ChayShan is the least documented of Sel's four known magic systems. It appears in *Elantris* primarily through **Shuden**, a JinDo nobleman who uses it in a pivotal combat scene. The forms are described as beautiful, precise, and athletic — a marked contrast to Dakhor's brutal body-horror and AonDor's cerebral glyph-tracing.

## Limitations and costs

ChayShan's primary limitation is **sustained physical effort**. The forms must be performed continuously, which means the practitioner is limited by their own stamina and physical conditioning. Unlike AonDor (which can produce persistent enchantments) or Dakhor (which is always-on once the bone-reshaping is complete), ChayShan's enhancement is strictly temporary and requires active maintenance through movement.

The second limitation is **scope**. ChayShan appears to be limited to physical enhancement — it does not produce the ranged effects, healing, or transformative capabilities of AonDor or Forgery. It makes the practitioner faster and stronger, but it does not reshape matter or rewrite history.

Like all Selish magic, ChayShan is **geography-dependent** and presumably weakens with distance from JinDo.

The system also requires formal training in the specific movement patterns. Improvised or incorrect forms do not channel the Dor.

## Practitioner access

ChayShan is a learned discipline taught within JinDo culture. There is no indication that it requires a special selection event like AonDor's Shaod or Dakhor's sacrificial rituals — any JinDo person with the physical aptitude and training can presumably learn it. However, its practice appears to be culturally specific to JinDo, and it is not widely known or practiced outside that region.

## Cultural role

ChayShan is presented in *Elantris* as a respected cultural tradition of the JinDo people — a martial art with deep roots in their society. Its appearance in the novel is limited, and its broader cultural significance within JinDo is largely implied rather than explored. Shuden's use of it in Kae (the Arelene capital) marks him as distinctively JinDo and serves as one of the novel's demonstrations that Sel's magic is not limited to the Elantrian tradition but is a continent-wide phenomenon with regional variation.
