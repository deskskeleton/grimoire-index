## Mechanics

**Truenaming** is based on the premise that everything in existence — creatures, objects, places, even abstract concepts — has a **truename**: a word in the primal language of creation (the **Truespeech**). By learning a thing's truename and speaking it with precision and intent, a truenamer can alter that thing's fundamental nature.

The truenamer accesses three **Lexicons** (categories of utterances):

**The Lexicon of the Evolving Mind** — utterances that affect creatures, modifying their physical abilities, mental states, or magical properties. **The Lexicon of the Crafted Tool** — utterances that affect objects, changing their material properties, enchanting them, or destroying them. **The Lexicon of the Perfected Map** — utterances that affect places and areas, altering terrain, creating environmental effects, or manipulating spatial properties.

Each utterance has two modes: a **normal** effect and a **reverse** effect. An utterance that enhances a creature's strength in its normal mode might weaken a creature's strength in its reverse mode. This duality gives the truenamer versatility but requires choosing which mode to apply.

The central mechanic is the **Truespeak check** — a skill check made each time the truenamer speaks an utterance. The DC is determined by the target's Hit Dice (for creatures) or caster level (for objects and places), plus modifiers for repeated use. Each time a specific utterance is used against the same target, the DC increases by 2, representing the difficulty of redefining something that has already been linguistically altered.

## Limitations and costs

The primary limitation is **scaling difficulty**. The DC of Truespeak checks increases with target HD, but the truenamer's Truespeak skill bonus does not keep pace — creating a mathematical gap that makes the class progressively weaker at higher levels. This is widely considered a design flaw rather than an intentional limitation.

The second cost is **the Law of Sequence**. The escalating DC for repeated utterances against the same target prevents the truenamer from stacking the same effect. This forces variety but also limits the ability to specialize.

The third limitation is **verbal dependency**. Truenaming requires speech — silence effects, gags, or conditions that prevent vocalization shut the truenamer down completely. The utterances must be spoken clearly and precisely; mumbling or rushing produces failure.

## Practitioner access

Truenaming requires extensive study of the Truespeech — a language that is not naturally spoken by any mortal race. Truenamers are scholars who have dedicated their lives to learning fragments of the universe's foundational language. The discipline has no institutional support in most D&D settings — truenamers are independent researchers working from fragmentary texts.

## Cultural role

Truenaming occupies a niche position in D&D's magical ecology. It is neither arcane nor divine in the traditional sense — it predates both, drawing on the language that was used to speak the universe into existence. Most characters in the game world have never encountered a truenamer or heard of truenaming as a practice. The discipline exists primarily in academic and philosophical circles, where the concept of a universal language of creation has scholarly appeal even if practical application is rare.
