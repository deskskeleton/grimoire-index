## Mechanics

A **Reality Marble** (固有結界, *Koyū Kekkai*) is a bounded field that overwrites external reality with the user's internal mental landscape. Where ordinary Bounded Fields create barriers or detection zones within the existing world, a Reality Marble replaces the world entirely within its area of effect — the sky, the terrain, the physical laws, and the ambient conditions all conform to the user's inner reality.

Each Reality Marble is unique, reflecting the fundamental nature of its user. **Unlimited Blade Works** (Emiya Shirou / Archer) manifests as an infinite wasteland of swords — every weapon Shirou has ever seen is recorded and reproduced here, because his identity is fundamentally defined by the act of tracing and wielding blades. **Ionioi Hetairoi** (Iskandar) manifests as a desert plain populated by the tens of thousands of soldiers who served in his army — because his identity is defined by the loyalty he inspired and the conquests they shared.

The activation of a Reality Marble requires an invocation (typically a spoken aria or chant) and an enormous expenditure of magical energy. Once deployed, the Reality Marble overwrites a sphere of the real world, trapping everyone within it in the user's personal domain. Inside the marble, the user has significant advantages — the environment conforms to their will, their unique abilities are enhanced, and they effectively control the battlefield.

The critical limitation is **Gaia's corrective force**. The World recognizes a Reality Marble as an aberration — a violation of natural law — and actively works to erase it. This means every Reality Marble is temporary: the user must continuously pour magical energy into maintaining it against the World's resistance, and eventually the marble collapses as the user's reserves are exhausted. Most Reality Marbles can be maintained for only minutes.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **enormous mana expenditure**. Deploying and maintaining a Reality Marble against Gaia's correction requires more magical energy than most Magi possess. Only Servants (heroic spirits with effectively superhuman energy reserves), exceptional Magi, or beings with innate Reality Marble abilities (like certain Dead Apostles) can maintain one. For a human Magus, deploying a Reality Marble is typically a last resort that will leave them completely drained.

The second cost is **temporal fragility**. Gaia's correction is relentless — the longer a Reality Marble is maintained, the more energy it costs, until collapse is inevitable. This makes Reality Marbles decisive but brief: they are suited for climactic confrontations, not sustained advantage.

The third cost is **uniqueness and inflexibility**. A Reality Marble reflects the user's fixed inner nature — it cannot be redesigned or adapted. Unlimited Blade Works is always a field of swords; Ionioi Hetairoi is always an army. If the Reality Marble's nature doesn't suit the current situation, the user cannot adjust it.

## Practitioner access

Reality Marbles are extraordinarily rare. They require either an innate ability (certain non-human entities like Dead Apostles possess them naturally) or a human psyche so thoroughly defined by a single concept that the internal world achieves sufficient coherence to overwrite reality. Among human Magi, possessing a Reality Marble is considered a miracle-tier achievement. Among Servants, Reality Marbles typically manifest as Noble Phantasms — the crystallized legends of heroic spirits.

## Cultural role

Within the Mage's Association, Reality Marbles are classified as a form of sorcery that approaches True Magic — they represent the closest Magecraft can come to the genuinely impossible (creating a world that shouldn't exist). In the narrative, Reality Marbles serve as the ultimate expression of character: each one is a physical manifestation of who the user truly is, making their deployment both a combat climax and a moment of complete self-revelation.
