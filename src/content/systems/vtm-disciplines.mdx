## Mechanics

**Disciplines** are the supernatural powers of vampires in the World of Darkness. Each Discipline is a named category of abilities — rated from one to five dots (or higher for ancient vampires) — that a vampire can develop over their unlife. Common Disciplines include:

**Dominate** — mental commands and memory alteration imposed through eye contact and spoken word. **Presence** — supernatural emotional manipulation, from awe to terror. **Auspex** — heightened senses, aura reading, and psychic perception. **Celerity** — supernatural speed. **Fortitude** — supernatural resilience. **Potence** — supernatural strength. **Protean** — shapeshifting into animals, earth-melding, growing claws. **Obfuscate** — invisibility and misdirection. **Animalism** — communication with and command of animals and the Beast within other vampires.

More specialized Disciplines include **Thaumaturgy** (blood sorcery practiced primarily by Clan Tremere, involving rituals and "paths" of blood manipulation, elemental control, and sympathetic magic) and clan-specific powers like **Vicissitude** (Tzimisce flesh-crafting) and **Dementation** (Malkavian madness projection).

The fuel for all Disciplines is **vitae** — the blood a vampire consumes from living beings. Activating many Disciplines costs blood points, creating a constant hunger pressure. A vampire's **generation** (distance from the mythological progenitor Caine) determines their maximum blood pool and the ceiling on their Discipline levels. Older, lower-generation vampires are categorically more powerful — a structural inequality baked into vampiric society.

In **Vampire: The Requiem** (the Chronicles of Darkness reimagining), the system is streamlined: Disciplines function similarly but are organized around five clans with three in-clan Disciplines each. Thaumaturgy is replaced by **Crúac** (blood sorcery) and **Theban Sorcery**. The generation mechanic is replaced by **Blood Potency**, which rises with age but can be reduced through voluntary torpor.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **blood hunger**. Every use of Disciplines depletes the vampire's blood pool, which must be replenished by feeding on living beings. This creates a constant moral pressure — the vampire must predate on humans to fuel their powers, and feeding carries the risk of killing, addicting, or blood-bonding the victim.

The second cost is **the Beast**. Vampires carry an inner predator (the Beast) that surfaces during stress, hunger, or provocation, producing **frenzy** — a loss of control in which the vampire attacks, feeds, or flees without rational thought. Many Disciplines interact with the Beast: Animalism engages it, Presence channels it, and overuse of violent powers risks triggering it.

The third cost is **social exposure**. The vampiric world operates under the **Masquerade** — a society-wide directive to conceal vampiric existence from mortals. Overt use of supernatural powers risks Masquerade breaches, which are punished severely by vampiric authorities.

The fourth cost is **generational ceiling**. A vampire's maximum power is constrained by their generation, which is fixed at the moment of embrace (creation). No amount of training can overcome this hereditary limit — a 13th-generation vampire will never match a 6th-generation elder.

## Practitioner access

All vampires have access to Disciplines. Every vampire has three **in-clan Disciplines** determined by their clan (bloodline), which are cheaper to advance. Out-of-clan Disciplines can be learned but require a teacher who possesses them, making cross-clan training a social negotiation. The most powerful Disciplines (levels 6+) are available only to vampires of sufficiently low generation — ancient beings who have walked the earth for centuries.

## Cultural role

Disciplines define the social order of vampiric society. Clans are identified and stereotyped by their signature Disciplines — the Ventrue lead through Dominate and Presence, the Nosferatu spy through Obfuscate and Animalism, the Tremere practice blood sorcery that other clans regard with suspicion. Discipline mastery is the primary currency of personal power among vampires, and the generational hierarchy ensures that the oldest vampires maintain structural dominance over younger Kindred regardless of individual talent.
