## Mechanics

**Free Magic** is the raw, untamed magical force that existed in the Old Kingdom before the Charter was created. When the seven Bright Shiners (powerful Free Magic entities) bound themselves and the world's magic into the structured lattice of Charter Marks, Free Magic was not eliminated — it was contained, pushed to the margins, and opposed. Free Magic persists in the spaces the Charter cannot reach: in Death (the river of the afterlife), in Free Magic creatures that refused or predate the binding, and in practitioners who deliberately step outside the Charter's order.

Free Magic manifests as a visceral, physical force. Its presence is marked by acrid white fire, a metallic taste, and a corrosive effect on Charter Magic — when the two come into contact, Charter Marks dissolve and the structured magic unravels. Free Magic creatures (including the Greater Dead and entities like Orannis the Destroyer) wield it instinctively, producing effects that range from animation of the dead to reality-warping destruction.

Human practitioners of Free Magic include **necromancers** — who use a combination of Free Magic and perverted Charter techniques to raise and command the Dead — and individuals like Clariel, who discover an innate affinity for Free Magic that the Charter cannot fully suppress. The Abhorsen occupies a unique position: a Charter Mage who uses Charter-sanctioned tools (the bells, the Book of the Dead) to walk in Death and oppose Free Magic entities on their own ground.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **corruption**. Sustained use of Free Magic erodes the practitioner's humanity and Charter-nature. Clariel's transformation into Chlorr of the Mask — from a reluctant Charter Mage into a centuries-old Free Magic entity — is the most fully documented example. The corruption is both physical (the body becomes less human) and psychological (the practitioner's will and identity are gradually subsumed).

The second cost is **opposition from the Charter**. Free Magic use is detectable by Charter Mages and actively opposed by the Charter's institutional defenders. The Abhorsen exists specifically to hunt and bind Free Magic practitioners and creatures. Using Free Magic openly marks the practitioner as an enemy of the established order.

The third cost is **instability**. Free Magic is inherently chaotic — its effects are powerful but difficult to control precisely. Where Charter Magic works through structured marks with predictable combinations, Free Magic responds to raw will and emotion, making it prone to catastrophic overreaction.

## Practitioner access

Free Magic is accessible through several paths: innate affinity (some individuals, like Clariel, are born with a natural connection to Free Magic that exists alongside or in tension with their Charter blood), necromantic study (learning to manipulate Death and the Dead through forbidden knowledge), or transformation (being corrupted or transformed by contact with Free Magic entities). There is no formal school of Free Magic — practitioners are isolated, self-taught, or mentored by existing Free Magic creatures. The knowledge is actively suppressed by the Charter bloodlines and the Abhorsen.

## Cultural role

In the Old Kingdom, Free Magic is understood as the fundamental threat — the chaos that the Charter holds at bay. Free Magic creatures are the monsters that the Abhorsen hunts, the Dead that necromancers raise, and the existential danger that the Great Charter Stones prevent from overwhelming the world. The distinction between Charter Magic and Free Magic structures the entire moral framework of the Old Kingdom: Charter is order, civilization, and life; Free Magic is entropy, corruption, and death. This binary is complicated by characters like Clariel and the Disreputable Dog, who demonstrate that the boundary between the two is less absolute than the Charter bloodlines claim.
