## Mechanics

Jade is a bioenergetic mineral found naturally on the island of Kekon. When worn against the skin by an individual with sufficient biological sensitivity, it enhances the body's capabilities across six trained disciplines:

**Perception** heightens the senses far beyond normal human range. A jade-wearing Green Bone can sense the heartbeat of a person in the next room, detect a thrown knife by its displacement of air, and read the emotional state of nearby individuals through subtle physiological cues. Perception is the foundational discipline — without it, the others cannot be used effectively, because the practitioner needs jade-enhanced awareness to coordinate their enhanced abilities.

**Lightness** reduces the practitioner's effective weight, allowing superhuman leaps, wall-running, controlled falls from great heights, and extraordinary agility. A skilled Lightness user can cross a city by rooftop or drop from a tall building and land unharmed.

**Strength** amplifies muscular force far beyond human limits. A Green Bone using Strength can shatter concrete with a punch, hurl objects with devastating velocity, and overpower multiple opponents in hand-to-hand combat.

**Steel** hardens the body against damage. A Green Bone channelling Steel can shrug off blows that would break bones, resist bladed weapons, and absorb impacts that would kill an unprotected person. Steel is not absolute — sustained or overwhelming force can still injure or kill — but it transforms the practitioner into something approaching bulletproof.

**Deflection** projects jade energy outward as a repulsive force, allowing the practitioner to push away projectiles, knock opponents off their feet, or create a brief kinetic barrier. Deflection can stop bullets at close range, though it requires precise timing and focused concentration.

**Channeling** is the most offensive discipline — the practitioner projects concentrated bioenergetic energy as a destructive blast, typically through the hands. A Channeling strike can kill at a distance of several metres, and a powerful Green Bone can use it to shatter barriers or destroy vehicles.

All six disciplines draw on the same jade-mediated bioenergetic field, and using multiple disciplines simultaneously is the mark of an elite warrior. The disciplines require training to use effectively — raw jade sensitivity is necessary but not sufficient. Green Bone training (typically through martial arts academies on Kekon) develops each discipline systematically, and most practitioners have relative strengths and weaknesses across the six.

## Limitations and costs

The most critical limitation is **biological sensitivity**. Not everyone can tolerate jade. Individuals without the hereditary sensitivity who wear jade experience the **Itches** — escalating symptoms from headaches and nausea to psychosis, seizure, and death. Even among the sensitive, the amount of jade a person can safely wear is finite and varies by individual. Wearing more jade amplifies abilities but increases the risk of overexposure.

**Jade overexposure** (wearing too much jade or wearing it for too long without adequate rest and training) produces symptoms collectively called **jade sickness**: paranoia, aggression, hallucinations, and progressive mental deterioration. The condition is physically addictive — removing jade from an overexposed wearer produces acute withdrawal symptoms, and long-term heavy jade use creates dependency.

**SN1** (Shine) and its pharmaceutical successors represent the foreign attempt to bypass the biological-sensitivity requirement. These drugs temporarily grant jade tolerance to people who lack natural sensitivity, but they are addictive, produce severe side effects with sustained use, and create a black market that destabilises Kekon's jade economy.

**Physical exhaustion** limits sustained jade use. Drawing on jade energy is metabolically demanding, and extended combat or heavy use of the more energy-intensive disciplines (Channeling and Deflection especially) leaves practitioners spent. A Green Bone who overtaxes their jade connection in a fight may collapse or become vulnerable.

The **social cost** is significant: Green Bone status carries obligations of loyalty, duty, and violence. Clan membership is not voluntary in any meaningful sense for those born into Green Bone families, and the warrior-aristocratic culture demands a willingness to kill and die that shapes every aspect of practitioners' lives.

## Practitioner access

Jade sensitivity is primarily hereditary, concentrated in the native Kekonese population and particularly in families with long histories of jade use. Training typically begins in childhood at martial arts academies (the most prestigious being Kaul Du Academy), where students develop their disciplines over years of intensive physical and mental training. A student who shows strong sensitivity and discipline mastery may be recruited into one of the major clans; those with weaker sensitivity serve in supporting roles.

Foreign access to jade abilities is a central tension of the trilogy. Without natural sensitivity, foreigners must use SN1 or its successors — producing jade soldiers who are effective but unstable and ultimately dependent on a drug supply. Some foreign nations attempt breeding programmes or genetic research to develop their own jade-sensitive populations, raising profound ethical and geopolitical questions.

## Cultural role

Jade and Green Bone culture are the foundation of Kekonese society. The Green Bone clans function simultaneously as martial orders, organised crime families, and governing institutions, controlling territory, adjudicating disputes, and providing social services in exchange for tribute and loyalty. Jade is sacred — traditionally, it belongs to the warriors who can use it and the island that produces it. The export, sale, or foreign use of jade is a source of deep cultural anxiety and political conflict.

The trilogy tracks how this traditional system adapts (and fails to adapt) to globalisation, foreign interference, and technological change over three decades. Jade's role shifts from sacred warrior emblem to contested global commodity, and the Green Bone clans must evolve from neighbourhood protection rackets into transnational organisations — or be destroyed by the forces they cannot control.
