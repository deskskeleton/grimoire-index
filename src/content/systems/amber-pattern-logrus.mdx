## Mechanics

The Amber multiverse is organized around two metaphysical poles. Amber, the one true city, is the seat of primal Order; the Courts of Chaos, at the opposite end of reality, are the seat of primal entropy. Between them stretch infinite Shadow worlds — parallel realities of varying fidelity to the real, cast outward from Amber like reflections in an infinite hall of mirrors. Earth is one such Shadow, unremarkable except that several of Amber's royal family have found it convenient.

The Pattern is a vast labyrinthine design inscribed in the dungeons of Castle Amber (with copies in other locations). Walking it — a grueling, potentially fatal ordeal — is the initiation rite for members of the royal family of Amber. Those who survive gain the ability to walk through Shadow, shifting between parallel worlds by concentrating on small changes in their surroundings until the reality around them matches their intention. A Shadow-walker moving through worlds might imagine the road becoming a little more forested, the sky a shade bluer, the technology a century more advanced, until they have arrived in a world that matches their desire. Whether they are traveling to pre-existing places or creating them by force of will is a question the series never fully resolves.

The Logrus is the chaotic counterpart to the Pattern, a shifting, maddening construct in the Courts of Chaos. Navigating it grants shapeshifting abilities and the power to extend tendrils of Logrus energy through Shadow to retrieve objects or people. Where the Pattern grants precise navigation, the Logrus grants reach and adaptability.

Trump cards are hand-painted portraits that function as psychic communication devices and teleportation gates. Concentrating on a Trump establishes a mental link with the person depicted; if both parties consent, one can step through the card to the other's location. Trump artistry is a rare skill even among Amber's royals, and the cards are treated as both tools and potential vulnerabilities.

## Limitations and costs

Walking the Pattern is dangerous — those without the blood of Amber who attempt it are destroyed, and even legitimate initiates may die if they falter partway through. The ordeal is physically and psychically exhausting, and it can only be survived once (though traversing copies of the Pattern is possible under certain circumstances). The Pattern also imposes a subtle alignment toward Order; sustained contact with it shapes the walker's perspective and loyalties.

Shadow-walking is limited by concentration and time. Shifting through Shadow is not instantaneous; it requires physical movement and sustained mental focus, and the farther one travels from Amber, the less stable reality becomes. Enemies can follow a Shadow-walker's trail, and certain locations — particularly those near Amber or Chaos — resist manipulation.

The Logrus carries its own costs: navigating it risks madness, and its gifts come with an alignment toward Chaos that can conflict with Pattern-based abilities. Characters who have walked both the Pattern and the Logrus experience direct metaphysical conflict, as the two forces resist coexistence within the same person.

Trump connections can be blocked, traced, or used as attack vectors by sufficiently skilled opponents. The cards also degrade or can be destroyed, and creating new ones requires rare artistic and psychic talent.

## Practitioner access

Access to the Pattern is strictly hereditary — only those of the blood of Amber (descendants of Dworkin and Oberon) can walk it and survive. The royal family of Amber is small, fractious, and immortal, which means the total number of Pattern initiates at any time is measured in the low dozens. The Logrus is similarly restricted to the royal house of Chaos. Trump artistry appears to be a learnable skill, but one that requires both talent and extended training, and it is practiced by only a handful of individuals across the series.

## Cultural role

Among Amber's royals, the Pattern is the foundation of identity and power. Having walked it is the basic qualification for political relevance, and the ability to move through Shadow is the mechanism by which the family maintains control over an infinite multiverse. The family's internal politics — a millennia-long contest of succession, betrayal, and grudging alliance — are the series' primary subject, and every magical ability is ultimately a tool in that contest. In the Courts of Chaos, the Logrus occupies an analogous role. For the inhabitants of Shadow worlds, including Earth, these forces are entirely invisible — the stage machinery behind a reality that appears self-contained.
