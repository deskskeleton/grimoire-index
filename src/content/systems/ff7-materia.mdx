## Mechanics

Materia are crystalline orbs — solidified fragments of Mako, the planet's spiritual lifeblood — that grant magical capabilities to whoever equips them. A character socketed with Materia can use the abilities the Materia confers; remove the Materia, and the abilities go with it. Each weapon and armor piece has a fixed number of Materia slots, sometimes paired ("linked") into combinatorial slots that allow Support Materia to modify other Materia in the linked slot.

Materia are sorted by color into five named families:

- **Magic Materia (green)** — grant spell abilities, organized in tiered sequences (Fire / Fira / Firaga, Cure / Cura / Curaga, etc.). Spell selection and tier unlock as the Materia gains AP through use.

- **Command Materia (yellow)** — grant new active commands beyond the standard "Attack / Magic / Item / Summon" set. Examples: Steal, Sense, Throw, Manipulate, Mime, Morph, Deathblow.

- **Support Materia (blue)** — paired with another Materia in a linked socket to modify it. All Materia + Magic gives spell-on-attack; Quadra Magic + a spell makes the spell cast four times; HP Absorb + a damaging Materia heals on hit; Final Attack + Phoenix auto-revives the user. Support Materia produce most of the system's combinatorial depth.

- **Independent Materia (purple)** — passive bonuses that work simply by being equipped. Examples: HP Plus, MP Plus, Counter, Cover, Pre-Emptive, Long Range, Underwater (the last is famously required to extend a fight against the Emerald Weapon).

- **Summon Materia (red)** — call a specific summoned being to deliver a powerful one-shot attack: Ifrit, Shiva, Bahamut, Knights of the Round, etc. Summons cost MP heavily and are typically the highest-tier per-cast options.

Each Materia gains experience points (AP) as the character uses the equipped Materia in battle. AP raises the Materia's Level; higher Levels unlock more spells (for Magic Materia), more uses per cast (for Support combinations), or stronger versions of the granted ability. When a Materia reaches its Mastered state, it produces a copy of itself at Level 1 — the canonical method for stockpiling powerful Materia is to grow Mastered copies and split them out.

A characteristic side effect: equipping certain Materia modifies the character's stats. Magic Materia tend to lower physical stats (Strength, Vitality) and raise Magic stats (Magic, Spirit) — the trade-off is consistent and produces meaningful build-shape decisions.

## Limitations and costs

The first cost is MP. Spell-casting Materia consume Magic Points per cast, drawn from the character's MP pool, which regenerates only at save points or with restorative items. Heavy spellcasting is expensive.

The second cost is socket scarcity. Equipment has a fixed number of slots, and linked slots are particularly scarce. Support Materia must be paired with the right primary Materia to be useful, and finding equipment with enough linked slots to enable a desired Materia combination is part of the build-economy.

The third cost is per-Materia growth. A new Materia starts at Level 1 with limited functionality. AP accumulation takes battle-time, so a character building toward a particular spell tier or combo has to grind to unlock it. The AP-doubling and AP-tripling weapon traits accelerate this but at a stat cost.

A subtler cost is environmental. Mako extraction by the Shinra Electric Power Company is a central narrative concern of the game — the world's principal villain corporation industrially harvests the planet's spiritual energy, accelerating the Lifestream's depletion. Materia exists because Mako can be crystallized; the more Materia in circulation, the more Mako has been pulled out of the planet's natural circulation. The game is explicit that this has long-term consequences.

## Practitioner access

Universal in-game: any party member can equip any Materia. There is no class gating, no innate-aptitude check, no Materia attunement requirement beyond physical possession. The system's openness is its principal design feature — a build can move between characters by transferring Materia.

In the wider fictional world, Materia is openly known and traded. Civilians can purchase Materia at shops; the Turks and Shinra military deploy Materia operationally; advanced specimens are prized and sometimes contested. The original Cetra (the in-game ancient race) had a different, deeper relationship with Materia and the Lifestream that informs the late-game plot.

## Cultural role

Gaia is a setting in which the magical economy and the industrial economy are the same economy. Mako energy powers Midgar's reactors; Materia is the consumer-electronics-tier output of the same magical resource. The game's central political conflict is the Cetra-coded ecological argument against this extraction, the company-coded justification for it, and the protagonist arc that resolves between them. Materia, as the system's most visible artifact, is both the story's central magic and its central political object.
