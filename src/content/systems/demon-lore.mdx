## Mechanics

The **Fallen** (also called Elohim or demons) are angels who participated in the rebellion against God and were cast into the Abyss — a featureless void — for millennia. When cracks appeared in the Abyss, they escaped into the modern World of Darkness, inhabiting the bodies of recently deceased or spiritually broken mortals. Their magical abilities are **Lores** — remnants of the divine powers they wielded during the creation of the universe.

Each demon belonged to one of seven **Houses** in Heaven, each responsible for a domain of Creation:

- **Devils** (House of the Morning Star) — Lore of the Celestials, Flame, Radiance
- **Scourges** (House of the Firmament) — Lore of Awakening, the Firmament, Winds
- **Malefactors** (House of the Fundament) — Lore of the Earth, Forge, Paths
- **Fiends** (House of the Spheres) — Lore of Light, Patterns, Portals
- **Defilers** (House of the Deep) — Lore of Longing, Storms, Transfiguration
- **Devourers** (House of the Wild) — Lore of the Beast, the Flesh, the Wild
- **Slayers** (House of the Second World) — Lore of Death, the Spirit, the Realms

Each Lore has five levels, and each level has two versions: a **low-Torment** version (compassionate, controlled, creative) and a **high-Torment** version (destructive, cruel, overwhelming). The demon's **Torment** score — a measure of their accumulated rage, bitterness, and inhumanity — determines which version manifests. A demon seeking redemption must keep Torment low, sacrificing raw power for moral integrity.

The fuel is **Faith** — the belief and devotion of mortal thralls. Demons build networks of **thralls** — mortals who have witnessed the demon's true nature and offered their faith. Faith replenishes the demon's power pool, enables Lore activations, and allows the demon to assume their **Apocalyptic Form** (a manifestation of their angelic nature, either beautiful or horrific depending on Torment).

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **Torment**. Cruel, selfish, or destructive actions increase Torment, which progressively corrupts the demon's Lores — making them more powerful but less controllable and more harmful. High-Torment demons produce effects that are devastating but indiscriminate, harming allies and innocents. The mechanical tension is that doing evil makes you stronger in the short term and more monstrous in the long term.

The second cost is **Faith dependency**. Without mortal thralls providing Faith, a demon's power diminishes rapidly. Building and maintaining a thrall network requires social interaction, trust-building, and the moral complexity of using mortals as spiritual batteries. Exploiting thralls is efficient but increases Torment; genuinely caring for them is harder but preserves the demon's humanity.

The third cost is **host body limitations**. The demon inhabits a mortal body that constrains their physical capabilities. The Apocalyptic Form (which reveals inhuman features — wings, extra eyes, radiant beauty, or monstrous deformity) can be assumed temporarily but risks exposure and Masquerade violations.

## Practitioner access

Only the Fallen — escaped angels — possess Lores. There are precisely 666 named Fallen in the game's cosmology (the biblical number), though not all have escaped the Abyss. Each demon's available Lores are determined by their House and any cross-House Lores they have traded for or acquired through pacts. Mortals cannot use Lores, though demons can invest Faith in thralls to grant them minor supernatural abilities.

## Cultural role

The Fallen have no unified society — they escaped the Abyss individually and must rebuild connections in a world they do not recognize. Five **Factions** represent different philosophical responses to their situation: the Luciferans (seek Lucifer and continue the rebellion), the Faustians (build earthly power through mortal manipulation), the Cryptics (investigate what went wrong with Creation), the Reconcilers (seek redemption and return to God's grace), and the Raveners (destroy Creation out of nihilistic rage). Lore use is shaped by factional ideology — a Reconciler uses low-Torment effects to help mortals, while a Ravener uses high-Torment effects to tear reality apart.
