## Mechanics

**Dreamwalking** is the trained ability to enter **Tel'aran'rhiod** — the World of Dreams — deliberately and with conscious control. Tel'aran'rhiod is a parallel dimension that reflects the physical world: its landscape mirrors the waking world's geography, but it is governed by **willpower** rather than physics. A dreamwalker who enters Tel'aran'rhiod can traverse the reflected landscape, observe events and locations across vast distances, communicate with other dreamers, and manipulate the dream environment through force of will.

The system's key mechanics include:

**Environmental control**: within Tel'aran'rhiod, a disciplined mind can reshape the immediate environment — conjuring objects, changing clothing, altering the landscape. An undisciplined mind does the same involuntarily, with random thoughts and fears manifesting as physical changes. Mastery of dreamwalking is largely mastery of one's own thoughts.

**Observation**: the Dream reflects the waking world in near-real-time. A dreamwalker can visit the reflection of a distant city and observe its current state — buildings, terrain, and objects are faithfully reproduced, though people do not normally appear unless they are also dreaming or present in Tel'aran'rhiod. This makes dreamwalking a powerful reconnaissance tool.

**Communication**: two dreamwalkers in Tel'aran'rhiod simultaneously can communicate as if physically present, regardless of their waking bodies' locations. The Wise Ones use this routinely for long-distance coordination.

**Danger**: the Dream is not safe. Nightmares and dark forces inhabit Tel'aran'rhiod. A dreamwalker who loses concentration can be reshaped by their own fears. Dying in Tel'aran'rhiod can kill the physical body. Other dreamers — including hostile ones — can attack, trap, or manipulate a visitor. The boundaries between dreaming and waking can blur dangerously, producing effects that persist after the dreamwalker wakes.

The Wise One tradition treats dreamwalking as **distinct from channeling**. Some dreamwalkers can channel; many cannot. The abilities are independent — a dreamwalker's power in Tel'aran'rhiod comes from mental discipline and trained willpower, not from the One Power.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **mental discipline**. Uncontrolled thoughts in Tel'aran'rhiod are dangerous. A stray thought about fire can produce real fire; a moment of fear can manifest what is feared. Training to maintain absolute mental control is the central challenge of the discipline, and it takes years of instruction under experienced Wise Ones.

The second cost is **physical vulnerability**. While dreamwalking, the practitioner's physical body is asleep and defenseless. Extended time in Tel'aran'rhiod means extended physical vulnerability in the waking world.

The third cost is **risk of entrapment**. Tel'aran'rhiod is seductive — the freedom and power it offers can make the waking world feel diminished. Additionally, hostile entities (including Forsaken who enter the Dream) can lay traps, create false environments, or compel a dreamwalker into dangerous situations.

A structural limitation is that dreamwalking is primarily observational. While a dreamwalker can interact with the Dream environment, their ability to affect the waking world from within Tel'aran'rhiod is limited and indirect.

## Practitioner access

Dreamwalking requires an innate Talent that cannot be acquired through training alone. The Talent must be present; training then develops it. Among the Aiel, the Wise Ones identify potential dreamwalkers early and apprentice them into a rigorous training tradition. Outside Aiel culture, dreamwalking is poorly understood — Egwene al'Vere is trained by the Wise Ones as an outsider, one of the few non-Aiel to receive formal instruction.

The Talent is rare. Even among the Aiel Wise Ones — the tradition most practiced in developing it — only a small fraction are dreamwalkers.

## Cultural role

Among the Aiel, dreamwalking is one of the most respected Wise One abilities. It serves practical functions — long-distance communication, reconnaissance, and inter-clan coordination — and carries deep cultural weight as a connection to a metaphysical dimension the Aiel regard with reverence and caution. Wise One dreamwalkers are among the most influential figures in Aiel society, and their counsel carries authority on both practical and spiritual matters.

Outside Aiel culture, dreamwalking is barely known. The Aes Sedai have fragmentary understanding of Tel'aran'rhiod, mostly through ter'angreal that allow entry, but they lack the Wise Ones' systematic training tradition. Egwene's cross-cultural training — learning Aiel dreamwalking techniques and bringing them to the White Tower — is presented as a significant transfer of knowledge.
