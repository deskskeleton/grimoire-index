## Mechanics

Tortallan magic operates through three distinct but coexisting systems.

**The Gift** is the most common form of magic. It manifests as colored magical fire — each practitioner's Gift has a distinctive hue (Alanna's is violet, Numair's is black with white sparkles, Thom's is orange). The Gift is innate: you are born with it or you are not, and its raw strength is fixed at birth, though training dramatically affects how effectively it can be used. Gifted practitioners study at the Royal University or under individual masters, learning to channel their power into specific applications: healing, elemental manipulation, combat magic, wards, illusions, and scholarly workings like scrying and translation. The Gift draws on the practitioner's personal energy reserves — overuse causes physical exhaustion, and extreme overuse can kill.

**The Sight** is a rarer ability that grants magical perception. Practitioners with the Sight can perceive magic directly (seeing spells, wards, and enchantments), detect lies, and sometimes receive prophetic visions. The Sight is passive rather than active — it provides information rather than enabling action. Alanna possesses the Sight in addition to her Gift, which allows her to perceive magical threats that other mages might miss.

**Wild Magic** is the rarest of the three systems, explored primarily through Daine Sarrasri in *The Immortals* quartet. Wild Magic grants a deep, instinctive connection to animals and the natural world. Daine can communicate telepathically with animals, heal them, sense their presence at great distances, and (at the height of her power) shapeshift into animal forms. Wild Magic operates outside the framework of the Gift — it cannot be taught at the University, and conventional mages often fail to recognize it as magic at all. Daine's Wild Magic is exceptionally strong, partly because she is the daughter of the god Weiryn and the Green Lady, giving her a divine connection to the natural world.

The three systems coexist with an active **divine framework**. The gods of Tortall are real, present, and occasionally interventionist. The Great Mother Goddess directly aids Alanna. The trickster god Kyprioth manipulates events in the Copper Isles. Divine magic operates at a scale beyond mortal practitioners, and the barrier between the mortal realm and the Divine Realms can weaken, allowing immortal creatures (griffins, spidrens, Stormwings) to cross over.

## Limitations and costs

The Gift's primary cost is **physical exhaustion**. Every magical working draws on the practitioner's personal energy. Minor workings are trivial; major workings can leave a mage bedridden for days. Pushing beyond one's limits risks unconsciousness, permanent damage, or death. Numair, despite being one of the most powerful mages alive, must carefully manage his energy expenditure in sustained conflicts.

Training is a **prerequisite for safe use**. An untrained Gift is dangerous — magic can discharge uncontrollably, harming the practitioner and bystanders. The Royal University and the master-apprentice system exist not merely to develop talent but to prevent magical accidents. Thom of Trebond's story is a cautionary example: his enormous raw power, combined with ambition and insufficient wisdom, leads to catastrophic outcomes.

Wild Magic carries a **psychological cost**. Daine's deep connection to animals makes her vulnerable to losing herself in animal consciousness. Early in her story, she "went animal" — losing her human identity entirely and living as a wolf pack member. The risk of dissolution into animal mind is a persistent danger for Wild Magic practitioners, requiring conscious effort to maintain human identity.

The divine framework imposes **obligations**. Gods who grant aid expect service in return. Alanna's relationship with the Goddess is reciprocal — the Goddess's gifts come with missions, expectations, and the implicit understanding that Alanna serves as the Goddess's agent in the mortal world. Divine magic is never free.

## Practitioner access

The Gift is **hereditary but unpredictable**. It runs in families but can appear in any bloodline. Noble families with strong magical traditions (like the Queenscoves, who are hereditary healers) tend to produce Gifted children, but commoners can also be born with the Gift. Training is available at the Royal University and through individual masters, and the crown sponsors talented students regardless of birth. Access to magical education is relatively open by the standards of Tortall's otherwise feudal society.

Wild Magic is **extremely rare** and is not taught through institutional channels. Daine's training comes from Numair, who recognizes her talent and improvises a curriculum. The Sight is similarly rare and largely self-manifesting — there is no school for it.

## Cultural role

Magic is an **accepted and integrated** part of Tortallan society. Healers serve in hospitals and on battlefields. Combat mages serve in the military. The Royal University is a prestigious institution. Magical talent confers social mobility — a commoner with a strong Gift can rise to positions of influence. However, specific forms of magic carry social stigma: necromancy is forbidden, and Wild Magic is poorly understood and sometimes feared. The gods' active involvement in mortal affairs means that religious institutions and magical practice overlap significantly, with temple healers and goddess-touched warriors occupying recognized social roles.
