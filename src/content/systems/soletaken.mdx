## Mechanics

**Soletaken** and **D'ivers** are two forms of supernatural shapeshifting that originate from the ancient **First Empire** (the first great human civilization in the Malazan world, associated with the T'lan Imass and the earliest mortal use of Warren-adjacent magic).

A **Soletaken** transforms into a single creature — typically a large, powerful animal or beast. The transformation is complete: the Soletaken assumes the full physical form, size, senses, and capabilities of the creature. Common forms include great cats (Treach becomes an enormous tiger), wolves, bears, eagles, dragons (the most powerful and rarest form), and various monstrous creatures. The Soletaken retains their intelligence and memories while transformed but is influenced by the instincts of the assumed form.

A **D'ivers** fractures into a **multitude** of smaller creatures — a swarm, pack, or flock. Gryllen becomes hundreds of rats; Ryllandaras becomes a pack of jackals; other D'ivers become swarms of insects, bats, or serpents. The D'ivers consciousness is distributed across all component creatures, maintaining a collective awareness. This makes D'ivers extraordinarily difficult to kill — destroying individual creatures in the swarm does not kill the D'ivers, though reducing the swarm beyond a critical threshold can.

The origin of both abilities is tied to ancient rituals — specifically, the **First Ritual** associated with the First Empire's sorcerous traditions and (for some) the Warren of **Soletaken** (a Warren-like space associated with shapeshifting). The ritual that creates Soletaken and D'ivers is rarely performed in the current age; most existing shapeshifters are ancient beings who underwent the transformation centuries or millennia ago.

The **Path of Hands** is a legendary pilgrimage in which Soletaken and D'ivers converge on a specific location — an Azath House — seeking to enter it and achieve **Ascendancy** (godhood). The Path of Hands is one of the series' major plot threads, particularly in *Deadhouse Gates*, where dozens of shapeshifters rampage across a continent converging on the Azath House in Tremorlor.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **bestial madness**. Over time, the animal instincts of the assumed form erode the human psyche. Ancient Soletaken and D'ivers are frequently described as partially or fully consumed by their beast natures — Ryllandaras is more jackal than man, operating on predatory instinct rather than rational thought. The longer a shapeshifter exists, the more dominant the beast becomes.

The second cost is **hunger and violence**. The assumed forms carry predatory drives. D'ivers swarms in particular are depicted as insatiably hungry — they consume everything in their path, leaving devastation. This makes Soletaken and D'ivers dangerous to everyone, including themselves.

The third cost is **social isolation**. Shapeshifters are feared and hunted. Their bestial madness makes them poor allies, and their predatory nature makes settled coexistence difficult. Most ancient Soletaken and D'ivers are solitary, nomadic, and avoided by mortal civilizations.

## Practitioner access

The shapeshifting ritual is ancient and the knowledge to perform it is nearly lost. Most existing Soletaken and D'ivers are survivors from earlier ages who have persisted for centuries or millennia. New shapeshifters are occasionally created but the process is dangerous and poorly understood. The ability is not hereditary (though some bloodlines carry predispositions), and it cannot be learned through training — it requires a specific ritual transformation.

## Cultural role

Soletaken and D'ivers exist outside normal Malazan society. They are forces of nature more than members of civilization — ancient, dangerous, and driven by instincts that predate mortal culture. In *Deadhouse Gates*, their convergence on the Path of Hands is treated as a natural disaster by the populations in their path. The few shapeshifters who maintain rational thought (Treach before his ascension, Mappo's partial transformation) are exceptions who demonstrate the cost through their visible struggle against bestial nature.
