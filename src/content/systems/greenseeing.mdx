## Mechanics

**Greenseeing** is a prophetic and perceptive ability tied to the **weirwood network** — a continent-spanning root system connecting the ancient heart trees carved with faces by the Children of the Forest. A greenseer can tap into this network to perceive events at other locations (where weirwoods stand), view the past through the trees' recorded memories, and receive symbolic visions of possible futures — typically through dreams.

The ability manifests in two tiers. The lesser form, **greensight**, produces prophetic dreams: vivid, symbolic, often frightening visions that the dreamer cannot control or fully interpret. Jojen Reed has this ability — his "green dreams" are reliable but cryptic, and he cannot choose what to see. The greater form involves direct communion with the weirwood network, achieved by physically bonding with a weirwood root system. Bran Stark, guided by Bloodraven (the Three-Eyed Crow), achieves this deeper connection by traveling to a cave beyond the Wall where a greenseer has been physically merged with the roots for decades.

Through the weirwoods, a fully realized greenseer can look backward through time — witnessing events that occurred near any weirwood tree, potentially across thousands of years. The faces carved into heart trees serve as eyes. There are hints that greenseers can also influence the past in limited ways, though the mechanics of this are deliberately left ambiguous.

Green dreams — the entry-level manifestation — come unbidden and cannot be directed. The dreamer sees symbolic imagery (drowning in tidal waves, wolves with human eyes) that maps to future events in ways that are obvious only in retrospect.

## Limitations and costs

The defining cost of full greenseeing is **physical immobility**. Bloodraven has been seated among weirwood roots for so long that the roots have grown through his body — he is alive but immobile, more tree than man. This appears to be the inevitable endpoint for any greenseer who fully embraces the weirwood network: omniscience purchased with embodiment.

The second limitation is **dependence on weirwoods**. A greenseer can only see through weirwood eyes, meaning regions where weirwoods have been cut down (most of southern Westeros, where the Andals destroyed heart trees centuries ago) are blind spots. The network's coverage is uneven and cannot be extended.

The third cost is **interpretive ambiguity**. Green dreams are not clear messages — they are symbolic and resist straightforward reading. Jojen knows his dreams are true but frequently cannot determine what they mean until events unfold. The greenseer sees, but does not necessarily understand.

A subtler cost is **temporal disorientation**. A greenseer immersed in the weirwood network can lose track of the present, becoming absorbed in past visions. The past is seductive in the same way the Wolf Dream is seductive to wolfbrothers — the wealth of sensory experience can overwhelm the practitioner's connection to their own time.

## Practitioner access

Greenseeing requires an innate gift. It appears to be related to skinchanging — most greenseers are also skinchangers — but the two abilities are not identical. Among the Children of the Forest, greenseers were rare even in their own species (said to be one in a thousand, and one skinchanger in a thousand skinchangers becomes a greenseer). Among humans, the gift is extraordinarily rare. The only confirmed human greenseers in the series are Bran Stark, Bloodraven, and Jojen Reed (at the lesser green-dream level).

## Cultural role

Greenseeing is tied to the **Old Gods** religion and the Children of the Forest. In the North, where weirwoods survive and the Old Gods are still worshipped, the heart trees are sacred spaces — but the connection to greenseeing has been forgotten by nearly all Northerners. The free folk beyond the Wall have somewhat more awareness, but even they treat greenseers as figures of legend rather than living practitioners. South of the Wall, the concept is essentially unknown. The Children of the Forest, who developed and maintained the tradition, have dwindled to near-extinction — Bran's training represents what may be the last transmission of greenseer knowledge.
