## Mechanics

The **True Power** is drawn directly from the **Dark One** — the primordial entity imprisoned at Shayol Ghul — rather than from the True Source that feeds the One Power. It functions similarly to the One Power in practical terms: a channeler weaves it into effects, and it can replicate most of what the One Power does. However, it differs in several critical respects.

The True Power is **not split by gender**. Any practitioner can access the full force without the saidin/saidar distinction. It is also **undetectable** by One Power channelers — a weave of the True Power cannot be seen or sensed by someone using saidin or saidar, and wards against the One Power do not block it. This makes it a potent tool for covert operations and ambush.

Access to the True Power requires the **Dark One's explicit permission**. It is not an ambient energy that can be seized; the Dark One grants or withholds access as he chooses. In practice, only the Forsaken (the Dark One's most powerful servants) have been granted access, and even among them it is rationed — the Dark One uses access as both reward and leash.

The visual signature of True Power use is the **saa** — dark specks that drift across the user's eyes, visible to observers. Prolonged use produces permanent black streaks across the whites of the eyes. Extreme use eventually replaces the eyes entirely with caverns of flame.

## Limitations and costs

The True Power is **intensely addictive**. Users describe it as intoxicating in a way that exceeds even the seductiveness of the One Power. Ishamael (later Moridin), the most prolific True Power user among the Forsaken, is visibly consumed by his dependency, and his erratic behavior in the later books is partly attributable to it.

The second cost is **physical degradation**. The saa are only the earliest symptom. Prolonged use produces progressive bodily corruption that mirrors the Dark One's influence: tissue decay, sensory distortion, and eventually the replacement of the user's physical features with manifestations of the Dark One's essence.

The third cost is **enslavement**. Because access requires the Dark One's permission, every use of the True Power reinforces the user's dependence on and subordination to the Dark One. The Forsaken — who pride themselves on their autonomy and political maneuvering — are systematically compromised by their reliance on a power source their master can revoke at will.

The True Power cannot be used to create wards against the One Power (and vice versa), and the two forces interact unpredictably. Balefire woven from the True Power appears to have enhanced or altered properties.

## Practitioner access

Access is strictly by the Dark One's grant. No training, talent, or birthright enables True Power use without the Dark One's active permission. The number of practitioners at any given time is small — typically limited to the thirteen Forsaken, and even among them, only a few are granted regular access. Rand al'Thor accesses the True Power once, involuntarily, through his metaphysical link with Moridin.

## Cultural role

The True Power is secret. The general population of Randland has no knowledge of its existence. Even among channelers, awareness of the True Power is limited to the most senior Aes Sedai scholars and the Forsaken themselves. It has no institutional structure, no teaching tradition, and no cultural presence — it exists entirely as a covert tool of the Shadow's most elite servants. Its existence is one of the series' late-stage revelations, recontextualizing several earlier events.
