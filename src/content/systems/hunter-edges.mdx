## Mechanics

The Hunter games present two distinct frameworks for mortal supernatural abilities.

In **Hunter: The Reckoning** (1999), ordinary people receive the **Imbuing** — a sudden, involuntary awakening triggered by witnessing supernatural horror. During the Imbuing, mysterious entities called the **Messengers** (or Heralds) grant the hunter the ability to perceive the supernatural (the **Second Sight**) and access to **Edges** — limited supernatural powers organized into three categories:

- **Innocence** (Zeal) — protective and purifying abilities: shielding against supernatural powers, cleansing corruption, bolstering allies
- **Martyrdom** (Mercy) — healing, sacrifice, and endurance: restoring the injured, absorbing damage meant for others, resisting mental domination
- **Redemption** (Vision) — perception and judgment: seeing through disguises, detecting lies, understanding a creature's nature

Edges are deliberately limited — they provide enough to survive encounters with vampires, werewolves, and other creatures, but not enough to match them in raw power. Hunters win through numbers, preparation, and mundane resources (weapons, fire, sunlight).

In **Hunter: The Vigil** (2008, Chronicles of Darkness), the framework shifts from divine imbuing to **organizational Endowments** — supernatural tools and techniques provided by hunter conspiracies:

- **Aegis Kai Doru** wields ancient supernatural relics (Relics)
- **The Lucifuge** has innate demonic bloodline powers (Castigation)
- **Task Force: VALKYRIE** uses government-developed anti-supernatural technology (Advanced Armory)
- **The Cheiron Group** grafts monster body parts onto hunter operatives (Thaumatechnology)
- **Ascending Ones** brew alchemical elixirs from ancient Egyptian recipes (Elixirs)
- **Malleus Maleficarum** channels Catholic benedictions (Benedictions)

Each conspiracy's Endowment reflects its ideology and methods — faith-based groups use prayers and relics, technocratic groups use technology, and the morally grey ones use the monsters' own weapons against them.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost in Reckoning is **Conviction** — the resource that powers Edges. Conviction is replenished by acting in accordance with the hunter's Creed (their philosophical approach to the hunt), but depleted by moral failures, doubt, and encountering phenomena that challenge their worldview. Hunters who lose all Conviction become vulnerable to despair and madness.

The primary cost in Vigil is **organizational dependency and moral compromise**. Each conspiracy's Endowments come with strings attached: VALKYRIE hunters answer to government handlers, Cheiron operatives are slowly dehumanized by their grafts, Lucifuge members struggle with their demonic heritage, and Ascending Ones risk addiction to their own elixirs.

In both versions, hunters are **fundamentally outmatched**. A single vampire can shrug off gunfire; a werewolf in Crinos form can tear through a squad. Hunters compensate with preparation, numbers, and targeting weaknesses, but casualties are expected and the emotional toll of the hunt — paranoia, isolation, moral erosion — is the game's central cost.

The third cost is **social destruction**. The hunt consumes relationships, careers, and mental health. Hunters who know about the supernatural cannot unknow it, and the knowledge isolates them from normal society.

## Practitioner access

In Reckoning, the Imbuing is involuntary and seemingly random — the Messengers choose, and the chosen cannot refuse. Not all Imbued survive their first encounter with the supernatural. The hunter community is small, fragmented, and communicates primarily through hunter-net (an encrypted internet forum).

In Vigil, access depends on conspiracy recruitment. Tier-one hunters (cells) have no Endowments and operate on mundane resources alone. Tier-two hunters (compacts) have informal organizational support. Only tier-three hunters (conspiracies) have access to Endowments, and joining a conspiracy requires meeting its specific criteria — bloodline (Lucifuge), military service (VALKYRIE), corporate employment (Cheiron), or religious ordination (Malleus Maleficarum).

## Cultural role

Hunters occupy the unique position of being ordinary humans in a world dominated by supernatural power structures. They have no place in vampire politics, werewolf cosmology, or mage philosophy — they are outsiders who fight because they cannot look away. The Reckoning version frames this as a divine calling; the Vigil version frames it as institutional responses to an existential threat. In both cases, hunters represent the World of Darkness from the perspective of its victims rather than its monsters.
