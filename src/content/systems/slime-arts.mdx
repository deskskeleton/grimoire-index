## Mechanics

The magic system of *The Eminence in Shadow* operates on two distinct tiers: the **conventional mana system** that governs the setting's sword-and-sorcery framework, and the **slime arts** — Cid Kagenou's unique approach to mana that functions on principles no one else in the world understands or can replicate.

**Conventional mana manipulation** is the world's standard supernatural framework. Mana is an ambient energy that most people can sense and channel to varying degrees. Standard applications include reinforcing the body for enhanced strength and speed, projecting mana as offensive blasts, imbuing weapons with cutting or elemental power, generating shields, and casting elemental spells. The system follows familiar fantasy-RPG logic: practitioners have mana reserves that deplete with use and regenerate over time, stronger practitioners have larger reserves and more efficient channeling, and training improves both capacity and control.

Combat magic in this world is primarily martial. Swordsmen channel mana through their blades to cut harder and faster; warriors reinforce their bodies to absorb punishment; mages project elemental attacks at range. There is an understood hierarchy of power — common soldiers, skilled knights, elite swordsmen, and legendary-tier combatants — and the gaps between tiers are significant but comprehensible. The world's magical establishment (including the Cult of Diablos, various academies, and national military orders) all operate within this framework.

**Cid's slime technique** shatters this framework entirely. Through obsessive experimentation in both his previous life and his reincarnated one, Cid discovered that mana can be compressed and manipulated at a molecular level — treated not as a fuel to be burned but as a substance to be shaped with arbitrary precision. His foundational technique involves compressing his own mana to extreme density, then releasing it in controlled expansions. He calls this his "slime" technique because he first developed it by practicing mana control on slimes — reshaping his body's mana pathways the way one would knead putty.

The practical applications are staggering. **Body modification**: Cid can reshape his physical form by redistributing compressed mana through his body, altering his height, build, facial features, and even his voice. **I Am Atomic**: his signature ultimate technique compresses mana to nuclear-reaction density and releases it as a directed explosion capable of obliterating everything in a massive radius — functionally a mana-fueled nuclear detonation. **Combat enhancement**: at baseline, Cid's mana reinforcement is so far beyond the world's standard that elite swordsmen cannot perceive his movements. **Mana constructs**: he can project mana as blades, shields, tendrils, and other shapes with precision that conventional practitioners cannot approach.

The **Shadow Garden** — Cid's organization of elite female warriors — operates at a level between conventional magic and Cid's personal tier. Cid taught the Seven Shadows (Alpha, Beta, Gamma, Delta, Epsilon, Zeta, Eta) a standardized version of his mana control techniques, adapted to their individual capacities. Shadow Garden members are dramatically stronger than conventional elite combatants but still fall far short of Cid's personal power. Their combat magic emphasizes efficient mana reinforcement, precise blade techniques, and coordinated tactical operations.

## Limitations and costs

**Mana reserves** are the primary resource constraint for conventional practitioners. Standard mana users deplete their reserves through combat and spellcasting, requiring rest to regenerate. Running dry in combat means losing all magical enhancement — a mana-depleted swordsman is just a swordsman.

For Cid, the limitation is theoretical rather than practical. His mana reserves are so absurdly vast — a consequence of his lifelong obsessive training — that he functionally never runs out during any depicted encounter. The system's comedy depends partly on this: the "cost" mechanics that constrain every other character simply do not apply to him.

**Knowledge and preparation** represent the true barrier in the system. Cid's slime technique is not replicable through talent alone — it requires a conceptual understanding of mana manipulation that no one else in the world possesses. He approaches mana the way a physicist approaches matter, while everyone else treats it as a mystical force to be channeled through tradition and instinct. This knowledge gap is the real limitation of the system: the techniques exist, but no one besides Cid has the framework to develop them independently.

**Physical toll** affects conventional practitioners more than Cid but is present throughout the system. Over-channeling mana damages the body — burst blood vessels, torn muscles, organ strain. The Cult of Diablos's mana-draining rituals exploit this vulnerability, forcibly extracting mana from victims to fuel their own operations, often killing or permanently crippling the source.

**Attention and concentration** matter in combat. Mana manipulation requires active focus; a distracted or disoriented practitioner loses efficiency. This applies even to Cid, whose most precise techniques (body reshaping, nuclear compression) require deliberate concentration rather than reflexive action.

## Practitioner access

Mana sensitivity is nearly universal in this world — most people can channel mana to some degree, making basic magical enhancement a standard component of martial training. The distribution of talent follows a steep curve: the majority of people have modest mana reserves and limited control, a smaller group achieves knight-level competence, a handful reach elite status, and the truly powerful are vanishingly rare.

The Shadow Garden operates as a parallel power structure. Its members are recruited from individuals with exceptional natural talent (often from marginalized backgrounds — many of the Seven Shadows were outcasts or victims before Cid found them). Cid's training elevates them far beyond conventional elite status, creating a hidden cadre of warriors who can overpower most opponents the world considers powerful.

Cid himself exists entirely outside the world's power hierarchy. His abilities are so far beyond the understood ceiling that observers consistently misinterpret what they are seeing — attributing his feats to artifacts, ancient magic, divine intervention, or simply refusing to believe what happened. The system's demographic structure is defined by this gap: everyone else operates within a comprehensible power framework, while Cid operates in a category the world does not know exists.

## Cultural role

Magic in *The Eminence in Shadow* is **openly known and integrated** into society. Nations maintain magical military forces, academies train young practitioners, and elite combatants are publicly ranked and recognized. The Cult of Diablos operates as a hidden power manipulating this structure from within, using mana-draining rituals and political infiltration to accumulate power.

The system's narrative function is primarily **satirical**. Conventional magic provides the straight-faced fantasy setting that Cid's absurd power level parodies. The gap between what the world considers powerful and what Cid can actually do generates the story's comedy — his "chuunibyou" theatrical performances (dramatic speeches, deliberately inefficient-looking techniques, the entire "Shadow" persona) are played for laughs precisely because the power behind them is genuine and overwhelming.

At a deeper level, the system comments on **power fantasy conventions** themselves. Cid's magic is the isekai protagonist's dream made literal: total power achieved through obsessive training, wrapped in a performative humility that fools everyone. The system's structure — a generic fantasy baseline with one character who has broken it completely — is both the joke and the point.
