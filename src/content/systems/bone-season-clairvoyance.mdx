## Mechanics

Clairvoyance in the Bone Season universe is the ability to perceive and interact with the aether, a spirit realm that coexists with the physical world. Every clairvoyant possesses a "dreamscape" — a mental landscape representing their inner consciousness — and the ability to sense or manipulate the aether in some fashion. The aether is populated by the spirits of the dead, and much of clairvoyant power involves interacting with, commanding, or being affected by these spirits.

Shannon's taxonomy organizes clairvoyants into seven orders based on the nature of their aetheric interaction. Soothsayers read numen — the traces left by the future — through various methods like cards, mirrors, or automatic writing. Mediums channel or communicate with the dead. Sensors detect aetheric disturbances and can identify other clairvoyants. Augurs read the future through physical signs. Furies project raw psychic force. Guardians bind and control spirits. And jumpers — the rarest order — can project their spirits outside their own bodies entirely.

Paige Mahoney is a dreamwalker, a subtype of jumper who can invade other people's dreamscapes and take control of their minds. This makes her one of the most dangerous and valuable clairvoyants alive. Dreamwalking requires her to project her spirit out of her body (leaving it vulnerable), break through the target's mental defenses, and impose her will on their dreamscape. Against Rephaim, whose dreamscapes are vastly more complex and fortified than human ones, this ability is both extraordinarily powerful and extraordinarily risky.

The Rephaim add a second dimension to the system. These beings feed on the aura produced by clairvoyants, and they can enhance a clairvoyant's abilities through a process called "linking" — a bond between Rephaite and human that amplifies power at the cost of dependency. Warden's link with Paige strengthens her dreamwalking but also creates a psychic and emotional connection she cannot fully control.

## Limitations and costs

Clairvoyance exacts a physical and psychological toll. Sustained use of abilities — particularly projection, dreamwalking, and spirit combat — causes exhaustion, nosebleeds, migraines, and in extreme cases, damage to the clairvoyant's own dreamscape. A shattered dreamscape means madness or death. Paige repeatedly pushes her abilities to the breaking point, and each time the strain accumulates.

The social cost is enormous. Under Scion law, clairvoyance is a capital crime. Clairvoyants who are discovered face execution or — worse — deportation to the Rephaim's penal colonies, where they are forced to serve as slaves, soldiers, and aura sources. Even within the criminal syndicate that shelters clairvoyants in London, a rigid hierarchy determines the value and treatment of different types, with rarer abilities commanding higher status and more common ones being treated as expendable.

The Rephaim's exploitation adds a layer of cost that most clairvoyants do not even know exists. The entire clairvoyant underworld is, unknowingly, a farming system — Scion identifies and captures clairvoyants, and the Rephaim harvest them. The linking process, while empowering, creates dependency and vulnerability, binding human practitioners to beings whose ultimate motives remain uncertain.

## Practitioner access

Clairvoyance is innate — a person is either born with the ability to perceive the aether or they are not. There is no way to acquire it through training, ritual, or technology. The gift appears to have some hereditary component, running in certain family lines, but it can also manifest spontaneously. Within the clairvoyant population, the specific type of ability a person possesses is fixed at birth and cannot be changed or expanded.

Training matters enormously in how effectively a clairvoyant uses their gift. The syndicate system of mime-lords and mime-queens taking on mollishers and hirelings functions partly as an apprenticeship system, with experienced clairvoyants teaching younger ones to refine and control their abilities. The Rephaim also train their human captives, though with considerably less regard for the students' wellbeing.

## Cultural role

Clairvoyance defines the social structure of Shannon's alternate London at every level. Scion has built an entire authoritarian government around the persecution of clairvoyants, using "unnaturalness" as the justification for surveillance, curfews, and state violence. Meanwhile, the clairvoyant underworld has developed its own parallel society with its own laws, territories, and hierarchies — the syndicate ruled by the Underlord or Underqueen from the Unnatural Assembly. Clairvoyant type determines social standing within this underworld: rare and powerful types like oracles and dreamwalkers command respect, while common types like lower-order sensors are treated as foot soldiers. The entire system mirrors real-world dynamics of marginalization, criminalization, and resistance — clairvoyants are simultaneously a persecuted minority and a hidden power structure.
