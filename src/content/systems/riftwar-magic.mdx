## Mechanics

**Riftwar Magic** operates through two fundamental pathways: the **Lesser Path** and the **Greater Path**. The Lesser Path is formulaic — practitioners learn specific spells through study, memorization, and precise execution. Each spell has a defined effect, and mastery comes through accumulating a repertoire of known spells and executing them with technical precision. The Greater Path is intuitive — practitioners channel ambient magical energy through sheer will, producing effects through mental visualization and force of personality rather than learned formulae.

On **Midkemia** (the primary world), the Lesser Path tradition dominates. The magicians of the Kingdom of the Isles and the Free Cities learn spell-craft through apprenticeship and study, mastering individual spells of increasing complexity. The Academy at Stardock later becomes an institutional center for magical education. Midkemian magic draws on a world with rich ambient magical energy, and powerful practitioners can produce effects ranging from fireballs and teleportation to dimensional rifts.

On **Kelewan** (the Tsurani homeworld, connected to Midkemia by the titular rifts), the Greater Path tradition is dominant. The **Assembly of Magicians** (the Great Ones) wield enormous power through will-based magic, operating above the law of the Tsurani Empire. A Greater Path magician like **Milamber** (Pug's Tsurani identity) can reshape reality through concentrated will — controlling weather, tearing open rifts between worlds, and producing effects of catastrophic scale.

**Pug** is unique in mastering both paths. His inability to learn Lesser Path magic on Midkemia was not a lack of talent but a misidentification of his nature — he is a Greater Path magician in a Lesser Path tradition. His training on Kelewan unlocks his true potential, and his eventual mastery of both traditions makes him the most powerful magician in either world.

The **rift** technology — magical gateways between Midkemia and Kelewan — represents the most dramatic application of Greater Path magic. Creating and maintaining a rift requires immense power and represents the literal intersection of two magical traditions across dimensional boundaries.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **path exclusivity**. Most practitioners can only follow one path — Lesser or Greater — and attempting the wrong one produces failure. This creates a diagnostic challenge: an apprentice who fails at Lesser Path magic may be a latent Greater Path talent, but without cross-cultural knowledge, this cannot be identified.

The second cost is **physical and mental exhaustion**. Both paths drain the practitioner. Lesser Path spells require concentration and mental energy proportional to their complexity; Greater Path workings drain physical stamina and willpower. Extended magical exertion can render a practitioner unconscious or worse.

The third limitation is **political constraint**. On Kelewan, the Assembly of Magicians operates above imperial law but is bound by its own internal politics and customs. On Midkemia, magicians serve (or resist) the crown. Magical power does not translate automatically into political freedom — practitioners are embedded in social systems that constrain their actions.

## Practitioner access

Magical talent is innate but requires training to develop. On Midkemia, apprentices are identified by practicing magicians and trained individually or (later) at Stardock. On Kelewan, talented individuals are identified and taken to the Assembly for training as Great Ones, a process that severs all previous social ties — a Great One belongs to the Assembly, not to their family or nation. Access to both traditions simultaneously requires the exceptional circumstance of cross-world exposure.

## Cultural role

Magic occupies different positions on each world. On Midkemia, magicians are respected but subordinate to political authority — they advise kings and serve in wars but do not rule. On Kelewan, the Assembly of Magicians stands above the Empire — Great Ones are outside all law and custom, answerable only to themselves and their Assembly. The Riftwar itself is partially a collision of these two magical cultures, and the series traces how the exchange of magical knowledge between worlds transforms both traditions.
