## Mechanics

The **Subtle Knife** (Æsahættr in the language of the angels) is a unique artifact forged three hundred years before the series' events in the city of Cittàgazze in a parallel world. It has two edges:

**The ordinary edge** cuts through any physical material — stone, metal, armor, flesh — with no resistance. It is the sharpest blade in any universe.

**The subtle edge** cuts through the fabric separating parallel worlds. By feeling for the thin points in reality with extraordinary mental focus, the bearer can use the subtle edge to slice a window — a door-sized opening — between two universes. The window remains open until deliberately closed by the bearer, and the bearer can close windows cut by themselves or by others.

The knife requires a specific **bearer** — someone whose mental state matches the knife's requirements. The bearer must be able to achieve a state of focused attention so absolute that they can feel the point between worlds through the knife's edge. This state is described as similar to meditation: the mind becomes still, finds the tiny gap in reality, and cuts. Distraction, emotional turmoil, or doubt causes the knife to fail — or worse, to break.

The knife is passed from bearer to bearer through a ritualized combat in which the new bearer loses two fingers on their left hand — a sacrifice that marks them physically.

**Windows** between worlds are visible as shimmering rectangles in the air. They remain open until closed. Each window leaks **Dust** (conscious particles) into the Abyss — the void between worlds — and this leakage is cumulatively catastrophic. Every window ever cut by the knife has been slowly draining Dust from all the worlds, contributing to a cosmological crisis.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **Dust leakage**. Every window the knife creates is a permanent wound in the multiverse. The accumulated effect of three centuries of windows is that Dust is hemorrhaging out of all worlds, causing consciousness itself to diminish. The knife's utility as a tool is directly opposed to its cosmological cost — using it to solve immediate problems creates long-term existential damage.

The second cost is **the bearer's sacrifice**. The bearer must lose two fingers, limiting their physical capabilities. The knife also imposes a psychological burden: the bearer carries the responsibility for a reality-cutting weapon and the knowledge that using it damages the universe.

The third cost is **mental vulnerability**. The knife responds to the bearer's mental state. If the bearer thinks about someone they love while cutting, the knife can break — it shatters when Will thinks of his mother at a critical moment. Repairing the broken knife requires extraordinary skill and is itself a morally complex act, since the repaired knife will continue to cause damage.

The fourth limitation is **Spectre generation**. Windows between worlds create **Spectres** — invisible entities that feed on consciousness, specifically on the settled-dæmon-having consciousness of adults. Spectres are a direct consequence of the knife's use, making the knife a generator of its own antagonists.

## Practitioner access

Only one bearer exists at a time. The knife selects its bearer through the combat-succession ritual, and only a person with the specific mental capacity to feel between worlds can use the subtle edge. The knife cannot be wielded by just anyone — it requires the bearer's particular form of focused attention.

## Cultural role

In Cittàgazze (the knife's home world), the knife's legacy is catastrophic — the Spectres it generated have depopulated the city of adults, leaving only children. In the broader multiverse, the knife is barely known. The angels regard it with alarm — it is a mortal-made tool that does things only divine power should be able to do. The knife's ultimate fate — being deliberately broken and its windows sealed — represents the series' thematic argument that some forms of knowledge and power, however useful, must be surrendered because their costs outweigh their benefits.
