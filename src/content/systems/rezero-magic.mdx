## Mechanics

Re:Zero's magical framework operates on three distinct layers that interact but follow different rules. The foundation layer is conventional elemental magic, which draws from six affinities: fire, water, wind, earth, yin, and yang. Practitioners channel mana from the atmosphere through a gate — an internal organ that connects the caster's body to the ambient mana field. Spells are cast by shaping this mana through incantation and will, producing effects that scale with the caster's gate capacity, affinity strength, and technical skill. Most inhabitants of Lugunica can use at least one affinity at a basic level, and trained mages can achieve devastating results within their elemental specializations.

Spirit Arts form the second layer. Rather than manipulating mana directly, a spirit arts practitioner forms a contract with one or more spirits — autonomous mana-based entities that range from minor elemental wisps to Great Spirits of immense power. The contract is a reciprocal bond: the practitioner provides mana and emotional sustenance to the spirit, and the spirit channels its elemental mastery on the practitioner's behalf. The depth and quality of the partnership directly determine the power output. Emilia's contract with the Great Spirit Puck, for instance, grants her access to ice magic on a scale that far exceeds what any solo caster could achieve, but the arrangement also means her combat capacity is partly dependent on Puck's willingness and emotional state. Beatrice, as a Great Spirit herself, represents the inverse case — a spirit who contracted with a human (Subaru) to gain a stable mana source after centuries of draining isolation.

Divine Protections constitute the third conventional power type. These are innate blessings bestowed at birth, granting specific passive or active abilities that do not require mana expenditure or training. Reinhard van Astrea holds an absurd number of Divine Protections, including the Divine Protection of the Sword Saint, making him functionally invincible in combat. Most people with a Divine Protection have only one, and many are minor — protection from getting lost, resistance to a specific ailment, enhanced night vision. The distribution appears random from the human perspective, though Od Laguna (the world's mana repository and metaphysical foundation) is implied to be the source.

Authorities operate on an entirely separate plane. Each Authority derives from a Witch Factor — a fragment of power associated with one of the seven deadly sins, originally wielded by the Witches of Sin four centuries ago. When a Witch Factor finds a compatible host, it grants an Authority: a reality-warping ability that bypasses conventional magical rules. Subaru's Authority of Sloth (later evolving) grants Return by Death, the ability to rewind time upon dying. Regulus Corneas's Authority of Greed allows him to stop his own time, making him functionally invulnerable and unstoppable. Petelgeuse Romanee-Conti's Authority of Sloth manifests as Unseen Hands — invisible appendages that can crush targets from a distance.

Authorities do not consume mana in the conventional sense. They draw from the Witch Factor itself, which operates on the holder's soul and psyche rather than their gate. This makes them fundamentally unpredictable — the same Witch Factor can manifest differently depending on the host, and the precise rules of each Authority often reveal themselves only through traumatic use.

## Limitations and costs

Conventional magic is gated by the practitioner's internal organ — the gate — which functions as both a channel and a bottleneck. Overuse damages the gate, potentially permanently. Subaru's gate was damaged early in the story from overuse, severely limiting his ability to cast conventional magic for the remainder of the narrative. Gates have a natural capacity ceiling, and pushing beyond it risks physical collapse, hemorrhaging, or death. Mana depletion without gate damage still produces extreme fatigue and vulnerability.

Spirit contracts impose reciprocal obligations that can become liabilities. A spirit that is emotionally dissatisfied, insufficiently fed with mana, or placed in conditions that violate the contract's terms may withdraw its cooperation or break the contract entirely. When Puck terminated his contract with Emilia, she lost access to the full scope of her ice magic until she developed the ability to channel Great Spirits independently. The emotional dimension of spirit contracts means that a practitioner's psychological state directly affects their combat readiness.

Authorities carry the heaviest costs in the system, and those costs are fundamentally psychological and spiritual. Return by Death resets Subaru's timeline but preserves his memories of every death — the accumulated trauma of dying repeatedly is a central driver of his psychological deterioration across the story. The Witch of Envy enforces a restriction that prevents Subaru from telling anyone about Return by Death; attempts to do so result in his heart being crushed or the listener being harmed. Other Authorities carry their own warping effects: the Authority of Greed hollowed out Regulus's capacity for genuine human connection, and the Authority of Sloth drove Petelgeuse into centuries of escalating madness.

The Witch Factors themselves appear to select hosts based on compatibility with the associated sin, meaning that the people most likely to receive an Authority are those already psychologically predisposed to its corruptive influence. The system is, in this sense, self-reinforcing: it empowers the damaged and damages them further through empowerment.

## Practitioner access

Conventional magic is accessible to most of Lugunica's population at a rudimentary level. Elemental affinity is determined at birth, and most people have one or two affinities they can develop through training. Professional mages — court magicians, combat casters, healers — represent a trained specialist class that has invested years in developing gate capacity and spell repertoire. Roswaal L Mathers stands at the extreme end of this spectrum, possessing all six affinities at high proficiency, a level of versatility that is historically unprecedented and partly the result of centuries of soul-transferring body possession.

Spirit Arts practitioners are rarer because they require both compatible temperament and the availability of willing spirits. Not every mage can form a spirit contract — the spirit must choose the partner as much as the partner chooses the spirit. Half-elves like Emilia have a natural affinity for spirit communication, but this advantage is offset by the intense social prejudice they face, which limits their access to institutional magical training.

Divine Protections are entirely innate and cannot be acquired through effort. Their distribution is uneven to the point of absurdity — Reinhard possesses dozens while most recipients have one or none. There is no known method for transferring, replicating, or artificially granting a Divine Protection.

Authority holders number fewer than a dozen at any given time in the story. Each Witch Factor is singular, and it passes to a new host only when the current host dies or is otherwise separated from it. The selection criteria are opaque, tied to the psychological resonance between the host and the sin the Factor embodies. Subaru received the Witch Factor of Sloth after defeating Petelgeuse, but the transfer was not guaranteed — it occurred because something in Subaru's soul was compatible with the Factor's nature.

## Cultural role

Magic in Lugunica is openly practiced and institutionally supported. The Royal Knights include combat mages, the Sage Council employs diviners and scholars, and magical talent is a recognized path to social advancement. The six-affinity framework provides a common vocabulary for discussing magical capability, and gate assessment is a standard part of military and academic evaluation.

The Witch Cult, built around the collection and exploitation of Witch Factors, represents the system's shadow institution. Where mainstream magical society treats power as a public resource to be trained and deployed for collective benefit, the Witch Cult treats Authorities as instruments of devotion to the Witch of Envy. The Sin Archbishops — each bearing a Witch Factor — function as both religious figures and weapons of mass destruction, and their periodic attacks on civilized areas are treated as natural disasters rather than military conflicts because conventional magical defenses are largely ineffective against Authorities.

The historical memory of the Witch of Envy — who consumed half the world four centuries ago using power derived from the Witch Factors — hangs over all magical practice. The prejudice against half-elves stems directly from the Witch's heritage, and the fear of Authorities specifically drives much of the kingdom's political caution. Magic is welcome; the power that lies beyond magic is feared with justification.
