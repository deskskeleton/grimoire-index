## Mechanics

Cytonic abilities are innate psychic powers that operate through interaction with the **nowhere** — a dimension that exists between normal space, functioning as both a medium for faster-than-light phenomena and a habitat for ancient entities. Cytonics can perform several categories of action, all rooted in their ability to reach into or through the nowhere.

**Cytonic sensing** is the most basic ability. A cytonic can detect other cytonics across vast distances, perceiving them as presences or signals in the nowhere. This sense operates passively and is often the first ability to manifest, sometimes before the cytonic is even aware of their nature. Spensa initially experiences this as hearing the "stars" — faint communications bleeding through from the nowhere.

**Hyperjump navigation** is the most strategically significant cytonic ability. Faster-than-light travel in the Skyward universe requires a cytonic to open a portal through the nowhere, translating a ship (or fleet) from one point in normal space to another. Without a cytonic navigator, FTL travel is impossible, which is why the Superiority jealously controls and suppresses cytonics — they represent both the key to interstellar travel and a potential existential threat. The Superiority uses captive or compliant cytonics to maintain their FTL monopoly.

**Nowhere interaction** encompasses the more advanced and dangerous abilities. Experienced cytonics can project themselves into the nowhere, communicate with entities there, create **mindblades** (psychic weapons), and manipulate the boundary between dimensions. Spensa eventually learns to pull objects and beings into and out of the nowhere, effectively using it as a transit system and a tactical tool. The nowhere is not empty — it is inhabited by the **delvers**, vast and ancient beings that exist in a state of perpetual solitude and react with catastrophic violence when disturbed by cytonic activity.

**Long-range communication** allows cytonics to send and receive messages across interstellar distances instantaneously, bypassing the light-speed limitation that constrains all other forms of communication. This makes cytonics invaluable as military and diplomatic assets.

## Limitations and costs

The most significant cost is the **delver threat**. Cytonic activity in the nowhere attracts the attention of the delvers, and sufficiently intense or prolonged activity can draw a delver into normal space, where it manifests as a world-destroying force. This creates a fundamental tension: the more cytonics use their abilities, the greater the risk of triggering a delver incursion that could annihilate entire planets. The Superiority's fear of cytonics is not irrational — it is based on historical catastrophes caused by uncontrolled cytonic activity.

**Concentration and mental strain** constrain immediate use. Hyperjumping requires intense focus, and navigating through the nowhere is psychologically harrowing. The nowhere is a disorienting, hostile environment that tests the cytonic's sense of identity and will. Extended time in the nowhere can erode a cytonic's connection to normal reality, and the temptation to simply remain — to lose oneself in the vast emptiness — is a recurring danger.

**Rarity** is itself a limitation. Cytonics are extremely uncommon across all known species. This means that most civilizations cannot develop cytonic capabilities in any systematic way — they depend on the chance birth of individuals with the right innate potential. There is no training program that can create a cytonic from a non-cytonic person; instruction can only help existing cytonics refine abilities they already possess.

## Practitioner access

Cytonic ability is innate and appears to have a genetic component, though the inheritance pattern is not fully understood. Among humans on Detritus, cytonic ability runs in certain family lines but does not manifest in every generation. Spensa inherited her abilities from her father, who was himself cytonic (and whose cytonic sensitivity to the delvers contributed to his disgrace and death). Jorgen Weight manifests cytonic abilities later in the series, suggesting that the potential can lie dormant.

Across the galaxy, cytonics appear in multiple species, but always as rare individuals rather than as a common trait. The Superiority actively identifies, controls, and in some cases eliminates cytonics to maintain their political order, making open practice dangerous. On Detritus, cytonic ability is poorly understood and carries the stigma of Spensa's father's perceived cowardice and treason.

## Cultural role

Cytonics occupy a paradoxical cultural position: they are simultaneously the most valuable and the most feared individuals in the galaxy. The Superiority's entire interstellar order depends on cytonic navigators for FTL travel, yet the same government suppresses cytonic development and portrays cytonics as existential threats. This mirrors real-world dynamics where those who hold essential capabilities are simultaneously depended upon and persecuted. For humans on Detritus, cytonic ability is poorly understood and entangled with the trauma of their exile — Spensa must reclaim her father's legacy and redefine what cytonic power means for a species fighting for survival.
