## Mechanics

A **dragonmark** is an arcane sigil — a complex, tattoo-like pattern of lines and whorls — that appears spontaneously on the skin of individuals within specific bloodlines. Each mark grants innate spell-like abilities tied to a thematic domain. The thirteen recognized marks are:

- **Mark of Detection** (House Medani) — divination and danger-sensing
- **Mark of Finding** (House Tharashk) — locating objects, creatures, and resources
- **Mark of Handling** (House Vadalis) — animal empathy, bonding, and breeding
- **Mark of Healing** (House Jorasco) — curative and restorative magic
- **Mark of Hospitality** (House Ghallanda) — comfort, sustenance, and shelter
- **Mark of Making** (House Cannith) — crafting, repair, and creation
- **Mark of Passage** (House Orien) — teleportation and speed
- **Mark of Scribing** (House Sivis) — communication, translation, and record-keeping
- **Mark of Sentinel** (House Deneith) — protection and bodyguard powers
- **Mark of Shadow** (House Phiarlan / House Thuranni) — illusion and stealth
- **Mark of Storm** (House Lyrandar) — weather and wind control
- **Mark of Warding** (House Kundarak) — magical locks, wards, and protection

Each mark manifests in three tiers: **Least** (minor abilities), **Lesser** (moderate abilities), and **Greater** (powerful abilities). The rarest and most powerful manifestation is the **Siberys dragonmark** — an enormous mark that covers much of the body and grants abilities far beyond the normal progression.

Dragonmarks are also essential components in **dragonmark focus items** — magical items designed to be activated only by a specific mark. This creates an industrial ecosystem: House Orien's lightning rail (magical train) requires Mark of Passage operators; House Lyrandar's airships require Mark of Storm pilots; House Cannith's creation forges require Mark of Making artificers.

**Aberrant dragonmarks** are mutations — marks that appear outside the recognized bloodlines, producing unpredictable and often dangerous powers (fire, disease, pain). Aberrant marks are stigmatized and feared, and their bearers face social persecution.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **bloodline dependency and social obligation**. Dragonmarks appear only in specific races and bloodlines, and the dragonmarked Houses exert enormous social pressure on their marked members. A mark-bearer is expected to serve their House's interests — the mark is not a personal gift but a corporate asset.

The second limitation is **domain restriction**. Each mark grants abilities within a narrow domain. A Mark of Healing cannot ward; a Mark of Warding cannot heal. This specialization is the foundation of the Houses' economic monopolies but limits individual versatility.

The third limitation is **rarity of advancement**. Most mark-bearers manifest only the Least dragonmark — Lesser and Greater marks are progressively rarer, and Siberys marks are once-in-a-generation phenomena.

## Practitioner access

Dragonmarks are strictly hereditary — they appear only in members of the thirteen marked bloodlines (or as aberrant mutations in others). The Houses carefully manage breeding to maximize mark manifestation, creating a quasi-eugenic aristocracy. Not every member of a marked bloodline develops a mark; manifestation rates vary. Unmarked family members occupy lower social positions within the Houses.

## Cultural role

The dragonmarked Houses are Eberron's most powerful economic and political institutions — not governments, but trans-national guilds whose services are essential to civilization. The Houses control banking (Kundarak), communication (Sivis), transportation (Orien and Lyrandar), manufacturing (Cannith), healthcare (Jorasco), and mercenary services (Deneith). The Treaty of Thronehold (which ended the Last War) granted the Houses political neutrality and economic privileges. The marks are not adventuring powers; they are the infrastructure of a magitech civilization.
