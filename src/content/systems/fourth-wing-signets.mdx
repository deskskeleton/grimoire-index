## Mechanics

The dragon signet system begins with the Threshing — a harrowing trial at Basgiath War College where unbonded dragons evaluate first-year cadets and choose whether to bond with them, ignore them, or kill them. A dragon's choice is final and instinctive; there is no negotiation or appeal. Once bonded, the rider gains access to a signet, a unique magical ability that manifests over time as the bond deepens. The signet is not chosen by the rider but emerges from the combination of the dragon's inherent nature and the rider's psychological makeup, making each ability a reflection of the specific pairing.

Signets vary enormously in type and power. Some riders develop elemental abilities like lightning manipulation or fire projection. Others gain cognitive powers such as mind-reading, distance-sight, or the ability to perceive memories through touch. Still others receive physical enhancements like superhuman strength or the ability to become invisible. The range of possible signets is large enough that no comprehensive taxonomy exists within the story, and the discovery of one's signet is treated as a defining moment in a rider's development.

Violet Sorrengail's situation is exceptional: she bonds two dragons — Tairn and Andarna — and develops two signets as a result. This dual bond is treated as unprecedented and marks her as a figure of enormous strategic significance. Dragons themselves possess independent magical capabilities, most notably the ability to breathe fire, and they channel additional power through their riders during combat. The bond is bidirectional — riders draw on their dragon's strength, and dragons benefit from their rider's tactical intelligence.

The bond is permanent and carries a lethal covenant. If a dragon dies, the rider typically dies from the psychic severance. If a rider dies, the dragon may survive but is often devastated, and some choose not to bond again.

## Limitations and costs

Signet use draws on the rider's physical reserves, channeled through the dragon bond. Overuse produces exhaustion, nosebleeds, loss of consciousness, and — in extreme cases — death. The dragon acts as both a power source and a regulator; using a signet without the dragon's active support is possible but far more taxing and dangerous. This creates a tactical dependency: a rider separated from their dragon is dramatically weaker.

The bond itself is the system's most significant cost. It is not optional once formed, and it ties the rider's survival to their dragon's. This makes every combat engagement a doubled risk — losing a fight means potentially losing both partners. The emotional weight of the bond also creates vulnerability; riders whose dragons are threatened may act irrationally, and the grief of severance is described as unsurvivable for many.

There is no path to magical power without the bond. Cadets who are not chosen during the Threshing wash out of the riders' quadrant entirely, and those whose dragons die lose their signets along with their partner. The system offers no second chances and no alternative routes to the same abilities.

## Practitioner access

Access is gated by two filters: admission to Basgiath War College and survival of the Threshing. Admission is nominally meritocratic but practically shaped by military lineage and political connections. The Threshing itself is entirely in the dragons' control — they choose based on criteria that are never fully explained to humans, and their judgment is absolute. Many cadets die during the process, making the entry cost potentially lethal.

Once bonded, riders enter a rigorous training program where signet development is cultivated but not directed. Instructors can help a rider learn to control an emerging signet, but they cannot influence what signet manifests. This creates a military structure where the most powerful weapons in the arsenal are fundamentally unpredictable until they reveal themselves.

## Cultural role

In Navarre, dragon riders are the apex of the military hierarchy and, by extension, the social order. The riders' quadrant at Basgiath produces the kingdom's most powerful soldiers, and rider families carry enormous political weight. The bond between rider and dragon is simultaneously a military asset, a social credential, and a deeply personal relationship, and the tension between these roles drives much of the series' conflict.

The secrecy surrounding what lies beyond Navarre's wards creates a second layer of cultural significance. Riders are told they are defending the kingdom, but the true nature of the external threat — and the role signets play in maintaining the wards — is concealed from most of the military. This makes the signet system not just a source of power but a tool of institutional control, where information about magic's true purpose is restricted to those at the top of the command structure.
