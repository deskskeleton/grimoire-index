## Mechanics

**Wraiths** are the ghosts of the dead — individuals whose unfinished business, unresolved passions, or traumatic deaths prevent them from passing on. They exist in the **Shadowlands**, a dark mirror of the living world, and their supernatural abilities are called **Arcanoi** (singular: Arcanos).

Common Arcanoi include: **Embody** (becoming partially visible or tangible in the living world), **Inhabit** (possessing objects and machinery), **Puppetry** (possessing living beings), **Phantasm** (creating illusions and entering dreams), **Keening** (emotional manipulation through supernatural sound), **Argos** (navigating the Tempest — the chaotic storm-realm between Shadowlands and deeper Underworld), **Moliate** (reshaping wraith corpus — the ghost's spiritual body), **Outrage** (poltergeist-style telekinesis), **Castigate** (interacting with other wraiths' Shadows), and **Usury** (manipulating Pathos — the emotional energy wraiths use as currency).

The fuel for Arcanoi is **Pathos** — emotional energy drawn from a wraith's **Fetters** (objects, places, or people in the living world that the wraith is emotionally tied to) and from mortal emotional states. Wraiths who can evoke strong emotions in the living — through hauntings, dream visitations, or subtle environmental manipulation — replenish their Pathos.

The central antagonistic mechanic is the **Shadow** — every wraith has a dark half, a personification of self-destruction, despair, and the pull of Oblivion (total annihilation). The Shadow is played by another player at the table. It whispers, tempts, and offers power in exchange for self-destructive behavior. The Shadow has its own pool of **Angst** and its own abilities (**Dark Arcanoi** and **Thorns**) that it can deploy when the wraith is vulnerable. If the Shadow ever gains full control, the wraith becomes a Spectre — a creature of Oblivion that actively works to destroy other wraiths and the living world.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **Pathos dependency**. Arcanoi cost Pathos, and Pathos is finite. Replenishing it requires interacting with Fetters or harvesting mortal emotions — activities that are difficult, risky, and tether the wraith to the living world they can no longer fully participate in.

The second cost is **Shadow interference**. Every time the wraith acts, the Shadow can attempt to subvert, corrupt, or co-opt the action. Using Arcanoi recklessly or in emotional extremity gives the Shadow opportunities to accumulate Angst and seize control. The wraith's internal battle against self-destruction is the game's primary tension.

The third cost is **Fetter vulnerability**. Wraiths are emotionally and supernaturally bound to their Fetters. If a Fetter is destroyed — a house demolished, a loved one dying, a keepsake lost — the wraith loses a connection to existence. Losing all Fetters begins the slide into the Tempest and eventually Oblivion.

The fourth cost is **soulforging**. The Underworld's economy runs on **soulsteel** — objects forged from the corpus of wraiths. Wraiths who fall into servitude, lose political power, or are captured can be literally melted down and shaped into tools, weapons, or building materials. The awareness of the forged wraith may persist within the object. This is the setting's ultimate horror: not death, but being used as raw material.

## Practitioner access

All wraiths have access to Arcanoi. The specific Arcanoi available are influenced by the wraith's Guild (a professional/social organization in Stygia, the wraith city) and their personal affinities. Learning Arcanoi requires training from experienced wraiths or Guilds. Some Arcanoi (particularly Puppetry and dark applications of other powers) are culturally taboo or politically restricted by Stygia's ruling hierarchy.

## Cultural role

Wraith society is centered on **Stygia** — a vast necropolis ruled by Charon (until his disappearance) and governed by a bureaucratic hierarchy of Anacreons, Marshals, and Legions. The Guilds (organizations of wraiths specializing in particular Arcanoi) were officially banned by Charon but operate underground. Wraith politics are characterized by rigid hierarchy, desperate resource scarcity (Pathos and Fetters), and the constant existential threat of Oblivion. The Arcanoi are not just personal powers but political currency — a wraith's usefulness to the hierarchy is determined by what they can do.
