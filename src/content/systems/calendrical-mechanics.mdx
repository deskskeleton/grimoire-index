## Mechanics

Calendrical mechanics is the foundational principle of the hexarchate: the laws of physics in this universe are partially determined by consensus. When a sufficient population observes the same calendar — performing the same remembrances on the same days, maintaining the same mathematical conventions, structuring their lives around the same ritual framework — "exotic effects" become possible. These effects include weapons that reshape local geometry, shields that bend space, communications that bypass normal physical limits, and formation-based combat techniques that alter probability.

The system operates through **formations**: precise geometric arrangements of soldiers, ships, or other units whose spatial relationships, combined with calendrical observance, produce specific exotic effects. A Kel banner (military unit) arranged in a particular formation can generate shields, amplify weapons, or produce tactical advantages that have no analogue in non-calendrical physics. The formation instinct — a psychological conditioning built into Kel soldiers that compels obedience to their commanding officer — is both a military tool and a calendrical necessity, ensuring that formations hold under combat stress.

**Remembrances** are the ritual component. The hexarchate mandates specific observances — some of which involve mass torture and execution — on calendrically significant days. These are not merely political theatre; they are mathematically necessary to sustain the exotic effects on which the hexarchate's technology depends. The horror of the system is that its atrocities are structurally required. A gentler calendar might be possible, but it would produce different exotic effects, potentially rendering the hexarchate's existing military infrastructure useless.

**Calendrical heresy** — the adoption of an alternative mathematical-ritual framework — is the hexarchate's greatest existential threat, not because heretics are morally dangerous but because their alternative mathematics literally destabilize the exotic effects in the surrounding region. A heretical zone is a place where the hexarchate's weapons do not work as expected, where shields fail and formations produce unpredictable results. This is why heresy is met with overwhelming military force: it is not a difference of opinion but a localised rewriting of physics.

## Limitations and costs

The most fundamental limitation is **dependence on consensus**. Calendrical mechanics only functions in regions where the population observes the high calendar. In areas of calendrical heresy or outside hexarchate-controlled space, exotic effects become unreliable or impossible. This means the hexarchate's military power is geographically bounded by its cultural reach — it cannot project force into regions that do not share its mathematical framework.

The **human cost** is staggering. Maintaining the calendar requires remembrances that include ritualised atrocity — the hexarchate tortures and kills people on schedule because the mathematics demand it. The Vidona faction exists specifically to carry out these observances. The system creates a civilisation that must commit regular acts of cruelty to keep its technology functioning, trapping its rulers in a cycle where moral reform would mean technological collapse.

Formation effects require **trained practitioners** in precise arrangements. If a formation breaks — through casualties, loss of discipline, or interference — the exotic effect collapses. The Kel formation instinct mitigates this by making soldiers psychologically incapable of breaking formation without orders, but this same conditioning makes them vulnerable to manipulation by anyone with sufficient command authority.

## Practitioner access

Calendrical mechanics is not practised by individuals in isolation — it is a civilisation-scale phenomenon. However, specific roles within the hexarchate interact with it differently. The Nirai faction develops new formations and calendrical mathematics. The Kel implement formations in combat. The Rahal maintain calendrical orthodoxy through law. The Shuos manage the strategic implications. Hexarchs like Nirai Kujen understand the underlying mathematics deeply enough to manipulate the system at a fundamental level, including the creation of immortality technologies that depend on calendrical principles.

Individual talent matters most in formation design and tactical deployment. Shuos Jedao's legendary military genius lies partly in his ability to see unconventional formation applications, and Kel Cheris's mathematical background allows her to understand calendrical mechanics at a level most Kel soldiers never reach.

## Cultural role

The calendar is not merely a source of power in the hexarchate — it is the organising principle of civilisation itself. Every aspect of society is structured around calendrical observance: art, education, governance, warfare, and daily life all conform to the high calendar's demands. Heresy is the worst crime not because it offends a deity but because it threatens the physical infrastructure of the state. The hexarchate's political structure — six factions, each with a defined role — exists to maintain and exploit the calendar. Lee uses this to explore how authoritarian systems make their own cruelties seem necessary, how ideology becomes infrastructure, and how resistance must contend not just with power but with the physics that power has built around itself.
