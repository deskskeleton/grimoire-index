## Mechanics

A **Sin-Eater** is a mortal who died and, at the moment of death, was offered a bargain by a **geist** — a powerful ghost-like entity that is itself an amalgam of death-resonance, ghostly remains, and archetypal energy. The Sin-Eater accepts the bargain, returns to life, and is permanently bound to the geist. This bond grants supernatural powers organized into a two-axis system: **Manifestations** and **Keys**.

**Manifestations** are modes of channeling the geist's power — how the magic is expressed:

- **The Boneyard** — area-of-effect perception and control; the Sin-Eater extends awareness through a zone, sensing death-related phenomena and manipulating the environment
- **The Caul** — the Sin-Eater overlays the geist onto their own body, gaining physical enhancements and death-themed transformations
- **The Curse** — projecting afflictions, debilitations, and supernatural penalties onto targets
- **The Marionette** — controlling or possessing corpses, objects, or (at high levels) living beings
- **The Rage** — destructive, offensive applications of death-energy
- **The Shroud** — concealment, invisibility, and protection through death-resonance

**Keys** are thematic domains — what the magic draws upon:

- **Stillness Key** — cold, silence, cessation
- **Passion Key** — emotion, desire, obsession
- **Phantasmal Key** — illusion, dreams, fear
- **Industrial Key** — technology, machines, the modern world
- **Pyre-Flame Key** — fire, purification, destruction
- **Grave-Dirt Key** — earth, decay, the physical remains of death
- **Tear-Stained Key** — water, grief, memory
- **Cold Wind Key** — air, isolation, haunting

Combining a Manifestation with a Key produces a specific effect. The Rage with the Pyre-Flame Key produces destructive fire; the Shroud with the Stillness Key produces cold, silent invisibility; the Boneyard with the Phantasmal Key creates an area of terrifying hallucinations. This matrix produces significant mechanical variety from a manageable number of components.

The fuel is **Plasm** — ectoplasmic energy associated with death, harvested from ghosts, deathly places, and the act of dying. Sin-Eaters can also channel Plasm through their **Keystone** — the physical object or site that anchors their geist to the world.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **Plasm expenditure**. Manifestations cost Plasm, which must be harvested from death-adjacent sources. Sin-Eaters who deplete their Plasm become vulnerable and lose access to their powers until they replenish.

The second cost is **Synergy** — the Sin-Eater's measure of harmony with their geist. Low Synergy causes the geist to act independently, potentially sabotaging the Sin-Eater's goals or manifesting in uncontrolled ways. High Synergy requires treating the geist as a partner rather than a tool — respecting its desires, fulfilling its obsessions, and maintaining the bargain's terms.

The third cost is **death proximity**. Sin-Eaters are drawn to death — they see ghosts, feel death-resonance, and are compelled to interact with the dead. This creates social isolation and exposure to traumatic situations. The Underworld — a vast, dangerous realm of the dead — is accessible to Sin-Eaters but extremely hazardous.

## Practitioner access

Only those who have died and accepted a geist's bargain become Sin-Eaters. The process is involuntary from the death side (you have to actually die) and voluntary from the bargain side (the geist offers, the dying mortal accepts). Sin-Eaters organize into **Krewes** — small, tight-knit groups bound by supernatural oaths — that provide community, mutual support, and shared purpose.

## Cultural role

Sin-Eaters occupy a liminal space between the living and the dead. They form Krewes for mutual protection and shared mission — typically some combination of laying ghosts to rest, exploring the Underworld, protecting the living from ghostly threats, and celebrating life with the intensity that only someone who has died can muster. The game's tone is notably more optimistic than other WoD lines: Sin-Eaters have been given a second chance and many of them are determined to make it count.
