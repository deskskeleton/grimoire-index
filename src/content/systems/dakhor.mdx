## Mechanics

Dakhor is the magic system of the Fjordell Empire on the Cosmere world of Sel. Like AonDor, it draws on the **Dor** — the ambient Investiture saturating Sel — but accesses it through the practitioner's own **skeleton** rather than through drawn symbols. Dakhor monks undergo a series of rituals that permanently restructure their bones into angular, twisted configurations. Each bone-pattern channels a specific aspect of the Dor, granting the monk a corresponding supernatural ability.

The most common manifestations are extreme **physical enhancement**: superhuman speed, strength, durability, and reflexes. Some monks can shatter stone with a touch, move faster than the eye can follow, or withstand blows that would kill an ordinary person. More advanced Dakhor abilities include a form of **teleportation** and the capacity to negate AonDor effects — the system includes a built-in counter to its Arelene rival.

The bone-reshaping is visible. A Dakhor monk's skeleton bulges and twists beneath the skin, producing a distinctive, unsettling appearance that marks them immediately as practitioners.

## Limitations and costs

Dakhor's primary cost is **permanent bodily deformation**. The bone-reshaping rituals are irreversible, painful, and fundamentally alter the monk's body. There is no undoing a Dakhor transformation; the practitioner is changed for life.

The second cost is **sacrificial ritual**. The bone-reshaping process requires human sacrifice — living people are consumed in the ritual that transforms a monk's skeleton. This cost is hidden from the Fjordell public but is structurally essential to the system. The more powerful the ability granted, the greater the sacrificial requirement.

Like all Selish magic, Dakhor is **geography-dependent**: it weakens with distance from Fjordell, though Dakhor appears to be somewhat more portable than AonDor, possibly because the channeling medium (the practitioner's bones) travels with them.

The monks' institutional structure imposes its own constraint: Dakhor is controlled exclusively by the Fjordell religious hierarchy, and the rituals are closely guarded secrets. Independent practice is effectively impossible.

## Practitioner access

Dakhor monks are recruited (or conscripted) into Fjordell monasteries, where they undergo the bone-reshaping rituals. The process is not voluntary in any meaningful sense for many recruits. There is no indication that Dakhor requires a natural selection event like the Shaod; rather, the rituals impose the transformation on whoever is subjected to them. This makes Dakhor more accessible in principle than AonDor — anyone can be made into a Dakhor monk — but the institutional gatekeeping and the cost in human lives make it rare in practice.

## Cultural role

Dakhor is the hidden military arm of the Fjordell theocracy. The Fjordell Empire's public religion, Shu-Dereth, preaches unity and obedience, and its expansion across Sel is presented as a religious mission. Dakhor monks are the enforcers behind this mission — deployed when persuasion, political pressure, and conventional military force are insufficient. Their existence is known but their methods (particularly the human sacrifice) are concealed from the general population. In *Elantris*, the Dakhor monks serve as the primary physical antagonists, and the revelation of their sacrificial requirements is one of the novel's moral turning points.
