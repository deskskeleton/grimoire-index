## Mechanics

**Blood Demon Arts** (血鬼術, *Kekkijutsu*) are supernatural abilities unique to each demon in *Demon Slayer*. Every demon that has consumed enough humans and accumulated sufficient power develops a personal Blood Demon Art — a unique ability that reflects their individual nature, memories, or psychological fixations from their human life.

All demons share a baseline set of abilities: superhuman strength, speed, and durability; regeneration (ranging from healing minor wounds in seconds to regrowing entire limbs or heads); immortality (demons do not age); and the ability to grow stronger by consuming humans. Blood Demon Arts represent the evolution beyond these baseline abilities — the point where a demon's power becomes truly individualized.

Examples of Blood Demon Arts include: **Rui's thread manipulation** (spider-silk threads strong enough to cut Nichirin blades), **Doma's ice manipulation** (cryogenic blood that freezes anything it touches), **Gyutaro's blood sickles** (poisonous blood weaponized into flying blades), **Kokushibo's Moon Breathing** (a corrupted Breathing Style enhanced with crescent-shaped blood blades), and **Muzan's biokinesis** (complete control over his own cellular structure and the ability to transform others into demons).

**Muzan Kibutsuji**, the progenitor demon, distributes his blood to create new demons. The amount of blood determines the new demon's starting power. The **Twelve Kizuki** (Demon Moons) are Muzan's most powerful subordinates, ranked Upper Moon One through Six and Lower Moon One through Six. Upper Moons are catastrophically powerful — each has killed multiple Hashira (the strongest demon slayers) — and their Blood Demon Arts operate at a scale that requires multiple demon slayers working in coordination to counter.

**Nezuko Kamado** represents a unique case: a demon who retains her human personality and develops a Blood Demon Art (blood-ignition fire) without consuming humans, powered instead by sleep. Her existence challenges the setting's fundamental assumption that demons are irredeemably monstrous.

## Limitations and costs

The primary limitation is **sunlight vulnerability**. All demons (except Nezuko, eventually) are instantly destroyed by sunlight. This confines demonic activity to nighttime and creates the strategic dynamic of demon slayers trying to survive until dawn.

The second limitation is **Nichirin blade vulnerability**. Demons can only be permanently killed by decapitation with a Nichirin sword (a blade forged from sun-absorbing ore) or by sunlight. All other damage regenerates. This makes demon slayers the only humans who can permanently eliminate demons.

The third cost is **Muzan's control**. All demons are ultimately subordinate to Muzan, who can read their thoughts, impose commands, and destroy them at will through the blood connection. Demons have no true autonomy — their power comes from Muzan's blood, and he can revoke it. This creates a hierarchy where even the most powerful Upper Moons serve at Muzan's pleasure.

## Practitioner access

Demons are created exclusively by Muzan Kibutsuji's blood. A human who receives Muzan's blood transforms into a demon (unless they die from the transformation). The amount of blood determines starting power, but a demon's ultimate strength depends on how many humans they consume over time. Blood Demon Arts develop organically as the demon grows stronger — there is no training or study, only accumulation through consumption.

## Cultural role

Demons exist as the existential threat that the Demon Slayer Corps was created to fight. In the broader human world, demons are the stuff of folklore and nightmares — most people do not believe they are real, and the Demon Slayer Corps operates without government recognition or support. The Blood Demon Arts represent the monstrous inversion of human Breathing Styles: where demon slayers achieve power through discipline and self-sacrifice, demons achieve power through consumption and the abandonment of their humanity.
