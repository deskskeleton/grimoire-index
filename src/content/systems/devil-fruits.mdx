## Mechanics

The Devil Fruits are a finite, named set of supernatural fruits scattered across the world, each conferring a single permanent power on whoever eats it first. A Devil Fruit can be eaten only once — the act of eating destroys the fruit and takes the power with it. The eater retains the power for life. When a Devil Fruit user dies, the same fruit reappears somewhere else in the world, available to be eaten again.

Devil Fruits are sorted into three taxonomic families:

- **Paramecia** — the largest family. Paramecia fruits grant idiosyncratic powers that do not fit cleanly into the other categories: rubber-body (Luffy's original Gomu Gomu no Mi, later revealed to be a Mythical Zoan), bomb-touch (Mr. 5's Bomu Bomu no Mi), gravity manipulation (Issho's Zushi Zushi no Mi), control of operations within a domed "Room" (Law's Ope Ope no Mi), love-petrification (Hancock's Mero Mero no Mi), and many more.

- **Zoan** — fruits that turn the user into a specific animal, or a half-human/half-animal hybrid form, with three canonical sub-categories: ordinary Zoan (mundane animal forms), Ancient Zoan (extinct creatures), and Mythical Zoan (legendary beings, the rarest and typically strongest sub-class).

- **Logia** — fruits that turn the user's body into a particular element. Logia users can become and reform from their element at will (smoke, fire, sand, light, magma, ice, darkness, etc.) and are functionally immune to ordinary physical attacks. Logia are usually the most personally powerful family, but every Logia user has specific elemental counters and is vulnerable to Haki.

Two universal weaknesses apply to every Devil Fruit user, regardless of family:

The first is the loss of swimming. Every fruit eater becomes a "hammer" in seawater — unable to swim, sapped of strength even by submersion in the sea. This is the system's signature drawback and is treated with absolute consistency in the franchise.

The second is sea-prism stone. Objects made of sea-prism stone (sourced from the deep ocean) emit a force that nullifies a Devil Fruit user's powers and saps their strength on contact. Marine handcuffs, prison cells, and weapon coatings made of sea-prism stone are the standard tactical neutralization.

A separate parallel discipline, **Haki**, is available to anyone in the world (Devil Fruit user or not) and divides into three forms: Observation Haki (precognitive sensing), Armament Haki (a damage-bypassing physical hardening that can hurt Logia in their elemental forms), and the rare Conqueror's Haki (a willpower-projecting effect that incapacitates lesser-willed targets and, at the highest tier, can clash with itself in displays of pure spiritual force). Haki is the principal counter to Devil Fruit power and is the standard high-tier discipline of the New World.

## Limitations and costs

The signature cost is the swimming disability. In a world that takes place almost entirely on or near the ocean, this is constant. Devil Fruit users routinely have to be rescued by their non-eater crewmates after falling overboard.

The sea-prism stone vulnerability is the second universal cost. Prison cells, restraint devices, and certain weapons can render a Devil Fruit user functionally helpless.

The one-fruit-per-person rule is a permanent ceiling. Eating a second Devil Fruit canonically kills the eater, with the single explicit exception of Blackbeard, who acquires a second fruit through means that are still partially obscured in fiction.

Within each family, fruits have specific counters. Logia users in their elemental form can be hit by Armament Haki, by sea-prism stone, or by another Logia of an antagonistic element (Akainu's magma vs. Aokiji's ice; Ace's fire vs. Akainu's magma). Zoan users in transformed states are still flesh-and-blood and can be hurt by ordinary attacks if those attacks land. Paramecia powers each have their own bespoke vulnerabilities.

A subtler cost is mastery. A Devil Fruit user does not automatically operate their power at full capacity; mastering its applications takes years of training and creative exploration. Many of the series' tournament-arc fights turn on a less-experienced user finding new uses for their fruit mid-combat.

## Practitioner access

Eating a Devil Fruit is open to anyone who can find one — they are real, scattered, and tradeable. The rare ones change hands in the world's black-market economy at extraordinary prices. There is no innate or hereditary gating; the act of eating produces the bond. Once eaten, the bond is permanent and exclusive.

## Cultural role

Devil Fruits are widely known across the One Piece world, openly studied (the Marines maintain catalog data on identified fruits), and feared. Most ordinary people will go a lifetime without meeting an eater; in the upper reaches of pirate, Marine, and Revolutionary leadership, Devil Fruit users are concentrated — most of the world's named major powers eat them. The fruits' rarity and the visible costs of using them shape the world's geopolitics: navy admirals, Yonko, Shichibukai, and the Revolutionary Army's leadership are nearly all Devil Fruit users, and the Fruits' distribution is a strategic concern across the World Government and beyond.
