## Mechanics

Forgery is the magic system of the MaiPon people on the Cosmere world of Sel. A **Forger** carves an intricate design — a **soulstamp** — into a piece of soulstone (a special mineral that channels the Dor), then presses the stamp onto a target object or person. When activated, the stamp **rewrites the target's spiritual history**: it convinces the target's Spiritual aspect (its soul, in Cosmere terms) that its past unfolded differently than it actually did, and the target's physical form updates to match.

A stamped wall might become marble instead of brick — because the stamp describes a plausible history in which the builder chose marble. A stamped painting might become a masterwork — because the stamp describes a history in which the artist received better training. A stamped person might gain new skills, new memories, or an entirely different personality — because the stamp describes a plausible alternate life for them.

The critical constraint is **plausibility**. A soulstamp that describes an impossible or wildly implausible alternate history will fail to take, or will take only briefly before the target's soul rejects the rewrite. The more thoroughly the Forger researches the target's actual history, the more convincingly they can craft a plausible deviation, and the longer the stamp holds. A crude stamp might last minutes; a masterwork stamp, built on exhaustive research, can hold indefinitely.

Soulstamps must be periodically **re-applied** — most Forgeries require the stamp to be pressed again at regular intervals (daily, in many cases) or the rewrite fades and the target reverts. Truly exceptional Forgeries can achieve near-permanence, but these are rare achievements.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **research time**. A Forger must understand the target's true history in detail before constructing a plausible alternative. Forging a wall requires understanding when it was built, by whom, with what materials, and why. Forging a person's soul requires understanding their entire life story. This makes Forgery slow and preparation-intensive; it is fundamentally unsuited to combat or improvisation.

The second cost is **material**: soulstone is required for the stamps, and the carving itself is a painstaking craft. A single stamp can take days or weeks to design and carve.

The third cost is **maintenance**: most Forgeries are temporary and require regular re-stamping. A Forger who walks away from their work will see it revert. This limits the system's scalability — a single practitioner can only maintain so many active Forgeries.

Like all Selish magic, Forgery is **geography-dependent** and weakens far from MaiPon, though the degree of degradation is not precisely established in the text.

Forgery of living people — especially soul Forgery that rewrites personality and memory — is considered profoundly transgressive even by other Forgers. The ethical weight of overwriting someone's identity is a central theme of *The Emperor's Soul*.

## Practitioner access

Forgery is a learned craft, not an innate gift. Any person with access to soulstone, training, and sufficient knowledge of the target can practice it. In practice, Forgers are trained within the MaiPon cultural tradition, and the discipline is regarded with deep suspicion elsewhere on Sel — it is, after all, the art of making convincing fakes. Shai, the protagonist of *The Emperor's Soul*, is simultaneously a master artist and a convicted criminal.

## Cultural role

Forgery occupies a contradictory cultural position. Within MaiPon, it is a recognized art form with a sophisticated tradition, though even there it exists in tension with conventional artistry (is a Forged masterwork "real"?). Outside MaiPon, Forgery is treated as fraud, theft, and — when applied to people — a form of violation. The Rose Empire, where *The Emperor's Soul* is set, has outlawed it but nonetheless commissions Shai to Forge the Emperor's soul after an assassination attempt leaves him alive but mentally destroyed. The novella's plot is built around the paradox of a society that despises Forgery but cannot survive without it.
