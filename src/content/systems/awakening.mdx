## Mechanics

Every person on Nalthis is born with one Breath — a quantum of soul-energy that is part of what makes them alive and aware. Breaths can be voluntarily transferred between people, by speaking a transferring formula. The recipient gains the Breath; the giver loses it. The transfer is one-way and permanent unless the original owner manages to recover their Breath later (which is difficult).

Awakeners use accumulated Breaths to magically animate inanimate objects. The procedure has three components: the Awakener invests Breath into a target object, speaks a Command in clear declarative language, and provides a color source from which the working draws. The color-draw is real and visible: the awakened working leaches color from the cloth or material around it, leaving the surrounding palette gray.

Each working accepts a specific kind of Command — a short imperative addressed to the awakened object. Examples in fiction include "Hold things," "Defend me," "Grasp items," "Protect me as I sleep." The clarity of the Command and the kinesthetic similarity of the awakened object to the intended action both shape the working's effectiveness. A rope awakened with "Tie things" will work better than the same rope awakened with "Defend me."

The system is tiered by Breath-count via the **Heightenings**. Specific population-thresholds of Breaths held by a single user grant qualitative tier-jumps:

- **First Heightening** (50 Breaths): perfect color recognition.
- **Second** (200): perfect pitch.
- **Third** (500): aura-perception of others' Breath.
- **Fourth, Fifth, ... up to Tenth** (50,000): increasing immunity to mundane disease, life-extension, slowed aging, the ability to awaken with progressively less Breath investment, and at the top tier, the qualitative effects approach divinity.

Breaths can be voluntarily withdrawn from awakened objects to recover them, in principle. Practical recovery is harder: the Breath stays in the object until it is dismissed, and dismissed objects revert to inert matter.

A specific subset of inhabitants are the **Returned**: those who have died and been called back, returning as divine beings. Each Returned carries a single Divine Breath and must consume a regular Breath every week to remain alive. The Returned of Hallandren form the local pantheon and sit at the center of the kingdom's politics.

## Limitations and costs

The headline cost is resource depletion. Breaths are finite — there is one per living person — and accumulating high counts requires either purchasing Breaths from many people (a real and morally fraught economy in Hallandren) or being given them as part of religious devotion. The poor sell their Breath to feed their families; the rich amass them for the tier-jumps.

The color-draw is a visible operational cost. Working magic in a populated environment leaves a perceptible gray patch on the surrounding cloth, drapery, or vegetation, marking the practitioner.

Per-working cost depends on the desired effect and on how closely the awakened object's form matches the requested behavior. An Awakener trying to make rope defend them must invest substantially more Breath than one asking the rope to tie things.

A subtler cost is moral. Selling one's Breath is depicted as both legally legitimate and ethically uncomfortable. Hallandren's culture treats high-Breath holders as quasi-aristocratic — the visible color-perception of the First Heightening, the aural acuity of higher tiers, and the long-life effects all mark the holder as set apart. The novel makes the moral trade-offs of this stratification explicit.

A specific upper-bound cost is the Returned. Returned beings need a fresh Breath every week. The state-religion of Hallandren handles this through public donation, but the implication — that the gods of the kingdom are sustained by an ongoing weekly tribute of life-force from the population — is one of the novel's central tensions.

## Practitioner access

Open to everyone in principle: any person born on Nalthis has one Breath and can transfer it. In practice, working Awakeners require either a personal accumulation of many Breaths or institutional access. Awakening at the basic level requires only a few Breaths and is a real if esoteric craft; the Heightenings sit further up and are practically gated by wealth or religious office.

Returned status is selected by some other agency — the conditions under which a deceased person Returns are not under any practitioner's control.

## Cultural role

In Hallandren, Awakening is openly known and culturally central. The royal family, the priesthood, and the assembled population organize their politics around the Returned and around the wealth-vs-Breath economy. In neighboring Idris, Awakening is considered religiously suspect, and the common practice is to hold one's Breath rather than transfer it. The novel sits at the cultural fault line between these two attitudes — Idrian asceticism vs. Hallandren accumulation — and uses Awakening to interrogate its political and religious stakes.
