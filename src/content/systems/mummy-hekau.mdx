## Mechanics

The **Amenti** are mummies — mortals who died and were resurrected through a fusion of their soul with a **tem-akh** (a fragment of an ancient Egyptian spirit). This fusion grants immortality (the mummy can regenerate from death, returning after a period of recovery) and access to **Hekau** — ancient Egyptian magical disciplines.

The six paths of Hekau are:

**Alchemy** — the creation of magical potions, oils, and substances using ancient Egyptian formulae. Alchemical preparations can heal, poison, transform, or preserve. **Amulets** — the crafting of protective and empowering talismans, inscribed with hieroglyphic formulae that store magical effects. **Celestial** — magic drawn from the stars, the sun, and the moon; includes divination, astral projection, and channeling celestial energy. **Effigy** — the creation and animation of magical figurines and constructs, from simple ushabti (servant figures) to complex animated guardians. **Necromancy** — communication with and power over the dead, including commanding ghosts, sealing or opening pathways to the Underworld, and preventing or causing death. **Nomenclature** — the magic of true names; knowing the true name of a person, spirit, or thing grants power over it.

Hekau are fueled by **Sekhem** — the mummy's life force, replenished by spending time in sacred sites, performing rituals aligned with Ma'at, and acting in accordance with their purpose. Sekhem is also the mummy's measure of spiritual potency; higher Sekhem enables more powerful Hekau and faster resurrection.

Each Hekau path is rated 1–5 dots. Higher ratings unlock more powerful effects. The system is structured and ritualistic — most Hekau require time, components, and preparation rather than instantaneous casting.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **Balance (Ma'at)**. The Amenti serve Ma'at — the Egyptian principle of cosmic order, truth, and justice. Actions that violate Ma'at (cruelty, dishonor, allowing corruption) degrade the mummy's **Balance** score, weakening their Hekau and their ability to resurrect. At very low Balance, the mummy risks losing their tem-akh and dying permanently.

The second cost is **Sekhem depletion**. Hekau and resurrection consume Sekhem, which is slow to replenish. A mummy who uses their powers freely risks depleting their life force and becoming temporarily inert — a vulnerable state that can last days or years.

The third cost is **preparation time**. Unlike the instant powers of vampires or werewolves, most Hekau require ritual preparation — crafting amulets, mixing alchemical substances, inscribing hieroglyphs, performing ceremonial invocations. This makes Hekau powerful but slow, rewarding planning over improvisation.

## Practitioner access

Only the Amenti — fused mortal-spirit hybrids resurrected through the Spell of Life — can use Hekau. There are six types of Amenti, each formed from a different kind of tem-akh fragment, with different strengths and predispositions. The Amenti are guided by **Isis** and the **Judges of Ma'at** (powerful elder mummies) and organized into the **Web of Faith** — a loose global network. Mortal sorcerers can learn fragments of Hekau through study, but they lack the Sekhem and tem-akh that make the full tradition accessible.

## Cultural role

The Amenti see themselves as champions of Ma'at in a world tipping toward Apophis (entropy and corruption). Their Hekau are tools of cosmic maintenance — healing the world's spiritual wounds, combating the forces of the Wyrm and Oblivion, and preserving sacred knowledge. The Egyptian mythological framework gives the Amenti a sense of purpose that most WoD characters lack: they know why they were resurrected, and their magic is explicitly in service of that purpose.
