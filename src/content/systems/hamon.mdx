## Mechanics

**Hamon** (波紋, "Ripple") is a martial art and energy discipline used in Parts 1 and 2 of *JoJo's Bizarre Adventure*. Practitioners generate a form of bioelectrical energy through controlled, rhythmic breathing — specifically, a breathing technique that produces energy with the same wavelength as sunlight. This energy can be channeled through the body, transmitted through objects, and used to produce a range of effects.

The primary application is **anti-vampire combat**. Because Hamon energy is identical in wavelength to sunlight, it is lethal to vampires, Pillar Men, and other undead creatures — the same beings that are destroyed by natural sunlight. A Hamon-charged punch dissolves vampire flesh on contact. This makes Hamon the only reliable weapon against the series' supernatural threats in Parts 1-2.

Beyond vampire-killing, Hamon has diverse applications. **Physical enhancement**: Hamon strengthens the user's body, increasing striking power, speed, and durability. **Healing**: Hamon energy can accelerate cellular regeneration, healing wounds and even restarting stopped hearts. **Material conduction**: Hamon can be channeled through water, metal, oil, fabric, and other materials — a soap bubble infused with Hamon becomes a cutting weapon, a pair of clackers becomes a devastating flail. **Adhesion**: Hamon allows the user to walk on water, stick to walls, or bond objects together. **Life energy manipulation**: advanced practitioners can transfer their life energy to others or sense living presences.

The technique is teachable — Zeppeli trains Jonathan, Lisa Lisa trains Joseph and Caesar — but requires significant natural aptitude and years of physical conditioning. The breathing must be precise and maintained under combat stress; if a Hamon user's breathing is disrupted (by injury, suffocation, or distraction), their Hamon output drops or ceases entirely.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **breathing dependency**. All Hamon generation requires controlled breathing. Any injury that impairs the lungs, throat, or diaphragm directly reduces Hamon output. Opponents who understand Hamon target the practitioner's breathing — a blow to the solar plexus or a stranglehold can neutralize a Hamon user entirely.

The second cost is **training requirements**. Hamon proficiency requires years of physical conditioning, breath control practice, and combat experience. The gap between a novice (Jonathan at the start of Phantom Blood) and a master (Lisa Lisa) is enormous, and there are no shortcuts — raw talent must be refined through disciplined practice.

The third limitation is **specialization against the undead**. Hamon is devastatingly effective against vampires and Pillar Men but has limited utility against ordinary human opponents or non-undead threats. Its offensive applications against living targets are essentially those of an enhanced martial art — powerful but not categorically different from conventional combat.

## Practitioner access

Hamon is a teachable discipline, but it requires both natural aptitude and intensive training. The Zeppeli family and other Hamon masters maintain a tradition of passing the art from teacher to student. The training is physically grueling — Lisa Lisa's regimen on Air Supplena Island involved weeks of extreme conditioning. By Part 3 of JoJo, Hamon practitioners are extremely rare, as the emergence of Stands has largely superseded Hamon as the primary supernatural combat system.

## Cultural role

Hamon exists as a hidden martial tradition, known to a small number of practitioners who train specifically to combat supernatural threats. It has no public presence or institutional support — Hamon users operate in secret, responding to vampire and Pillar Men threats that the ordinary world does not know exist. Within the JoJo narrative, Hamon represents the structured, training-based power system of the early series — a system that Araki deliberately replaced with the more expressive and personality-driven Stand system starting in Part 3.
