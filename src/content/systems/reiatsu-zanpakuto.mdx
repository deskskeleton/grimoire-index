## Mechanics

Every soul in the *Bleach* cosmology generates **reiryoku** — spiritual energy — in a quantity that varies enormously between individuals. When a soul actively projects this energy outward, it becomes **reiatsu** ("spiritual pressure"), a tangible force that warps space, presses on bystanders, and is the medium through which all combat-grade techniques operate.

Soul Reapers — the principal practitioners — interface with their reiatsu through a **Zanpakutō**, a sword forged from and bonded to the wielder's own soul. Each Zanpakutō is a unique entity with its own personality and name; mastery is the practitioner's ability to communicate with that spirit and persuade it to disclose its name and abilities. Three release tiers exist:

- **Sealed state** — an ordinary-looking katana. The blade still channels reiatsu and supports basic techniques but exposes none of its unique power.
- **Shikai** ("Initial Release") — invoked by a spoken release command unique to the blade ("*Roar*, Zabimaru"; "*Scatter*, Senbonzakura"). The sword transforms into its expressed shape and the wielder gains its named ability — fire, ice, multiplication into thousands of blades, water-pressure manipulation, time stop, and so on.
- **Bankai** ("Final Release") — the second, drastically more powerful release. Bankai requires the wielder to physically subdue the sword spirit during a multi-day inner-world ordeal; it is gated to perhaps a few dozen practitioners across the entire Soul Society at any given time.

Alongside Zanpakutō, Soul Reapers practice four umbrella disciplines: **Zanjutsu** (swordsmanship), **Hakuda** (unarmed combat), **Hohō** (high-speed movement, including the teleport-step *shunpo*), and **Kidō** — a parallel spell-style system of numbered Hadō (destruction) and Bakudō (binding) incantations that operate independently of one's Zanpakutō.

Other factions in the setting use related but distinct systems: **Hollows** and Arrancar use a cero blast and resurreción transformations; **Quincy** archers convert ambient reishi (free spiritual particles) into bows and arrows; **Visored** are Soul Reapers fused with a Hollow mask. These are siblings of, not subsets of, the Reaper system.

## Limitations and costs

The headline cost is **reiatsu reserves**. Even captain-class Soul Reapers can be exhausted by sustained Bankai use; a long fight becomes a contest of reserves and efficiency. Drained reiatsu also leaks involuntary pressure that suppresses weaker souls in the area — a captain on a battlefield can incapacitate ordinary humans simply by standing nearby.

The second cost is **the relationship with the sword spirit**. A wielder cannot use Shikai without learning the spirit's name; cannot use Bankai without physically dominating it; and cannot use either reliably while estranged from it. Severed or rejected relationships render the sword inert. The sword also reflects the wielder's character — a sadistic Reaper produces a sadistic spirit — so character and capability are linked diegetically rather than abstractly.

The third is **knowledge cost**, particularly for Kidō. High-numbered destruction and binding spells require long incantations and precise focus; abbreviating them produces weaker, less reliable effects. Kidō expertise is its own academic specialty within the Gotei 13.

A fourth, structural limitation is that Soul Reaper bodies are spirit-tier and largely invisible to ordinary humans. They cannot freely interact with the living world without specialized gigai (artificial bodies) and special permits.

## Practitioner access

Soul Reapers are recruited from souls in Soul Society with sufficient innate reiryoku, then trained at the **Shin'ō Academy** before assignment to one of the **Gotei 13** military divisions. Officer rank is gated by reiatsu output, technical skill, and (for senior captain seats) the ability to achieve Bankai. Living humans with unusual spiritual gifts occasionally enter the system — Ichigo Kurosaki being the central example — and a small number of human bloodlines (Quincy, Fullbringers) carry parallel powers.

The supporting factions — Hollows, Arrancar, Visored, Quincy, Fullbringers — recruit through entirely different mechanisms (death-and-corruption, Hollowification, lineage), and the political relationships among them drive most of the series' arcs.

## Cultural role

Soul Society is structured as a feudal-bureaucratic afterlife society, and the Gotei 13 functions as its standing army and police. Reiatsu and the practice of it are openly known and ranked within Soul Society; a captain's spiritual pressure is a known quantity, and rank insignia map roughly to capability. From the perspective of the living world, however, almost all of this is hidden — most living humans cannot perceive Soul Reapers, Hollows, or their fights at all, and the masquerade is preserved by both biology and policy. The series' inciting tension is the leakage between these layers.
