## Mechanics

The **Holds** are the oldest known framework of magical power in the Malazan world — predating the Warrens, the Deck of Dragons, and mortal civilization itself. Where Warrens are relatively discrete dimensional spaces attuned to specific aspects of reality (fire, shadow, death), the Holds are broader, more primal structures. Each Hold corresponds to a fundamental archetype or force: the **Hold of the Beast** (predation, savagery, animal power), the **Hold of Ice** (cold, stasis, preservation), the **Hold of Fire** (destruction, transformation, heat), the **Hold of Death** (endings, undeath, the boundary between living and dead), and others.

The Holds are accessed and consulted through the **Tiles** — a divinatory system analogous to the Deck of Dragons but older and cruder. The Tiles are physical objects (carved bone, stone, or wood) that a reader casts and interprets. Where the Deck of Dragons maps the current political-magical landscape through its changing roster of Houses and cards, the Tiles map older, more elemental forces. The Tiles reading does not merely predict — it can invoke, drawing the Hold's attention and power into the reader's immediate reality.

The relationship between Holds and Warrens is one of ancestry. The Warrens were carved from or built upon the Holds — a refinement imposed by the ascendant K'rul when he created the Warren system by bleeding his own blood into the fabric of reality. The Holds represent what magic was before that refinement: less controlled, less categorizable, and less safe. Some Holds have "become" Warrens (the Hold of Fire maps roughly to the Warren of Telas), but the mapping is imperfect. The Holds contain aspects that no Warren fully captures.

Certain peoples use the Holds instead of Warrens. The **Tiste Edur** (the grey-skinned Elder race) access their power through the Hold of Shadow and its connections. The **Letherii** (the expansionist human empire in *Midnight Tides*) have magic-users who work through Hold-based frameworks rather than Warren-based ones.

## Limitations and costs

The primary limitation is **lack of refinement**. Hold-based magic is rawer and less controllable than Warren-based magic. A mage accessing the Hold of Fire encounters primal destruction, not the channeled energy of Telas. This makes Hold magic more powerful in absolute terms but harder to direct precisely.

The second cost is **archetypal compulsion**. The Holds are not neutral power sources — they are archetypes that exert influence on those who engage with them. A practitioner who draws deeply on the Hold of the Beast risks behavioral changes: increased aggression, predatory instincts, diminished empathy. The Hold shapes the user.

The third limitation is **historical displacement**. The Warren system has largely superseded the Holds across most of the Malazan world. Most contemporary mages do not know how to access Holds, and the Tiles tradition is rare and fragmentary. The Holds are legacy infrastructure — powerful but poorly maintained.

## Practitioner access

Hold magic is practiced by cultures that predate or resist the Warren system. The Tiste Edur, certain Letherii sorcerers, and ancient peoples who never adopted K'rul's refinement still access power through Hold-based frameworks. The Tiles readers are rare specialists within these cultures. Among mainstream Malazan civilization (which uses the Warren system), Hold magic is unknown or treated as an anthropological curiosity.

## Cultural role

The Holds represent Erikson's archaeological approach to worldbuilding — the idea that the present magical order is built on earlier, rougher foundations. In *Midnight Tides*, the collision between the Hold-using Tiste Edur and the Warren-adjacent Letherii is partly a collision between two magical paradigms. The Holds are not better or worse than the Warrens; they are older, and their age carries both power (depth, primacy) and cost (crudeness, danger). The Tiles readings function as both divination and ritual — consulting the Holds is not a passive act but an invitation for ancient forces to take notice.
