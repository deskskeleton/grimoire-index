## Mechanics

In **Changeling: The Lost**, changelings are humans who were abducted by the True Fae (the Gentry), held captive in Arcadia, and transformed by the experience — gaining inhuman features, supernatural abilities, and deep psychological scars. Those who escape return to the mortal world changed, and their magic reflects both what was done to them and what they learned to survive.

The primary magical framework is **Contracts** — formalized agreements between changelings and aspects of reality itself. A Contract is not a spell cast by force of will but a bargain: the changeling asks a fundamental force (Darkness, Stone, Dreams, the Hearth, Smoke, the Moon) for a specific effect, and the force grants it under defined conditions. Each Contract clause has a **catch** — a specific circumstance under which the power can be activated for free, without spending Glamour. Catches reflect the alien logic of Faerie: a Contract of Darkness might be free when used in a place that has never known artificial light; a Contract of Stone might be free when the changeling stands barefoot on natural ground.

Contracts are organized into themed groups: **Contracts of the Elements** (fire, water, air, earth), **Contracts of Darkness** (shadow manipulation), **Contracts of Dream** (entering and shaping mortal dreams), **Contracts of the Hearth** (domestic magic — food, shelter, warmth), **Contracts of Smoke** (stealth and misdirection), **Contracts of the Moon** (madness and perception), and many more, including Court-specific Contracts tied to emotional resonance (Spring/desire, Summer/wrath, Autumn/fear, Winter/sorrow).

The fuel is **Glamour** — emotional energy harvested from human emotions. Unlike Dreaming's creative-inspiration model, Lost's Glamour is harvested from emotional experiences — witnessing or provoking strong emotions in mortals. Each Court has a favored emotion that its members harvest most efficiently.

**Pledges** are another magical framework: formal supernatural oaths enforced by the Wyrd. A pledge binds all parties to specific obligations, with defined benefits and sanctions. Breaking a pledge invokes automatic supernatural punishment. Pledges structure changeling society — alliances, court membership, and even commercial agreements are formalized through Wyrd-enforced oaths.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **Glamour expenditure**. Each Contract clause costs Glamour unless activated through its catch. Glamour must be harvested from human emotions, which requires social interaction and can be exploitative.

The second cost is **Clarity**. Changelings maintain a Clarity score — a measure of their ability to distinguish reality from Faerie delusion. Using certain powers, encountering Faerie-related stimuli, or behaving in ways that echo their captivity degrades Clarity. Low Clarity produces hallucinations, paranoia, and inability to distinguish the mortal world from Arcadia — a mechanical representation of trauma's ongoing effects.

The third cost is **the Fetch**. When the True Fae abducted the changeling, they left behind a Fetch — a simulacrum that took the changeling's place in mortal life. Returning to find that a copy has been living your life, with your family and friends, is one of the game's central sources of emotional conflict. The Fetch is not itself a limitation on magic, but it constrains the changeling's ability to reintegrate into the mortal world.

The fourth cost is **vulnerability to the Gentry**. The True Fae can reclaim escaped changelings. The Mask (the changeling's human-seeming appearance) protects them from mortal notice, but powerful Fae can pierce it. Overt magical activity can attract the Gentry's attention.

## Practitioner access

All changelings have access to Contracts. The specific Contracts available depend on the changeling's **Seeming** (the broad category of their Fae transformation — Beast, Darkling, Elemental, Fairest, Ogre, Wizened) and their **Court** affiliation. Learning new Contract clauses requires spending experience and, narratively, negotiating with the relevant force. No mortal can use Contracts — the ability is a consequence of having been transformed by Arcadia.

## Cultural role

Changeling society is organized around the **Courts** — seasonal political factions (Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter) that rotate governance. Each Court embodies a survival strategy against the True Fae: Spring hides in desire and new beginnings, Summer fights with righteous fury, Autumn studies Faerie magic to use it defensively, Winter hides in cold sorrow and endurance. Contracts and Pledges are the structural glue of this society — they enforce agreements in a community where trust is scarce and trauma is universal. The game is fundamentally about building community after surviving abuse, and the magic system reflects this: power comes from connection (to forces, to Courts, to pledges with allies) rather than from individual mastery.
