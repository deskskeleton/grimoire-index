## Mechanics

**Mystic Eyes** (魔眼, *magan*) are a broad category of supernatural ocular abilities in the Nasuverse. They range from relatively common types (Mystic Eyes of Charm, which compel attraction; Mystic Eyes of Binding, which restrict movement) to extraordinarily rare and powerful variants that represent some of the setting's most potent abilities.

Common Mystic Eyes function as innate magical abilities focused through the user's gaze. They activate upon making eye contact with the target and produce effects ranging from suggestion and compulsion to petrification (Medusa's Cybele). These are treated as a biological magical trait — some mage families cultivate them through selective breeding, and they can occasionally be transplanted.

The most significant variant is the **Mystic Eyes of Death Perception** (直死の魔眼, *Chokushi no Magan*) — possessed by Shiki Tohno (in *Tsukihime*) and Shiki Ryougi (in *Kara no Kyoukai*). These eyes perceive the **lines and points of death** on all things — conceptual fault lines where a thing's existence is most vulnerable. By cutting along these lines (typically with a knife or blade), the user can kill or destroy anything, regardless of its physical durability, magical defenses, or apparent immortality. If something has a concept of death — if it can theoretically end — the Mystic Eyes can perceive where that ending lies.

The eyes perceive death as visual overlays: thin lines (cutting which severs a component of the target's existence) and points (piercing which causes instant, total death). The user must physically trace the lines or strike the points — the eyes provide perception, not automatic destruction. This creates a combat style centered on precision and speed rather than power: both Shikis fight with mundane blades, relying on their perception to make each cut existentially lethal.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **cognitive overload**. The Mystic Eyes of Death Perception show the user the death inherent in everything they look at — the lines of death on walls, furniture, the ground, the air, and every living thing. This produces an overwhelming, nauseating sensory experience that can cause severe headaches, mental instability, and eventual brain damage. Both Shikis mitigate this with special glasses that suppress the eyes' perception when worn.

The second cost is **physical fragility**. The Mystic Eyes do not make their users physically stronger, faster, or more durable. Both Shikis are essentially normal humans (or near-human) with a perception-based killing ability. If they cannot reach their target, avoid attacks, or maintain the precision needed to cut death lines, their ability is useless.

The third cost is **limitation against the truly deathless**. While the Mystic Eyes can perceive death in almost anything, beings or concepts that have no death — that are genuinely eternal and unchanging — cannot be killed through their lines. This is an edge case (very few things in the Nasuverse are truly deathless), but it establishes a hard ceiling.

## Practitioner access

Common Mystic Eyes can be inherited, bred for, or occasionally acquired through magical means (transplantation, Dead Apostle transformation). The Mystic Eyes of Death Perception are fundamentally different: they arise from a near-death experience or a connection to the Root (Akasha) that rewrites the user's perception. They are not hereditary, not reproducible, and not transferable. In the entire setting, only two characters possess them, and each acquired them through unique, traumatic circumstances.

## Cultural role

Within the Mage's Association, Mystic Eyes are catalogued and ranked by rarity (with the most powerful designated as "Rainbow" class). High-quality Mystic Eyes are valuable enough to be harvested, preserved, and sold — a practice that underscores the Association's instrumentalist attitude toward magical abilities. The Mystic Eyes of Death Perception exist outside this classification system entirely — they are too rare and too conceptually unusual for the Association's frameworks, which is appropriate for abilities that perceive a level of reality most Magi cannot even theorize about.
