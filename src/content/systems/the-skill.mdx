## Mechanics

The **Skill** is a telepathic ability that allows mind-to-mind communication, emotional sharing, and eventually physical manipulation through mental contact. At its simplest, a Skill-user can send and receive thoughts across large distances — reaching other Skill-users or (with difficulty) touching the minds of ordinary people. At intermediate levels, the Skill enables **Skill-healing** (repairing injuries by mentally guiding the body's own repair processes) and **Skill-commands** (implanting compulsions in other minds). At its most advanced, the Skill can reshape physical matter — King Verity carves a stone dragon and animates it through Skill, and the Elderlings used the Skill to transform their cities into living structures.

The Skill operates through a metaphysical medium sometimes called the **Skill-river** — a current of mental energy that Skill-users perceive as a flowing stream. Submerging oneself in the Skill-river amplifies power but is intensely dangerous: the experience is ecstatic, and practitioners who go too deep may lose their individual identity, dissolving into the collective flow.

**Coteries** are formal groups of six Skill-users who train together and form a permanent mental bond. A coterie functions as a single unit — its members can pool their Skill-power, communicate instantly, and even sacrifice their individual identities to the group if necessary. The coterie structure is the traditional framework for Skill training, supervised by a **Skillmaster** who serves the Farseer crown.

**Skill-pillars** are standing stones left by the Elderlings that function as telepathic waypoints and teleportation nodes. A Skill-user who knows how to use them can step into one pillar and emerge from another, anywhere in the world. The pillar network represents the Elderling civilization's mastery of the Skill at levels far beyond current practitioners.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **addiction**. The Skill is intensely pleasurable to use — Skill-contact produces a euphoric merging that practitioners crave. Heavy Skill-use produces dependency, and the temptation to remain in the Skill-river (abandoning one's body and identity) is a constant danger. Several characters lose themselves permanently to Skill-addiction.

The second cost is **physical debilitation**. Intense Skill-use exhausts the body. Practitioners who overextend themselves experience nosebleeds, seizures, unconsciousness, and potentially death. Skill-healing, while powerful, costs the healer enormous physical energy.

The third cost is **vulnerability to other Skill-users**. A mind opened to the Skill is a mind exposed. Hostile Skill-users can attack, manipulate, or compel through the same channel used for communication. Fitz's vulnerability to Regal's Skill-corrupted coterie is a recurring plot element.

## Practitioner access

The Skill is hereditary, running primarily through the Farseer royal line. Not all Farseers have it, and the strength varies considerably. Bastards (like Fitz) can inherit the ability. Training occurs through the coterie system, historically supervised by a Skillmaster. The knowledge of how to train Skill-users was nearly lost during the Red Ship War era, and much of the later series involves recovering Elderling-era Skill knowledge.

## Cultural role

The Skill is the Farseer dynasty's political instrument — it enables secure long-distance communication, intelligence gathering, and (when necessary) mental compulsion of subjects. The monarchy's legitimacy is partly built on Skill-power. The loss of Skill training during the chaos of the Red Ship War weakens the dynasty, and its recovery is a multi-series arc. Culturally, the Skill is known to exist but its capabilities are not widely understood — commoners know the Farseers have "the Skill" but not what it specifically does.
