## Mechanics

A shinobi's basic resource is **chakra**: a blend of physical energy (drawn from the body's cells) and spiritual energy (drawn from training and experience). The two components must be mixed in the right proportions for a given technique, and that mixing — together with shaping the resulting energy into a specific effect — is performed through sequences of **hand-seals**, twelve standardized two-handed mudras corresponding to animals of the zodiac. Many techniques also have a spoken name, which is the conventional invocation cue rather than a mechanical requirement.

Techniques fall into three principal categories:

- **Ninjutsu** uses molded chakra to produce direct effects: throwing fire, walking on water, replacing oneself with a log, summoning a contracted beast, sealing a target's chakra inside an artifact. The largest sub-class is **elemental ninjutsu**, organized around five "natures" — Fire, Water, Earth, Wind, Lightning — that exist in a rock-paper-scissors relationship (Water beats Fire, Fire beats Wind, Wind beats Lightning, Lightning beats Earth, Earth beats Water). A practitioner has one or two natural affinities and can learn others with training. Yin and Yang are paired non-elemental natures used for illusion and life-creation respectively.
- **Genjutsu** projects chakra into a target's nervous system to disrupt their senses, producing illusions, false memories, or paralysis. Genjutsu is comparatively cheap in raw chakra but demands precise control and is hard to maintain against a focused opponent who recognizes the intrusion.
- **Taijutsu** is unarmed and weapon combat, often enhanced by chakra channeled into the limbs for striking power, speed, or impact-resistance. The Eight Gates and similar advanced forms unlock progressively more lethal levels of self-enhancement at correspondingly severe physical cost.

A fourth category, **senjutsu** ("sage technique"), draws on **natural energy** from the surrounding environment and blends it into chakra to produce a stronger, more efficient hybrid. It must be learned at one of a small number of remote sacred sites, and the practitioner must hold themselves perfectly still long enough to absorb the energy correctly — failure turns the user partially or fully to stone.

## Limitations and costs

The principal cost is **chakra exhaustion**. Every shinobi has a finite reserve that scales with bloodline, training, and age, and major ninjutsu deplete it quickly; a fight that runs long enough becomes a contest of remaining reserves. Severe overuse causes loss of consciousness or death.

The second cost is **knowledge and preparation**. Each named jutsu requires its specific hand-seal sequence, an understanding of the underlying nature transformation, and (for the largest techniques) a long pre-fight ritual involving scrolls, blood seals, and binding contracts. Top-tier sealing arts (fūinjutsu) are an academic specialty in their own right.

A third, structural cost is **inheritance**. Many of the strongest techniques are **kekkei genkai** ("bloodline limit") or **dōjutsu** ("eye technique") — abilities tied to specific clans (Hyūga's Byakugan, Uchiha's Sharingan and Mangekyō Sharingan, Senju's Wood Release, Kaguya's bone manipulation, etc.). These are inaccessible to outsiders by training alone, and several of them inflict their own costs (Mangekyō Sharingan use causes progressive blindness; Eight Gates use damages the body and the eighth gate is fatal).

Genjutsu and senjutsu add their own constraints: the genjutsu user must maintain attention on the target, and senjutsu requires either total stillness or a partner-clone reservoir to keep gathering natural energy while the practitioner fights.

## Practitioner access

Chakra is universal — every living person has it — but the disciplined molding and shaping that produces jutsu is a profession. Most practitioners are graduates of the **Hidden Village** academies in the five Great Shinobi Nations (Konoha, Kumo, Iwa, Kiri, Suna) and a handful of smaller villages. Within that population, there are sharp tiers: genin, chūnin, jōnin, and the village leader (Kage). Civilians, monks, and a few independent traditions outside the village system also use chakra, but the village military model dominates.

Bloodline access cuts orthogonally to rank. A genin from a kekkei-genkai clan may have access to techniques unreachable by an experienced jōnin from outside that clan, and the Tailed Beasts — nine massive entities of pure chakra sealed into human "jinchūriki" — confer reserves so large they constitute strategic-tier assets in their own right.

## Cultural role

Chakra and the shinobi profession are openly known. The Hidden Villages function as for-hire militaries within their respective nations; missions are graded D through S by danger, and shinobi are dispatched as soldiers, bodyguards, assassins, and couriers. The villages collectively form the world's military balance of power, and the major arcs of the series are essentially conflicts among them. Civilian governments coexist with the village system but defer to it on matters of force.

Beyond the military frame, chakra is woven into ordinary life: water-walking, wall-walking, simple healing, and message-summoning are basic-academy techniques, and most practitioners use them constantly. The system is the world's transit infrastructure as much as it is its weaponry.
