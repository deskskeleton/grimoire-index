## Mechanics

**Prometheans** (the Created) are artificial beings — constructed from corpses, animated by the **Divine Fire** (Azoth), and modeled after mythological archetypes (Frankenstein's monster, the Golem, the Muse, the Galateid). They are not alive in the conventional sense; they are sustained by Azoth and driven by an existential imperative called the **Pilgrimage** — the alchemical journey toward becoming truly human.

Their supernatural abilities are **Transmutations** — powers organized into **Refinements** (philosophical approaches to the Pilgrimage, modeled after alchemical stages). Each Refinement emphasizes different aspects of self-transformation:

- **Aurum** (Gold / Mortality) — studying and imitating human behavior; Transmutations focus on social interaction and emotional understanding
- **Cuprum** (Copper / Self) — introspection and self-knowledge; Transmutations focus on perception, Azoth manipulation, and understanding one's own nature
- **Ferrum** (Iron / Corpus) — physical mastery; Transmutations focus on bodily enhancement, resilience, and combat
- **Mercurius** (Quicksilver / Pyros) — mastering the Divine Fire itself; Transmutations focus on Pyros manipulation, energy channeling, and alchemical transformation
- **Stannum** (Tin / Torment) — channeling rage and pain; Transmutations focus on emotional manipulation, intimidation, and destructive force

Additional Refinements include **Aes** (Bronze / Aid), **Argentum** (Silver / Mystery), **Cobalus** (Cobalt / Impurity), and **Plumbum** (Lead / Source).

The fuel is **Pyros** — the raw energy of the Divine Fire, generated by the Promethean's Azoth. Pyros powers Transmutations and can be spent to enhance physical actions, activate special abilities, or fuel alchemical processes. Pyros is replenished through exposure to electrical storms, ley lines, and sites of intense Azothic resonance.

The **Azoth** score represents the intensity of the Divine Fire within the Promethean. Higher Azoth enables more powerful Transmutations and advances the Pilgrimage, but also intensifies the two great curses of Promethean existence: **Disquiet** and **Wasteland**.

## Limitations and costs

The primary cost is **Disquiet** — a supernatural aura that causes living beings (human and animal) to develop an instinctive revulsion toward the Promethean. Brief exposure produces unease; prolonged exposure produces hatred, paranoia, and eventually mob violence. Disquiet is not optional — it is a constant, escalating effect that makes sustained human contact impossible. The Promethean cannot turn it off.

The second cost is **Wasteland** — environmental degradation caused by the Promethean's presence. Plants wither, animals sicken, water sours, and technology malfunctions in areas where a Promethean stays too long. Wasteland forces the Created to be nomadic — staying in one place too long destroys it.

The third cost is **Torment** — when a Promethean's Humanity (morality) degrades, they risk entering **Torment**, a state of destructive rage in which their Transmutations become uncontrolled and harmful. The Promethean's Lineage (Frankenstein, Galateid, Osiran, Tammuz, Ulgan) determines the specific manifestation of Torment.

The ultimate "cost" is the game's victory condition: completing the Pilgrimage and becoming human means **losing all Transmutations permanently**. The Promethean gives up supernatural power in exchange for mortality, human connection, and freedom from Disquiet and Wasteland. Success means becoming ordinary.

## Practitioner access

Prometheans are created, not born — each is assembled from corpses and animated through a specific alchemical process by an existing Promethean (the demiurge). The process is dangerous and frequently produces **Pandorans** (failed, monstrous creations) instead. Each Promethean belongs to a **Lineage** that determines their nature, appearance, and Torment. The number of Prometheans in existence at any given time is very small — perhaps a few hundred worldwide.

## Cultural role

Prometheans have no culture in the conventional sense. They are solitary by necessity (Disquiet prevents community) and nomadic by circumstance (Wasteland prevents settlement). They form temporary **throngs** (traveling groups of Created) for mutual support, but these are inherently unstable — multiple Prometheans in one area intensify each other's Disquiet and Wasteland effects. The Pilgrimage is a solitary journey, and the social isolation is the game's central metaphor: what does it mean to strive for humanity when humanity instinctively rejects you?
