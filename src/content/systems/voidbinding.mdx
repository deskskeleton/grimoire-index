## Mechanics

Voidbinding operates on the same ten-Surge framework as Surgebinding — Adhesion, Gravitation, Division, Abrasion, Progression, Illumination, Transformation, Transportation, Cohesion, and Tension — but draws its power from **Odium** rather than Honor and Cultivation. Its primary practitioners are the **Fused**: ancient singer souls, loyal to Odium, who are periodically reborn by displacing a living singer's consciousness and inhabiting their body.

Each Fused has access to a single Surge (not two, as Surgebinders get). A Fused who wields Gravitation can fly and manipulate gravitational lashing; one who wields Illumination can create illusions; one who wields Transformation can Soulcast. The effects are functionally similar to Surgebinding's but are fueled by **Voidlight** — Odium's Investiture — rather than Stormlight, and the operational feel is distinct: Voidlight is described as thrumming and discordant where Stormlight is expansive and pressurized.

The Fused also command **Regals** — living singers who have bonded lesser spren associated with Odium (voidspren), gaining more modest combat enhancements. Regals are the rank-and-file of Odium's forces, analogous to squires in the Radiant system.

## Limitations and costs

The defining cost of Voidbinding is **cognitive and spiritual degradation**. The Fused have been reincarnating for thousands of years. Each cycle of death and rebirth degrades their sanity: many Fused in the current Desolation are mentally broken, trapped in obsessive loops, or barely functional. The most ancient and powerful are often the most damaged. This is a structural cost — the system trades long-term psychological integrity for indefinite persistence.

A second cost is **single-Surge limitation**. Unlike Surgebinders, who bond a spren and gain access to two Surges, each Fused is locked to one. This makes individual Fused tactically narrower than their Radiant counterparts and forces Odium's forces to coordinate specialists.

A third cost is **host displacement**. Each Fused incarnation requires a living singer body. The original singer's mind is destroyed or suppressed. The moral weight of this — repeatedly consuming members of their own species — is a source of internal conflict even among the Fused.

Voidlight must be accessed through the Everstorm (Odium's storm, a counterpart to the Highstorm that carries Stormlight), limiting its availability to the storm cycle.

## Practitioner access

Voidbinding is not learned or chosen in the conventional sense. The Fused are a fixed population of ancient singer souls bound to Odium; new Fused are not created under normal circumstances. They are reincarnated by Odium during Desolations, and their access to a specific Surge is an innate property of each Fused soul, determined at the time of their original investiture millennia ago.

Regals are created when living singers bond a voidspren during the Everstorm. This is closer to a recruitment model: any singer can potentially become a Regal, but the bond is with one of Odium's spren and carries implicit allegiance.

## Cultural role

The Fused are the senior military and political caste of Odium's forces on Roshar. Their reincarnation cycle gives them an institutional continuity that dwarfs mortal memory — they remember the original conflicts with the human settlers and regard the current war as a continuation of a grievance thousands of years old. Within singer society, the Fused command reverence and obedience, though their mental degradation increasingly strains that authority. The Regals occupy a middle tier: elevated above ordinary singers, subordinate to the Fused, and often caught between the Fused's ancient agenda and the living singers' present-day concerns.
